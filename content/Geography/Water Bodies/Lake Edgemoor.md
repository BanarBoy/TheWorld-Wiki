# Lake Edgemoor

**Type:** Inland Lake  
**Location:** Western-Central [[Valecaryn]], near [[Greenhollow]]  
**Connected To:** Fed by minor tributaries; drains into local river systems feeding the [[Virelois River]]  
**Bordering Regions:** Greenhollow woodlands, agricultural lowlands  
**Major Settlements:** [[Greenhollow]]  
**Primary Use:** Fishing, Local Transport, Irrigation, Regional Trade  
**Navigability:** Limited (small craft only)  
**Notable For:** “A quiet inland lake sustaining Greenhollow and its surrounding lands”

---

# Overview

Lake Edgemoor is a broad, calm inland lake nestled beside the settlement of [[Greenhollow]], serving as both a natural resource and a stabilizing presence for the surrounding region.

Unlike the major waterways of Valecaryn, Edgemoor does not carry the weight of continental trade or strategic conflict. Its importance is quieter, but no less essential. The lake supports agriculture, provides reliable freshwater, and sustains a steady fishing economy that feeds nearby communities.

To those who live along its shores, Lake Edgemoor is not a route or a boundary.

> It is a constant.

---

# Geography and Structure

Lake Edgemoor is moderately sized, with a rounded, irregular shoreline shaped by gradual erosion and seasonal water variation.

• gently sloping banks allowing easy access to the water  
• mixed shoreline of grass, light woodland, and small inlets  
• several shallow coves suitable for small docks and boats  
• deeper central basin with stable water levels

The surrounding terrain transitions smoothly into farmland and managed woodland, giving the lake a natural integration into the landscape.

---

# Hydrology and Conditions

The lake is stable and predictable.

• fed by small streams and seasonal runoff  
• minimal current, with mostly still waters  
• moderate seasonal fluctuation in water level  
• surface remains calm except during storms

Water quality is high, making it suitable for both consumption and irrigation.

---

# Economic Role

Lake Edgemoor supports the regional economy in practical ways:

• fishing industry providing food for local populations  
• irrigation for surrounding farmland  
• transport of goods between lakeside points using small craft  
• supply of freshwater for settlements and agricultural use

It does not generate large-scale wealth—but it **sustains it**.

---

# Settlements and Civilization

Human activity around the lake is steady and integrated.

• [[Greenhollow]] serves as the primary lakeside settlement  
• small docks and fishing piers line portions of the shore  
• scattered farmsteads extend outward from the water  
• minor transport routes connect the lake to inland roads

The lake is not urbanized—it is **lived with**.

---

# Cultural Significance

Lake Edgemoor represents:

• stability and continuity  
• local identity tied to land and water  
• a slower, more grounded way of life

It is often associated with:

• seasonal rhythms (harvest, fishing cycles)  
• community gatherings  
• generational settlement

To outsiders, it may seem unremarkable.

To locals, it is everything.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

The lake is generally safe, but not without risk:

• occasional storms that disrupt small vessels  
• seasonal flooding along low-lying edges  
• limited capacity for large-scale transport  
• dependence on stable environmental conditions

Its scale limits both its danger—and its potential.

---

# Strategic Importance

Lake Edgemoor has minimal strategic importance at the continental level.

However, regionally:

• it supports food production and water stability  
• provides a reliable inland resource  
• anchors the settlement of Greenhollow

It is not fought over.

It is depended upon.

---

# Notable Locations

• [[Greenhollow]] — primary settlement on the lake’s edge  
• lakeside fishing piers and small docks  
• sheltered coves used for local transport and storage

---

• disputes over fishing rights between local families

• rumors of something unusual in the deeper basin  
• quiet tensions over expansion of farmland along the shore
