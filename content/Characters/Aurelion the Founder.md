# Aurelion the Founder

**Title(s):** King of Valecaryn, Founder of Aureth Caldras  
**Role:** Monarch / State Builder  
**Nationality:** [[Valecaryn]]  
**Culture / People:** [[Valdar]] 
**Born:** 862  
**Died:** 932  
**Age:** 70  
**Gender:** Male  
**Species / Race:** Human  
**Residence:** [[Aureth Caldras]]  
**Affiliation(s):** The Crown of Valecaryn  
**Known For:** Founding Aureth Caldras and ushering in the Golden Age of Valecaryn

---

# Overview

Aurelion the Founder is widely regarded as the greatest monarch of the Old Crown and one of the most transformative rulers in the history of Valecaryn.

Ascending the throne during a period of stability created by earlier legal and infrastructural reforms, Aurelion turned his attention toward shaping the kingdom into a lasting political and cultural power. His reign saw the construction of monumental architecture, the expansion of trade networks, and the founding of institutions that would influence the realm for centuries.

His most famous achievement was the establishment of the royal capital [[Aureth Caldras]] in the year 900. Built along the Virelois River near the fertile lands of the Gold Marches, the city was designed to serve as the administrative and cultural heart of the kingdom.

Under Aurelion’s rule, Valecaryn experienced unprecedented prosperity and stability. Later historians would refer to his reign as the beginning of the **Golden Age of Valecaryn**.

---

# Early Life

Aurelion was born in 862 into the royal lineage that had governed Valecaryn since the age of the early crown kings. By the time of his birth, the kingdom had already been transformed by the reforms of Caldran I and Caldran II, whose legal codes and infrastructure projects had stabilized the realm.

Educated by royal tutors and temple scholars, Aurelion developed a strong interest in governance, architecture, and the study of history. Contemporary accounts describe him as unusually thoughtful for a prince, preferring long discussions with advisors and scholars over the martial pursuits favored by many earlier kings.

Despite this reputation for scholarship, Aurelion was also trained in warfare and leadership, preparing him for the responsibilities of ruling a growing kingdom.

---

# Rise to Power

Aurelion ascended to the throne in the late ninth century during a period of relative peace. The realm he inherited possessed a strong legal system and a network of roads connecting its agricultural regions, but it lacked a centralized political and cultural center.

Recognizing this weakness, Aurelion envisioned the creation of a monumental capital that could serve as the heart of the kingdom. In the year **900**, he ordered the founding of a new royal city along the Virelois River.

Construction of [[Aureth Caldras]] quickly became the defining project of his reign. The city was designed not only as a royal residence but also as a center of administration, scholarship, and commerce.

Within a generation, the new capital had become the most influential city in Valecaryn.

---

# Personality

Historical records portray Aurelion as intelligent, patient, and highly ambitious in matters of statecraft.

Unlike many earlier monarchs who focused primarily on conquest, Aurelion believed the true strength of a kingdom lay in its institutions and prosperity. He favored careful planning, consultation with advisors, and long-term projects designed to strengthen the realm.

Though generally calm and diplomatic, he was known to act decisively when necessary, particularly when defending the authority of the crown.

Many later scholars described him as a ruler who preferred to build rather than conquer.

---

# Skills and Abilities

Aurelion’s greatest strengths lay in governance and statecraft.

He possessed exceptional skill in administration and political organization, overseeing the development of new ministries and bureaucratic systems centered in Aureth Caldras.

He was also a patron of architecture and engineering, commissioning major construction projects throughout the kingdom.

In diplomacy, he proved adept at maintaining peace with neighboring realms, allowing Valecaryn to focus its resources on economic and cultural development.

---

# Relationships

Aurelion maintained strong relationships with the major noble houses of Valecaryn, often strengthening alliances through marriage and political patronage.

He worked closely with temple authorities and scholars, supporting the establishment of academies and religious institutions in the new capital.

Many of the advisors who served in his court later became influential figures in Valecaryni administration.

---

# Role in Major Events

Aurelion’s reign marked the transition of Valecaryn from a powerful regional kingdom into a fully developed continental power.

The founding of Aureth Caldras centralized governance and allowed royal administration to expand across the realm.

Trade flourished as improved infrastructure connected agricultural regions, mining settlements, and coastal ports.

These developments collectively ushered in the period historians later called the **Golden Age of Valecaryn**.

---

# Political Influence

As monarch, Aurelion strengthened the authority of the crown while preserving the administrative reforms established by earlier rulers.

By concentrating government institutions within the new capital, he ensured that future monarchs would possess the administrative tools necessary to govern a large and prosperous kingdom.

---

# Reputation

Among Valecaryni historians, Aurelion is celebrated as a visionary ruler whose achievements defined the height of the Old Crown.

His reign is often contrasted with the chaos that followed centuries later during the Crown Crisis.

Outside the kingdom, he is remembered as a powerful and sophisticated monarch who oversaw the rise of one of the most stable realms of the western continent.

---

# Appearance

Portraits and statues depict Aurelion as a tall and imposing man with dark hair and sharp features.

He is commonly shown wearing ceremonial robes of the royal court rather than battlefield armor, emphasizing his identity as a builder and statesman rather than a conqueror.

---

# Legacy

Aurelion’s greatest legacy remains the city of [[Aureth Caldras]], which continues to serve as the political heart of Valecaryn more than six centuries after its founding.

His reign represents the peak of the Old Crown’s power and prosperity.

Even after the fall of the royal line during the Crown Crisis, many later rulers—including [[King Halvair Drosen]]—invoked Aurelion’s legacy when claiming authority over the realm.

---

# Rumors and Secrets

Some scholars speculate that the location chosen for Aureth Caldras may have held earlier religious or strategic significance long before Aurelion’s reign.

Others believe certain architectural features of the earliest royal palace contain hidden chambers or symbols tied to ancient traditions of the kingdom.
