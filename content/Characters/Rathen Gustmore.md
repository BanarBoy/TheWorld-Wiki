---

# Overview

Rathen Gustmore is one of the Five who rule [[Corsair’s Rest]], and the one who ensures that nothing enters or leaves the harbor without consequence.

Where [[Cirel Redmark]] dominates the open sea, Gustmore controls the point where all movement ends: the harbor itself. Ships that survive the waters still pass through him. Cargo that reaches the docks does so under his oversight. Nothing large moves without his awareness.

He does not chase ships.

He waits for them.

And when they arrive, they do not leave unless he allows it.

Among the Five, Gustmore represents **final control**—not of systems, not of information, but of **physical reality once it can no longer escape**.

---

# Early Life

Rathen Gustmore was born into the brutal labor systems of dockyards and chain crews—those responsible for anchoring ships, hauling cargo, and maintaining the heavy infrastructure that ports depend on.

He learned early:

• how ships are held in place  
• how cargo is secured—or taken  
• how quickly control shifts when weight is applied correctly

Unlike sailors, he did not grow up chasing the horizon.

He grew up understanding:

> everything that moves eventually stops somewhere

---

# Rise to Prominence

Gustmore rose by taking control of that “somewhere.”

He began by:

• organizing dock labor into disciplined, loyal crews  
• controlling chain yards, anchor points, and mooring systems  
• ensuring that once a ship entered harbor, it was effectively under his influence

From there, he expanded into:

• seizure operations against non-compliant vessels  
• containment of ships attempting to flee  
• control of bulk cargo once it reached port

His power became absolute in one domain:

> once something reached Corsair’s Rest, it belonged to him to decide its fate

---

# Personality

Gustmore is quiet, deliberate, and immovable.

• speaks rarely, but with finality  
• does not argue—he states outcomes  
• does not rush—he waits

He is not aggressive in the way others are.

He is worse:

> he is patient

Others perceive him as:

• inevitable  
• unyielding  
• impossible to pressure

His defining trait:

> once he commits, nothing changes the outcome

He excels in:

> stopping things that were never meant to be stopped

# Relationships

**[[Cirel Redmark]]** – Counterbalance. Redmark controls the sea; Gustmore controls what happens when ships reach harbor. Neither interferes with the other’s domain, but both understand their power is incomplete without the other.

**[[Albrecht Lanmast]]** – Structural ally. Lanmast keeps ships functional; Gustmore determines whether they move.

**[[Niamh Auldrahm]]** – Functional alignment. She determines outcomes; Gustmore enforces them at the point of no return.

**[[Seressa Fairwind]]** – Tension. Seressa relies on speed and exit routes. Gustmore represents the end of both.

# Role in Major Events

**The Locking of the Harbor**

During a period of internal instability, Gustmore ordered the harbor sealed.

Chains were drawn across key passages. Ships attempting to leave were held in place or destroyed. Cargo flow halted—but control remained intact.

This act prevented the fragmentation of Corsair’s Rest during a critical moment.

It also established a truth that has never been challenged:

> when Gustmore closes the harbor, nothing moves
