---

# Overview

Albrecht Lanmast is one of the five dominant powers of [[Corsair’s Rest]] and the single greatest authority on ships within the Reach. While others command fleets, Lanmast controls whether those fleets sail at all.

He oversees the shipyards, drydocks, and repair infrastructure that sustain piracy across the region. From hull reinforcement to cannon refitting, no vessel of consequence operates long without passing through systems he influences or directly controls.

Lanmast’s power is not derived from conquest or charisma, but from necessity. Ships break. Crews need resupply. Hulls require maintenance. And in a region defined by constant maritime activity, those realities place him at the center of everything.

Among the Five, he is regarded as a stabilizing force—difficult to provoke, but nearly impossible to circumvent. Among those most dependent on his systems is [[Cirel Redmark]], whose fleets require constant maintenance and resupply to sustain large-scale operations. This reliance creates a mutual necessity—Redmark projects power, but Lanmast ensures that power endures.

---

# Early Life

Albrecht Lanmast was born into a working dock family tied to ship construction and maintenance. His early years were spent in shipyards rather than aboard vessels, learning the structure and function of ships from keel to mast.

He demonstrated an early aptitude for understanding not just how ships were built, but how they failed.

• stress points under prolonged use  
• weaknesses in materials and joinery  
• the effects of poor maintenance on long voyages

Rather than taking to piracy directly, Lanmast embedded himself in the systems that supported it. He learned that while captains gained glory, it was the dockyards that determined how long that glory lasted.

His worldview formed around a simple truth:

> every ship returns to the dock—or it doesn’t return at all

---

# Rise to Prominence

Lanmast’s rise was gradual and structural.

He began by:

• taking control of key repair operations within Corsair’s Rest  
• standardizing processes that increased turnaround speed  
• ensuring reliability where others offered inconsistency

Over time, he expanded his influence by:

• acquiring or absorbing smaller shipyards  
• securing supply chains for timber, pitch, and fittings  
• building a network of craftsmen loyal to his operations

As his control grew, so did his leverage.

Eventually, it became clear that:

> any fleet operating at scale depended on Lanmast’s infrastructure

His position among the Five was not seized—it was recognized as already established.

**[[Seressa Fairwind]]** – Represents volatility. Lanmast tolerates her operations but ensures she remains dependent on his resources.
