# Frostcure Tradition

**Associated People:** Humans and Dwarves (primarily highland, frontier, and coastal communities)  
**Primary Lineage(s):** Eryndori highlanders, frontier settlers, dwarven hold cultures  
**Primary Regions:** [[Crown of Winter]], [[Lake Morra]] basin, [[Threewaters]], [[Stormhaven]], [[Frostgate]], [[Ulthridge]], [[Severin]]  
**Population Distribution:** Regional (widespread across Eryndor, strongest in cold and resource-variable regions)  
**Origin Era:** Early highland settlement period (parallel to early Stonebrew development)  
**Language(s):** Regional Eryndori dialects, trade cant, shared preservation terminology  
**Defining Trait:** A culture that believes **survival is secured not by abundance, but by preparation**

---

# Overview

The Frostcure Tradition is a cultural system centered on preservation, foresight, and the disciplined management of resources in a harsh and often unpredictable environment. It is practiced across Eryndor wherever cold climates, long winters, and unreliable supply conditions make survival dependent on planning rather than availability.

At its core, Frostcure is a method—using Glacier Salt, cold air, and time to transform perishable food into something stable. But beyond technique, it is a philosophy. Those who live within the tradition do not trust abundance. They trust what has been secured, stored, and proven to last.

From Rimecut Strips carried on long journeys to Stonehams aging in cellar vaults and Icefang Pike preserved along coastal routes, Frostcure shapes how people eat, travel, trade, and prepare for hardship.

To belong to this culture is to live with an awareness that scarcity is not a possibility—it is a certainty that must be anticipated.

> Winter is not an event.  
> It is a condition.

---

# Origins and History

The Frostcure Tradition emerged from necessity in the early highland and northern settlements of Eryndor, where long winters, poor harvest cycles, and geographic isolation made reliance on fresh food impossible.

Early communities developed basic salt-curing techniques using mineral deposits and glacial runoff. Over time, these methods evolved through:

• improved understanding of Glacier Salt properties  
• refinement of cold-air drying techniques  
• integration with storage infrastructure such as stone vaults

Dwarven influence contributed to:

• controlled storage environments  
• structural preservation systems  
• consistency in long-term aging

As trade expanded and populations spread into regions such as [[Threewaters]], [[Stormhaven]], and [[Severin]], Frostcure methods adapted to new resources, particularly fish.

The tradition spread not through cultural dominance, but through:

> necessity, repetition, and success

# Cultural Identity

The Frostcure Tradition is built on a central belief:

> **Preparation is the only defense against inevitability**

This manifests as:

• distrust of excess reliance on fresh resources  
• respect for time, patience, and delayed reward  
• emphasis on storing, planning, and redundancy  
• acceptance of hardship as a constant

Practitioners believe:

• what is preserved is more valuable than what is abundant  
• survival depends on discipline, not luck  
• waste is a form of failure

This creates a culture that is:

- pragmatic
- forward-thinking
- quietly severe

# Social Structure

Frostcure is not governed by a centralized authority, but is embedded in everyday social organization.

It operates through:

• family-level preservation practices  
• cooperative storage systems  
• local knowledge networks

Communities often maintain:

• shared curing spaces  
• communal storage reserves  
• seasonal labor systems for preservation

Status is influenced by:

• preparedness  
• reliability of stored goods  
• ability to sustain others through scarcity

---

# Traditions and Practices

Frostcure practices are deeply tied to seasonal cycles.

---

### Seasonal Practices

• late-season mass curing before winter  
• coordinated livestock processing  
• preservation of fish during peak runs

---

### Everyday Practices

• regular inspection of stored goods  
• rationing and controlled consumption  
• pairing preserved foods with Stonebrew

---

### Ceremonial Practices

• opening long-aged Stonehams  
• sharing preserved food during winter gatherings  
• marking survival through difficult seasons

Food is not treated casually—it is:

> accounted for, measured, and respected

---

# Relationship to Magic

The Frostcure Tradition is generally skeptical of magic.

Common beliefs include:

• magic introduces unpredictability into preservation  
• natural processes are more reliable over time  
• true mastery requires control without magical assistance

In rare cases:

• minor magic may be used for storage stabilization

However:

> reliance on magic is often seen as weakness or risk

---

# Economic and Material Life

Frostcure is a foundational component of Eryndor’s economy.

It supports:

• production of Rimecut Strips (travel and military supply)  
• Stonehams (storage and trade)  
• Icefang Pike Cure (maritime and inland food systems)

It is directly tied to:

• Glacier Salt extraction and distribution  
• livestock and fishing industries  
• caravan and naval provisioning networks

Frostcure transforms raw resources into:

> stable, tradable, and reliable goods

---

# Relationship with Other Cultures

The Frostcure Tradition is:

• respected in Valecaryn for its practicality  
• valued in Seluqar for its trade reliability  
• often misunderstood in more temperate regions

Other cultures may view it as:

• overly austere  
• unnecessarily cautious

Those who depend on it understand:

> it is the difference between survival and collapse

# Internal Divisions

Frostcure varies by region.

---

### Highland Tradition

• meat-focused  
• longer aging cycles  
• emphasis on Stonehams

---

### Coastal Tradition

• fish-based preservation  
• faster curing cycles  
• Icefang Pike dominance

---

### Frontier Tradition

• adaptive methods  
• reliance on available resources  
• greater variability in quality

---

Disagreements often arise over:

• proper curing techniques
