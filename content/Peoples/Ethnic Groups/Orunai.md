# Orunai

**Species:** Human  
**Parent Culture(s):** Indigenous Kiyoran steppe cultures  
**Primary Regions (Historic):** Open steppe regions of northern and central Kiyora, migratory corridors beyond fixed borders  
**Current Distribution:** Mobile / Semi-dispersed / Present across frontier regions and outer imperial boundaries  
**Origin Era:** Early Kiyoran formation period (pre-imperial consolidation)  
**Language(s):** High Orunai (oral, formal), clan dialects (variable)  
**Defining Trait:** A lineage shaped by movement, where **power, identity, and survival exist only so long as they are maintained in motion**

---

# Overview

The Orunai are a human lineage native to the vast steppe regions of Kiyora, where the land stretches beyond fixed borders and permanence is an illusion. Their environment does not reward settlement or rigid structure—it rewards movement, cohesion, and the ability to carry identity across distance.

To be born Orunai is to inherit a world where nothing truly belongs to anyone unless it can be held in motion. Land cannot be owned in the traditional sense—it is passed through, contested, and revisited. Authority cannot remain static—it must be continually reinforced through action, presence, and loyalty.

Unlike the great imperial systems of Kiyora, which define themselves through structure and continuity, the Orunai define themselves through **ongoing cohesion**. Their identity is not rooted in place, but in people—clans, bonds, and shared direction. What endures is not territory, but the ability to remain unified while moving through an ever-changing world.

In the modern era, the Orunai exist both within and beyond the reach of imperial power. Some operate along the edges of Luming-controlled territories, others move freely across lands that no empire fully controls. They are often perceived as unpredictable or destabilizing, but this reflects a misunderstanding:

> they are not resisting order—they simply do not believe it exists without constant reinforcement

---

# Origins

The Orunai lineage formed across the open steppe regions of Kiyora, a landscape defined by:

• vast, unobstructed terrain  
• seasonal shifts in resources and climate  
• limited permanent natural boundaries  
• necessity of continuous movement for survival

Early populations in these regions faced a defining condition:

> stillness led to stagnation, and stagnation led to loss

Resources shifted with the seasons. Herds moved. Weather patterns changed unpredictably. Groups that remained in one place too long depleted their surroundings or became vulnerable.

Attempts to establish permanent settlements were often unsustainable.

Instead, survival depended on:

• mobility across large distances  
• strong internal cohesion within moving groups  
• flexible leadership structures that adapted to circumstance  
• the ability to maintain identity without reliance on place

Over time, this produced a lineage that did not build permanence into the land—

but into **people, movement, and shared direction**

---

# Lineage Identity

The Orunai are defined by an intrinsic orientation toward **movement as continuity**.

Common tendencies include:

• comfort with change and shifting environments  
• identity tied to group cohesion rather than location  
• emphasis on action and presence over abstract authority  
• distrust of systems that rely on static control

They do not ask:

> “Who controls this land?”

They ask:

> “Who can hold it—and for how long?”

Even when raised outside the steppe, Orunai individuals often:

• resist long-term confinement to rigid systems  
• prioritize relationships over institutions  
• adapt quickly to changing conditions

---

# Physical Characteristics

Orunai individuals tend toward:

• lean, durable builds suited to endurance and travel  
• strong posture and balance from riding and movement  
• efficient, purposeful physical motion

Common traits include:

• weathered or wind-exposed skin tones  
• dark hair, often worn for practicality  
• sharp, alert gaze

They often appear **ready to move, even at rest**

---

# Inherited Traits

Across cultures, Orunai individuals commonly exhibit:

• strong adaptability to changing environments  
• instinct for group cohesion and loyalty  
• comfort operating without fixed structure  
• quick decision-making in dynamic situations

They excel in:

• mobility and navigation  
• leadership in fluid or unstable environments  
• maintaining cohesion under pressure  
• rapid response to shifting conditions

Limitations include:

• difficulty integrating into rigid, hierarchical systems  
• resistance to static authority  
• potential fragmentation if cohesion weakens

---

# Relationship to Magic

The Orunai do not exhibit strong lineage-based magical tendencies.

However:

• they favor practical, immediate applications of magic  
• they distrust magic that requires long-term stability or fixed structures

To the Orunai:

> if it cannot move with you, it cannot be relied upon

---

# Diaspora and Integration

The Orunai maintain a **mobile and diffuse presence**.

They are found:

• along steppe routes and frontier regions  
• at the edges of imperial territories  
• within military or exploratory groups

They integrate selectively:

• often retaining strong internal group identity  
• adapting externally without fully adopting foreign structures  
• maintaining mobility even when temporarily settled

They do not disperse in the traditional sense—

they **extend outward while remaining connected**
