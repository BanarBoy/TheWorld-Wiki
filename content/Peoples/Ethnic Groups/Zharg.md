# Zharg

**Species:** Dwarf  
**Parent Culture(s):** [[Zharg]] (present in forge-heavy and volcanic regions across dwarven territories)  
**Primary Regions (Historic):** Volcanic zones, deep forges, magma-adjacent tunnel systems, and high-heat industrial centers  
**Current Distribution:** Regional / Clustered in extreme-environment settlements  
**Origin Era:** Early expansion into deep-heat zones following initial dwarven settlement  
**Language(s):** High Zhargic (forge cant), industrial dialects, clipped working speech  
**Defining Trait:** A lineage shaped by heat, pressure, and the understanding that creation requires destruction

---

# Overview

The Zharg are the lineage of transformation within dwarven society, defined by their relationship with **heat, force, and irreversible change**. Where others build, refine, or repair, the Zharg alter—breaking material down and reshaping it into something new.

To be Zharg is to understand that nothing remains as it was. Even outside traditional environments, individuals of this lineage tend to approach problems through **conversion rather than preservation**—what can be broken down, what can be reforged, what must be sacrificed to achieve a new form.

They are most commonly associated with forges, deep-earth industry, and extreme environments where other lineages struggle to operate.

> What is cannot remain—only what is remade endures.

---

# Origins

The Zharg lineage emerged when early dwarven expansion pushed into regions defined not by stability, but by **heat and volatility**.

These environments included:

• magma-adjacent tunnel systems  
• volcanic chambers  
• unstable geothermal regions  
• deep forges where temperatures approached material limits

Survival in these conditions required more than endurance or precision—it required an understanding that:

• materials would fail  
• structures would shift  
• stability was temporary

Rather than resist these conditions, early Zharg adapted to them.

They learned to:

• work within extreme heat  
• break down materials to their base components  
• reshape rather than preserve

Over time, this produced a lineage that did not fear destruction—

they **expected it**.

---

# Lineage Identity

The Zharg are defined by an intrinsic orientation toward **transformation through force**.

Common tendencies include:

• comfort with high-risk, high-impact processes  
• willingness to break existing systems to improve them  
• acceptance of loss as part of creation  
• preference for decisive action over gradual refinement

They do not hesitate when change is required.

Instead:

> they commit fully—and accept the cost

Even outside traditional environments, Zharg individuals often:

• dismantle systems to understand them  
• favor bold, irreversible solutions  
• act decisively where others deliberate

# Physical Characteristics

Zharg dwarves tend toward:

• dense, heat-conditioned builds  
• skin marked by long-term exposure to extreme environments  
• strong, force-oriented movement

Common traits include:

• darkened or heat-tempered skin tones  
• visible scarring or hardened tissue  
• steady tolerance for heat and physical strain

They often appear **forged rather than grown**.

# Inherited Traits

Across cultures, Zharg individuals commonly exhibit:

• high tolerance for heat and physical stress  
• decisiveness under pressure  
• willingness to take necessary risks  
• resilience in destructive or unstable environments

They excel in:

• forging and high-temperature metallurgy  
• demolition and restructuring  
• large-scale industrial processes  
• crisis response requiring decisive action

Limitations include:

• tendency toward overcommitment  
• acceptance of collateral damage where others would avoid it  
• difficulty with delicate or precision-focused work

# Relationship to Craft and Material

Zharg lineage expresses itself through **transformation of material under extreme conditions**.

They are not concerned with:

• preservation (like [[Durnak]])  
• perfection (like [[Mazzinark]])  
• salvage (like [[Brathnul]])

But with:

> **change through force**

Their work emphasizes:

• melting, reforging, and reshaping  
• high-energy processes  
• decisive conversion of material

A Zharg-forged object carries:

• strength born from stress  
• evidence of transformation  
• purpose defined by what it became—not what it was

---

# Diaspora and Integration

The Zharg are concentrated in environments others avoid.

They are commonly found in:

• major forge complexes  
• volcanic or geothermal regions  
• high-output industrial zones

They integrate less easily into:

• highly structured systems  
• precision-focused environments

But are indispensable where:

• transformation is required  
• large-scale change must occur quickly  
• failure to act decisively carries greater risk

# Naming Conventions

Zharg names tend to reflect:

• harsh, forceful phonetics  
• strong consonant clusters  
• shortened or clipped forms in working environments

Common traits include:

• names that feel abrupt or direct  
• reduced emphasis on formality  
• adaptation for ease of use in loud, industrial settings

Names are often practical rather than ceremonial.

# Relationship with Other Lineages

**[[Durnak]]:**  
Respected for stability, but seen as resistant to necessary change—too focused on preservation.

**[[Mazzinark]]:**  
Viewed as overly delicate—precision without transformation is incomplete.

**[[Brathnul]]:**  
Understood and often aligned—both accept imperfection, though Zharg prefer to remake rather than reuse.

**[[Stornavak]]:**  
Closely related in temperament—both act decisively, though Zharg channel force into transformation rather than impact.

**[[Roskul]]:**  
Seen as adaptable but distant—less connected to the realities of deep-earth change.
