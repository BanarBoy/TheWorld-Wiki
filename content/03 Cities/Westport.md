# Westport

**Type:** Port City / Trade Hub / Fishing Port  
**Continent:** [[Valecaryn]]  
**Region:** [[Western Coast]]  
**River / Coast:** Western coastline facing the great western sea  
**Population:** ~140,000  
**Elevation:** Rolling coastal hills surrounding a wide natural harbor  
**Government:** Merchant council led by influential shipping families and harbor guilds  
**Founded:** Approximately 620 years before present as a fishing settlement  
**Strategic Role:** Primary maritime gateway along Valecaryn’s western coastline

---

# Overview

Westport is one of the most important maritime cities along the **[[Western Coast]]** of Valecaryn. Built around a large natural harbor facing the western ocean, the city serves as a gateway for trade and travel across distant seas.

Unlike the highly structured ports of the Coinward Coast, Westport developed in a more relaxed and independent manner. Merchant captains, fishing fleets, and shipbuilders have shaped the city’s culture for centuries, giving it a reputation as a place where sailors and traders enjoy greater freedom than in many eastern ports.

The harbor supports a wide variety of maritime activity. Fishing vessels depart daily for the rich coastal waters, while merchant ships arrive carrying goods from distant regions. Shipyards along the waterfront construct and repair vessels used in long-distance ocean trade.

Although it is an active trade center, Westport retains a laid-back atmosphere compared to larger cities such as **[[Lethryn Prime]]**. The pace of life is slower, and the culture reflects a strong connection to the sea.

Travelers often describe Westport as a city where the ocean defines everything—from the economy and architecture to the stories told in its waterfront taverns.

# Physical Geography

## Regional Context

Westport lies along the rugged shoreline of the **[[Western Coast]]**, facing the vast western ocean.

The city sits:

• west of the inland forests and farmland of Valecaryn  
• south of the smaller coastal settlement **[[Driftwood]]**  
• far across the continent from the Coinward Coast ports such as **[[Velmar]]** and **[[Velossa]]**

Its location along the open ocean allows ships from distant lands to reach Valecaryn’s western shores.

---

## Immediate Terrain

Westport occupies a broad natural harbor surrounded by low coastal hills.

Key terrain features include:

• wide sheltered harbor waters  
• rocky headlands protecting the harbor entrance  
• sandy beaches stretching along nearby coastline  
• rolling coastal hills dotted with houses and lookout towers

The harbor itself is large enough to accommodate hundreds of ships, making it ideal for both fishing fleets and merchant vessels.

---

## Hydrology

Freshwater flows into the harbor through several small rivers and coastal streams.

Important water sources include:

• freshwater rivers descending from inland hills  
• natural springs feeding the city’s wells  
• reservoirs built to support the growing population

The harbor benefits from natural currents that help keep the waters relatively calm and navigable.

# Urban Structure

## City Layout

Westport grew naturally around one of the largest natural harbors along the **[[Western Coast]]**. Unlike many planned cities, its streets developed organically over centuries as fishermen, traders, and shipbuilders expanded the settlement along the waterfront.

The harbor forms the heart of the city. From the docks the city rises gradually up the surrounding coastal hills, creating layers of districts overlooking the water. The oldest neighborhoods sit closest to the harbor where the original fishing village once stood.

Winding streets climb the hills behind the waterfront, connecting residential districts, shipyards, and merchant quarters. These elevated areas provide sweeping views of the harbor and the open western sea.

Because the city expanded gradually over generations, its layout feels lively and irregular rather than strictly organized. This gives Westport a character that many sailors find welcoming and familiar.

---

## Districts

### The Old Harbor

The historic center of Westport and the site of the original fishing settlement. Narrow streets weave between weathered stone buildings, taverns, and maritime guild halls. Fishing boats and small merchant vessels dock here daily.

### The Grand Docks

A massive stretch of wharves and piers where larger merchant ships anchor. Warehouses, cargo cranes, and merchant counting houses dominate this district.

### The Shipyards

Located along the southern edge of the harbor, this district contains large shipbuilding facilities where ocean-going vessels are constructed and repaired. Sailmakers, rope-makers, and carpenters operate workshops throughout the area.

### Seafarer’s Row

A lively waterfront district filled with taverns, inns, gambling houses, and music halls frequented by sailors and travelers.

### The Hill Quarter

Residential neighborhoods built along the hills overlooking the harbor. Merchants and ship captains maintain comfortable homes here with views of the sea.

---

## Architecture

Westport’s architecture reflects centuries of maritime life.

Common building materials include:

• rough coastal stone  
• weathered timber beams  
• brick used in warehouses and dock structures

Roofs are often steep and reinforced to withstand heavy ocean winds and seasonal storms.

Buildings near the waterfront tend to be sturdy and utilitarian, while houses in the Hill Quarter are larger and more refined.

Many structures incorporate balconies or observation decks where residents can watch ships entering the harbor.

The overall aesthetic of the city is rugged, practical, and deeply tied to its maritime heritage.

Westport’s food supply comes from both sea and land.

Defensive features include:

The city maintains a small coastal patrol fleet to protect merchant vessels from pirates.

# Society and Culture

## Cultural Identity

Westport’s culture is deeply rooted in the traditions of the sea. Generations of sailors, fishermen, and shipbuilders have shaped the city into a place where maritime skill and experience are respected above titles or wealth.

Life in Westport moves at a steady but unhurried pace compared to the busy ports of the Coinward Coast. The harbor is always active, yet the city carries a relaxed atmosphere where sailors linger in taverns telling stories of distant voyages and storm-tossed seas.

Independence is highly valued among the people of Westport. Many merchant captains operate with a great deal of autonomy, and the city’s culture reflects a long tradition of self-reliance and maritime freedom.

The sea itself is central to the identity of the city. Residents grow up hearing tales of legendary voyages, lost ships, and distant lands beyond the western horizon.

---

## Social Hierarchy

Westport’s society is shaped by maritime professions rather than noble hierarchy.

**Merchant Captains**

Experienced ship captains who command large trading vessels and control significant trade routes.

**Shipping Families**

Established maritime families who own fleets of ships and maintain influence over harbor trade.

**Guild Leaders**

Leaders of maritime guilds such as shipwrights, fishermen, and dockworkers.

**Sailors and Fishermen**

The largest segment of the population, working aboard ships or within the fishing fleets.

**Dockworkers and Laborers**

Workers responsible for loading cargo, maintaining docks, and supporting harbor operations.

---

## Daily Life

Life in Westport begins early in the harbor.

Before sunrise fishing vessels depart for the coastal waters while dockworkers prepare cargo shipments for merchant ships.

During the day the docks remain busy as sailors unload goods, repair ships, and negotiate new voyages with merchant houses.

Markets along the waterfront fill with traders selling fresh fish, imported goods, and supplies for ships preparing to sail.

Evenings bring a lively atmosphere to the harbor districts. Taverns fill with sailors and travelers sharing food, drink, and stories from distant ports.

---

## Festivals and Holidays

Several celebrations in Westport revolve around the sea.

**Festival of the Western Wind**

A celebration marking the start of the favorable sailing season when merchant ships begin long-distance voyages.

**The Mariners’ Return**

A festival welcoming fleets returning from distant journeys.

**Harbor Founders’ Day**

A civic celebration honoring the original fishermen who established the settlement that became Westport.

---

## Cuisine

Cuisine in Westport reflects the abundance of the sea.

Common foods include:

• grilled fish and shellfish  
• seafood stews  
• salted fish preserved for long voyages  
• hearty breads and vegetables from inland farms

Meals are often accompanied by strong ales or rum-like spirits favored by sailors.

## Clothing

Clothing in Westport is practical and suited for maritime life.

Common garments include:

• heavy wool coats worn by sailors  
• leather boots designed for wet dock conditions  
• simple tunics and trousers

Merchant captains and wealthy traders often wear fine coats and hats, reflecting their status within the harbor community.

# Religion

Religion in Westport centers largely on maritime traditions.

Temples near the harbor are dedicated to deities associated with the sea, storms, and safe travel.

Sailors often leave offerings before long voyages and return to give thanks after surviving dangerous journeys.

Small shrines along the docks are maintained by sailor guilds to protect ships and crews.

# Government and Politics

## Political Structure

Westport is governed by a **merchant council composed of prominent shipping families and guild representatives**.

This council oversees harbor operations, regulates maritime trade, and resolves disputes between merchant captains and trade houses.

The system reflects the city’s long tradition of maritime independence.

---

## Power Centers

Several groups exert influence in Westport.

**Shipping Families**

Powerful merchant dynasties controlling fleets of trading vessels.

**Maritime Guilds**

Organizations representing sailors, shipbuilders, and dockworkers.

**Harbor Administration**

Officials responsible for maintaining port infrastructure and regulating trade.

---

## Law and Order

The **Harbor Watch of Westport** maintains order throughout the city.

Their duties include:

• protecting merchant shipments  
• preventing smuggling and piracy  
• patrolling harbor districts

Due to the constant presence of sailors and travelers, disputes occasionally arise in the tavern districts, but the watch generally maintains control.

---

# Military

## Garrison

Westport maintains a modest garrison of roughly **1,500 guards and coastal patrol sailors**.

These forces are responsible for harbor security and maintaining a small patrol fleet.

## Strategic Importance

Westport serves as a major maritime gateway along the Western Coast.

• supporting fishing fleets that supply large portions of the region
