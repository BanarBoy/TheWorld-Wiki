# The Blackwater Hunt

**Type:** Targeted Killing / Magical Confrontation / Personal Event  
**Location(s):** [[Darkwater Cay]], surrounding coastal waters  
**Time Period:** c. 1018–1020 (approx. 20 years before present)  
**Duration:** Several days of pursuit; less than one hour for the final encounter  
**Key Factions:** Independent pirate crews of Darkwater Cay  
**Key Figures:** [[Hadrian Garamond]], Leidric, Alcindor the Principled  
**Outcome:** Leidric is deliberately hunted and killed by Alcindor; Hadrian survives and is fundamentally changed by the encounter.

---

# Overview

The Blackwater Hunt was not a chance encounter—it was a deliberate pursuit.

In the years following the Fall of Sylrae, individuals exhibiting strong Sylraen traits became increasingly rare. Among them, Leidric stood out. His ability to align with weather and sea was not merely intuitive—it was developing into something precise, controlled, and visible enough to draw attention.

That attention found Alcindor.

Operating according to a personal and uncompromising philosophy, Alcindor identified Leidric as a rising threat—one not because of what he had done, but because of what he could become. He tracked him across the waters surrounding [[Darkwater Cay]], observing, confirming, and waiting.

When he chose to act, the outcome was never in doubt.

The final confrontation was brief, controlled, and entirely one-sided. Leidric was isolated and killed with precision. Hadrian Garamond, who stood beside him, was not the target—and was judged as such.

The encounter marks the moment Hadrian’s world fractured: the loss of his closest companion, and the realization that raw ability without control is ultimately defenseless against something more deliberate.

---

# Causes

• **Emergence of a Sylraen Talent** — Leidric’s growing ability made him increasingly visible to those capable of recognizing it.

• **Alcindor’s Philosophy** — Alcindor operates on the belief that certain forms of power must be confronted before they fully develop.

• **Deliberate Tracking** — Leidric was not discovered in passing; he was followed, observed, and selected.

• **Unaware Target** — Neither Leidric nor Hadrian understood they were being hunted.

---

# Key Events

**Initial Observation**  
Alcindor identifies Leidric as a Sylraen-aligned individual of unusual potential. He begins tracking him across multiple movements near [[Darkwater Cay]].

**Pattern Confirmation**  
Leidric’s influence over wind and sea is observed repeatedly. Alcindor confirms his assessment.

**Controlled Approach**  
Rather than striking immediately, Alcindor waits for a moment of isolation—minimizing interference and variables.

**The Interception**  
Hadrian and Leidric engage what they believe is a standard opportunity at sea. Alcindor enters the encounter.

**Collapse of Control**  
Leidric attempts to influence the environment. The response is immediate—his alignment fails to take hold as expected.

**Isolation of Target**  
Alcindor focuses entirely on Leidric. The rest of the encounter becomes irrelevant.

**Leidric’s Death**  
Leidric is killed quickly and decisively. No extended struggle, no escalation.

**Judgment of Hadrian**  
Hadrian confronts Alcindor and attempts resistance. He is overmatched but not killed. Alcindor states:

> “You are not what I seek.”

**Departure**  
Alcindor leaves without pursuit or further engagement.

# Major Factions

### Independent Pirate Crew (Darkwater Cay)

Operating with growing skill and coordination, but unaware of the level of threat they faced.

**Motivation:** Survival, profit, reputation.

---

### Alcindor (Independent Actor)

A singular figure operating under a defined and rigid philosophy.

**Motivation:** Identification and elimination of emerging powers he deems unacceptable or dangerous.

---

# Key Figures

**Hadrian Garamond — Witness and Survivor**  
A capable and rising figure whose worldview is shattered by the encounter. His later pursuit of control is rooted here.

**Leidric — Target**  
A naturally gifted Sylraen-aligned individual whose potential marked him for death before it could fully develop.

**Alcindor the Principled — Hunter**  
A figure of extreme precision and intent. His actions are selective, not reactive, and governed by principles not yet fully understood.

---

# Turning Points

• **Identification of Leidric** — Marks the beginning of the hunt, not the encounter.

• **Failure of Alignment** — Leidric’s inability to assert control over the environment signals a fundamental difference in power.

• **Selective Kill** — Confirms Alcindor’s intent was singular, not indiscriminate.

• **Spared Survivor** — Hadrian’s survival introduces uncertainty into Alcindor’s otherwise absolute logic.

---

# Outcome and Resolution

Leidric is killed.  
The hunt concludes.

Alcindor departs without explanation, leaving no immediate trace beyond the outcome itself.

The event ends as cleanly as it began.

---

# Consequences

**Short-Term:**

• Death of Leidric  
• Psychological shock and loss for Hadrian  
• Collapse of perceived control over their environment

**Long-Term:**

• Hadrian’s rejection of reliance on instinct or alignment alone  
• His pursuit of structured, enforceable magical control  
• A lasting personal vendetta—less emotional than ideological  
• Removal of a potentially powerful Sylraen-aligned individual

---

# Impact on the Modern World

Though not widely recorded, the event represents a critical pattern:

• Powerful Sylraen-aligned individuals may not be allowed to fully develop  
• There exist actors capable of identifying and eliminating such individuals  
• The philosophical divide between alignment and control is not theoretical—it is enforced
