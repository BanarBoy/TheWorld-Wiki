# Black Oak Timber

**Resource Type:** Timber / Strategic Construction Material  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Black Oak Forest]]  
**Major Production Centers:** [[Oakrest]], [[Fort Asten]], [[Greenhollow]]  
**Controlling Groups:** Timbercutters’ Guild, Sawmill Guild, regional timber merchant houses  
**Primary Uses:** Shipbuilding, fortress construction, heavy architecture, naval infrastructure  
**Trade Value:** Strategic

---

# Overview

Black Oak timber is one of the most valuable natural resources found in Valecaryn. Known for its exceptional strength, durability, and resistance to rot, it is widely considered the finest hardwood available for large-scale construction.

The towering Black Oak trees grow primarily within the dense woodlands of the **[[Black Oak Forest]]**, where centuries-old stands produce massive trunks suitable for beams, ship hulls, and defensive fortifications.

Because of its strength and longevity, Black Oak has become essential to several industries across the continent. Shipyards rely on it for the structural frames of ocean-going vessels, while cities and fortresses use it to reinforce gates, towers, and defensive walls.

Although the forest contains vast quantities of timber, the slow growth rate of Black Oak trees means that careful management is required to prevent overharvesting. As a result, the timber trade has become tightly regulated by guilds and merchant houses operating out of **[[Oakrest]]**.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Black Oak trees grow almost exclusively within the ancient forests of the **[[Black Oak Forest]]**.

The region’s unique soil composition, climate, and centuries of uninterrupted woodland growth allow the trees to reach exceptional size and density.

The largest stands are concentrated around:

• the deep forest regions surrounding [[Oakrest]]  
• logging territories near [[Fort Asten]]  
• the outer forest settlements around [[Greenhollow]]

Outside this region, Black Oak is extremely rare.

---

# Production

Harvesting Black Oak timber is a demanding and dangerous process.

Logging crews venture deep into the forest to identify mature trees suitable for harvesting. Once selected, teams of timbercutters use specialized saws and wedges to bring down the enormous trees.

After cutting, the trunks are trimmed and transported to lumber yards near **[[Oakrest]]**, where they are processed into beams, planks, and structural timbers.

Because the trees are extremely large and heavy, teams of oxen and draft horses are often required to transport the logs along forest roads.

---

# Processing and Transport

Once harvested, Black Oak logs are transported to sawmills in **[[Oakrest]]** where they are processed into usable lumber.

Processing typically includes:

• cutting large beams for ship frames  
• producing planks for hull construction  
• shaping structural timbers for architecture

After processing, the timber is shipped across Valecaryn using several transport systems:

• wagon caravans traveling inland trade routes  
• river transport where available  
• coastal shipping routes supplying shipyards and ports

Major ports such as **[[Velossa]]**, **[[Westport]]**, and **[[Lethryn Prime]]** regularly receive shipments of Black Oak for naval construction.

---

# Economic Importance

Black Oak timber plays a crucial role in the economy of Valecaryn.

Its primary uses include:

• shipbuilding for merchant fleets and naval vessels  
• construction of city gates and fortifications  
• structural beams used in major buildings  
• harbor infrastructure such as docks and piers

Because many critical industries rely on this resource, control of Black Oak production provides significant economic influence.

Cities dependent on shipbuilding or maritime trade are particularly reliant on steady supplies of Black Oak timber.

---

# Controlling Powers

Several groups influence the production and distribution of Black Oak timber.

**The Timbercutters’ Guild**

Responsible for regulating logging operations within the Black Oak Forest.

**The Sawmill Guild**

Controls lumber processing and oversees sawmill operations in [[Oakrest]].

**Timber Merchant Houses**

Commercial families that negotiate large-scale timber contracts with shipyards and construction projects.

---

# Trade Networks

Black Oak timber is exported across much of Valecaryn.

Major destinations include:

• coastal shipyards constructing naval vessels  
• fortress cities requiring strong defensive materials  
• large urban construction projects

Timber caravans frequently transport Black Oak toward ports along both the **Coinward Coast** and the **Western Coast**, where it can be shipped to distant markets.

---

# Cultural Significance

Black Oak has become a symbol of strength and resilience within Valecaryn.

Many naval traditions consider ships built with Black Oak frames to be exceptionally reliable in harsh seas.

Some fortress gates and ancient structures made from Black Oak have stood for centuries, further reinforcing the wood’s legendary reputation.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

Despite its abundance, Black Oak timber presents several challenges.

The trees grow slowly and require decades to reach harvestable size. Excessive logging could eventually reduce the forest’s long-term productivity.

Logging operations are also dangerous, as crews must work deep within dense forests where accidents and wildlife threats are common.

Because of its value, shipments of Black Oak timber occasionally attract bandits or smugglers attempting to intercept cargo.

---

# Notable Locations

Important locations connected to the Black Oak timber trade include:

**[[Oakrest]]**  
The central hub of timber harvesting and processing operations.

**[[Fort Asten]]**  
A fortified settlement protecting logging routes within the forest.

**[[Greenhollow]]**  
A smaller forest town supporting nearby logging camps.

---

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some foresters claim that certain merchant houses are secretly harvesting trees beyond the regulated logging zones.

Others believe that portions of the deepest forest remain unexplored and may contain even older and larger stands of Black Oak.

There are also whispers that rival trade houses have begun sabotaging each other’s logging operations in order to control the timber market.

# Plot Hooks

**The Forbidden Grove**

A section of the forest believed to contain ancient Black Oak trees has been declared off-limits by the guilds.

**The Missing Caravan**

A shipment of valuable Black Oak beams disappears while traveling toward the coast.

**The Sabotaged Mill**

One of Oakrest’s largest sawmills suffers a mysterious fire that halts production.

**The Ancient Tree**

Loggers discover a massive Black Oak tree unlike any seen before, believed to be centuries older than the surrounding forest.
