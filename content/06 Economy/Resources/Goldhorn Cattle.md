# Goldhorn Cattle

**Species Type:** Domesticated Livestock (Cattle)  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Gold Marches]]  
**Common Uses:** Beef production, dairy, leather, draft labor  
**Primary Products:** [[Goldhorn Beef]], dairy products, hides  
**Economic Importance:** Major agricultural foundation of Valecaryn

---

# Overview

Goldhorn Cattle are the dominant cattle breed raised throughout the fertile plains of the [[Gold Marches]]. Known for their broad bodies, hardy constitution, and distinctive curved horns tipped with pale golden coloration, they form the backbone of Valecaryn’s livestock economy.

Large herds graze across the expansive grasslands and pasturelands of the region, tended by ranchers and herdsmen whose livelihoods depend on the health and productivity of the animals. Over generations of selective breeding, Goldhorn cattle have become well adapted to the climate and terrain of the Gold Marches, thriving on open pasture and seasonal grazing cycles.

Because of their size and high meat yield, Goldhorn cattle produce the highly valued commodity known as [[Goldhorn Beef]], which is widely traded across Valecaryn and exported to foreign markets.

---

# Physical Characteristics

Goldhorn cattle are large, muscular animals well suited to open-range grazing.

Typical traits include:

• broad shoulders and strong legs  
• thick reddish-brown or dark chestnut coats  
• long sweeping horns that curve outward before turning forward  
• horn tips that often develop a pale golden hue with age  
• calm temperament when properly handled

Mature animals can grow quite large, making them ideal for meat production.

---

# Habitat and Grazing

Goldhorn cattle are most commonly raised across the wide grasslands of the [[Gold Marches]]. The region’s fertile soil and open pastures provide excellent grazing conditions throughout much of the year.

Herds are typically moved seasonally to prevent overgrazing, following established grazing routes maintained by local ranching communities.

Water sources such as rivers, streams, and seasonal ponds play an important role in determining grazing patterns.

---

# Husbandry

Ranching communities across the Gold Marches specialize in raising and managing Goldhorn herds.

Common practices include:

• open pasture grazing across large ranchlands  
• seasonal herd movements  
• selective breeding for size and meat quality  
• branding or marking animals for identification

Young calves are raised alongside the herd until they reach maturity, after which they may be sold to meat markets or retained for breeding.

---

# Economic Importance

Goldhorn cattle are one of the most important agricultural resources of Valecaryn.

The herds provide:

• beef for domestic consumption and export  
• leather used in clothing, armor, and equipment  
• dairy products such as cheese and butter  
• draft animals for farm work and transport

Large cattle drives regularly transport herds from the Gold Marches to major cities and trade centers including [[Aureth Caldras]] and [[Geldmere]].

---

# Cultural Significance

Cattle ranching is deeply embedded in the culture of the Gold Marches.

Many families have managed herds for generations, developing traditions and skills tied to livestock management. Seasonal cattle drives and regional livestock markets are important economic and social events in rural communities.

The image of the sweeping Goldhorn horns has also become a symbol associated with the strength and prosperity of Valecaryn’s agricultural heartland.

**[[Aureth Caldras]]**

---

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some ranchers claim that unusually large Goldhorn cattle have been bred in remote ranchlands deep within the Marches.

Others worry that expanding farmland may reduce grazing territory for the region’s massive herds.

There are also occasional conflicts between ranchers over grazing rights and water access.
