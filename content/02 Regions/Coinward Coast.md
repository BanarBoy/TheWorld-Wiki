# Coinward Coast

**Continent:** [[Valecaryn]]  
**Nation / Realm:** [[Crown of Valecaryn]]  
**Region Type:** Coastal Plain / Maritime Coast  
**Climate Zone:** Temperate Maritime  
**Primary Terrain:** Low coastal plains, sheltered bays, tidal estuaries, and inland pasturelands  
**Population:** ~1,300,000  
**Major Cities:** [[Lethryn Prime]], [[Velmar]], [[Rillwatch]]  
**Primary Economy:** Maritime trade, shipbuilding, fishing, cargo logistics, livestock export  
**Strategic Importance:** Valecaryn’s primary maritime trade corridor and naval gateway to the wider world

---

# Overview

The **Coinward Coast** stretches along the southeastern shoreline of Valecaryn and forms the kingdom’s most economically vital maritime region. The coastline hosts a chain of harbors and port cities that connect the inland resources of Valecaryn to international markets across the western seas.

At the center of the region stands **Lethryn Prime**, the largest port in the kingdom and the principal maritime gateway for Valecaryn’s trade. Massive docks and warehouses line its harbor, handling cargo from nearly every region of the realm before it is loaded onto ships bound for foreign shores.

Further along the coast lie several specialized ports.  **Velmar** is known for its shipyards and maritime artisans, producing vessels for merchant fleets and naval forces alike. **Rillwatch**, positioned along a strategic coastal approach, functions as both a defensive harbor and an important waypoint for ships navigating the coastline.

To travelers arriving by sea, the Coinward Coast appears as a landscape of bustling ports, towering masts, and endless cargo traffic. Inland trade routes converge on these harbors, carrying timber, livestock, grain, and wine toward the docks where the wealth of Valecaryn is loaded onto ships and sent across the world.

---

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Coinward Coast lies along the southeastern edge of Valecaryn, where the kingdom’s interior trade routes meet the open sea.

The region borders:

• the **Crownlands** to the northwest  
• the **Ambervale Downs** to the west  
• inland ranchlands and agricultural territories further north  
• the open western seas to the east and south

Because the coast sits at the terminus of several major inland trade routes, the region naturally developed as the kingdom’s primary maritime outlet.

Ships departing from the Coinward Coast connect Valecaryn to distant markets across the seas, including major trade partners throughout Thalassar.

---

## Terrain

The terrain of the Coinward Coast consists primarily of **broad coastal plains broken by natural harbor inlets and low ridges**.

Key landscape features include:

• deep natural bays suitable for large ports  
• sandy and rocky shorelines  
• low rolling hills inland  
• tidal marshlands near river mouths  
• pasturelands supporting livestock herds

These geographic conditions created numerous natural harbors, allowing multiple port cities to develop along the coast.

The plains just inland from the coast are particularly well suited for grazing livestock, which supports one of the region’s major export industries.

---

## Hydrology

The coastline is shaped by several small rivers and estuaries draining inland plains.

Important water systems include:

• coastal estuaries used as natural harbors  
• tidal wetlands and salt marshes  
• inland streams connecting trade routes to ports

These waterways historically allowed goods from inland regions to be transported efficiently to coastal markets.

---

## Climate

The Coinward Coast experiences a **mild maritime climate** influenced heavily by ocean winds.

### Spring

Frequent coastal rains and morning fog are common as the sea warms.

### Summer

Warm temperatures and strong sea breezes dominate the season, creating ideal conditions for maritime travel and port activity.

### Autumn

Shipping activity remains high, though seasonal storms occasionally sweep along the coast.

### Winter

Winters are cool and wet with powerful coastal winds, though temperatures rarely fall far below freezing.

The sea moderates temperature extremes throughout the year.

A busy port city dominated by merchant guilds and trading houses.

**[[Velmar]] — Shipbuilding City**

Home to the region’s largest shipyards and maritime craftsmen.

**[[Rillwatch]] — Coastal Fortress Port**

---

## Trade and Transportation

Trade routes converge on the Coinward Coast from across Valecaryn.

Major transportation corridors include:

• coastal shipping lanes linking regional ports  
• caravan roads connecting inland markets to Lethryn Prime  
• timber roads carrying hardwood from forest regions  
• cattle routes transporting livestock from inland ranchlands

Large merchant fleets operate constantly from the ports of Lethryn Prime and Velossa.

---

## Infrastructure

Major infrastructure includes:

• extensive port complexes and cargo docks  
• shipyards and dry docks in Velmar  
• lighthouse networks guiding ships along the coast  
• coastal fortifications protecting key harbors  
• massive warehouse districts handling export goods

These systems make the Coinward Coast one of the most developed maritime regions in the known world.

---

# Economy

## Major Industries

The Coinward Coast hosts the logistical infrastructure that connects Valecaryn’s interior resources to global markets.

Major industries include:

• maritime trade  
• cargo logistics and port handling  
• shipbuilding and ship repair  
• fishing fleets  
• timber processing and export preparation  
• livestock transport and meat processing

Large freight yards and warehouses near Lethryn Prime handle enormous cargo volumes arriving from inland trade routes.

---

## Major Exports

Exports shipped from the Coinward Coast include:

• **Black Oak timber** used in shipbuilding and construction  
• **Ironwood beams** prized for fortress and naval construction  
• **beef and salted meats** from Valecaryn’s ranchlands  
• **grain** from the Gold Marches  
• **wine** from Ambervale Downs and Merrowfen vineyards  
• **wool and animal hides**

Timber shipments are particularly valuable, as Valecaryn’s hardwood forests produce some of the strongest shipbuilding lumber in the world.

Much of this cargo is loaded at **Lethryn Prime**, making it one of the busiest ports on the continent.

---

## Major Imports

Imports arriving through the region include:

• spices and luxury foods  
• rare metals and manufactured goods  
• foreign textiles  
• **coffee from Kelresh**  
• exotic wines and liquors

These goods are distributed inland through merchant networks tied to **Geldmere** and the Crownlands.

# Society and Culture

## Cultural Identity

The people of the Coinward Coast are deeply shaped by maritime life.

Common cultural traits include:

• seafaring traditions  
• merchant pragmatism  
• openness to foreign cultures

Many families have worked in maritime industries for generations.

---

## Population Distribution

Population is heavily concentrated in port cities.

Smaller fishing villages and farming communities are scattered along the coast and inland plains.

---

## Traditions and Festivals

Important regional traditions include:

• maritime festivals celebrating successful voyages  
• ship launching ceremonies  
• large trade fairs attracting merchants from distant lands

---

# Government and Control

The region falls under the authority of the Valecaryni crown but is heavily influenced by maritime merchants and port authorities.

Several noble houses maintain economic interests in the region’s ports and shipping fleets.

---

# Military Importance

The Coinward Coast holds enormous strategic importance.

Key advantages include:

• control of Valecaryn’s largest naval ports  
• shipbuilding infrastructure supporting merchant and naval fleets  
• coastal fortifications protecting major harbors

Any naval power seeking influence in Valecaryn must contend with the ports of this region.
