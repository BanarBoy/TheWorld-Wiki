# Gold Marches

**Continent:** [Valecaryn]]  
**Region Type:** Plains / Agricultural Basin  
**Climate Zone:** Temperate Continental  
**Primary Terrain:** Vast fertile plains and gently rolling farmland  
**Population:** ~900,000  
**Major Cities:** [[Sarnhold]], [[Aureth Caldras]]  
**Primary Economy:** Grain agriculture, cattle ranching, food supply  
**Strategic Importance:** Primary breadbasket of Valecaryn and one of the most fertile agricultural regions on the continent

---

# Overview

The **Gold Marches** are the most fertile farmlands in Valecaryn and among the richest agricultural territories on the continent of Aurelia. Vast plains of deep soil stretch across the region, producing enormous harvests of grain that feed cities throughout the kingdom.

The region’s name comes from the golden waves of wheat and barley that blanket the plains each harvest season. Grain grown in the Marches supplies major urban centers including the nearby capital **Aureth Caldras**, making the region vital to the stability of the realm.

At the center of the region stands **Sarnhold**, a large market city surrounded by enormous granaries and trading depots. Farmers and estate managers from across the plains bring their harvests here before grain is distributed along trade routes leading north to the Crownlands and south toward major export markets.

Though much of the population lives in scattered villages and estate lands, the wealth generated by the Marches gives the region enormous economic influence. Many of Valecaryn’s most powerful landowners maintain estates here, and control of its harvests has long been considered one of the greatest sources of political leverage within the kingdom.

---

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Gold Marches lie in the central interior of Valecaryn.

The region sits:

• southwest of capital [[Aureth Caldras]]  
• west of the **Ambervale Downs**  
• east of the **Black Oak Forest**  
• north of southern agricultural and trade regions

Because of its central location, the Marches sit near several of the kingdom’s major transportation corridors.

Grain harvested here moves quickly toward population centers such as Aureth Caldras and toward major trade routes leading to coastal ports.

---

## Terrain

The terrain consists of **broad open plains broken by gentle rolling hills**.

Important features include:

• deep fertile soils formed by ancient river sediments  
• long open plains ideal for large-scale farming  
• low ridges that divide agricultural districts  
• scattered streams and irrigation channels

The lack of major natural barriers allowed the region to develop enormous contiguous farmlands.

These open landscapes also make the region visually distinctive, with wide horizons and uninterrupted fields stretching for miles.

---

## Hydrology

The region is supported by several small rivers and tributaries that feed into the greater river systems of Valecaryn.

Important water sources include:

• seasonal streams flowing from surrounding highlands  
• irrigation canals constructed to support large farms  
• shallow rivers used for local transport of grain

Reliable rainfall combined with fertile soils makes the region extremely productive.

---

## Climate

The Gold Marches experience a **temperate continental climate** well suited to grain farming.

### Spring

Frequent rains nourish newly planted crops.

### Summer

Warm temperatures and long daylight hours allow grain fields to grow rapidly.

### Autumn

Harvest season dominates the region, with enormous grain yields gathered before winter.

### Winter

Cold but manageable winters allow farmland to recover before the next planting season.

Seasonal winds help prevent crop diseases and improve growing conditions.

# Ecology

## Flora

### Native Vegetation

Before large-scale farming, the region supported:

• tall prairie grasses  
• scattered oak groves  
• wildflower meadows

Most natural vegetation has been replaced by cultivated farmland.

---

### Agricultural Landscape

Today the region is dominated by large agricultural estates.

Common features include:

• massive wheat and barley fields  
• cattle pastures  
• irrigation canals  
• grain storage complexes

Many estates operate on an enormous scale.

---

### Economically Important Plants

Important crops include:

• wheat  
• barley  
• rye  
• oats

These grains form the foundation of the region’s economy.

---

## Fauna

### Common Wildlife

Wildlife in the region includes:

• deer  
• foxes  
• hares  
• migratory birds

Bird populations are especially common near grain fields.

---

### Predators and Dangerous Creatures

Predators are rare but include:

• wolves in more remote plains  
• wild dogs  
• large hawks

---

### Domesticated Animals

Livestock plays a major role in the regional economy.

Common animals include:

• cattle  
• horses  
• sheep  
• pigs  
• chickens

Cattle ranching complements grain farming throughout the plains.

---

# Human Geography

## Major Settlements

**[[Sarnhold]] — Granary City**

The largest settlement in the region and the central hub of the grain trade.

Sarnhold contains enormous storage facilities where harvests from across the Marches are collected and redistributed.

Smaller villages and estate communities surround the city across the plains.

---

## Trade and Transportation

The region is connected by extensive agricultural roads.

Major transportation routes include:

• grain roads linking farms to Sarnhold  
• caravan routes leading north toward Aureth Caldras  
• trade roads connecting to coastal export ports

Large grain caravans regularly travel through the region during harvest seasons.

---

## Infrastructure

Important infrastructure includes:

• massive granaries in Sarnhold  
• irrigation canals across the plains  
• road networks connecting farms to market towns  
• storage depots protecting harvest reserves

These systems allow the region to feed much of Valecaryn.

---

# Economy

## Major Industries

The region’s economy revolves around agriculture.

Primary industries include:

• grain farming  
• cattle ranching  
• grain storage and distribution  
• agricultural trade

The scale of production makes the Gold Marches one of the most productive farming regions in the world.

---

## Major Exports

Exports include:

• wheat and barley  
• flour and grain products  
• beef and livestock

Much of this food supply is shipped to major cities such as Aureth Caldras and exported through coastal ports.

---

## Major Imports

Imports include:

• tools and metal equipment  
• luxury goods  
• wine from neighboring regions

Trade with nearby regions supports the agricultural economy.

---

# Society and Culture

## Cultural Identity

The people of the Gold Marches are closely tied to the rhythms of farming life.

Values commonly emphasized include:

• hard work  
• land stewardship  
• seasonal traditions

Large estate households dominate much of the rural culture.

---

## Population Distribution

Population is widely distributed across farms and estate lands.

The only major urban center is **Sarnhold**, with smaller villages scattered across the plains.

---

## Traditions and Festivals

Important cultural traditions include:

• harvest festivals celebrating the grain harvest  
• seasonal agricultural fairs  
• livestock markets held in Sarnhold

These events attract merchants and travelers from across Valecaryn.

---

# Government and Control

The region technically falls under the authority of the Valecaryni crown.

However, many powerful noble estates dominate the region’s farmland.

Control of large agricultural estates often translates into significant political influence.

---

# Military Importance

Though not heavily fortified, the Gold Marches hold enormous strategic value.

Control of the region means control of Valecaryn’s food supply.

Armies operating in the region rely heavily on its grain reserves.

---

# History

## Early Settlement

Early settlers established farms across the fertile plains centuries ago.

---

## Development

Agricultural expansion gradually transformed the region into Valecaryn’s primary grain-producing area.

Large estate systems emerged to manage the enormous farmland.

---

## Major Conflicts

During the Crown Crisis, several factions attempted to control the region’s harvests to secure food supplies for their armies.

---

## Modern Era

Today the Gold Marches remain the agricultural heart of Valecaryn.

---

# Notable Landmarks

• the **Great Granaries of Sarnhold**  
• enormous estate farms across the plains  
• ancient irrigation canals still used today

---

# Reputation

Outsiders often describe the region as:

• the **breadbasket of Valecaryn**  
• the richest farmland in Aurelia  
• a land of endless golden fields

---

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some whisper that rival noble estates secretly compete for control of the region’s grain trade.

Others fear that a single failed harvest could threaten food supplies across the kingdom.
