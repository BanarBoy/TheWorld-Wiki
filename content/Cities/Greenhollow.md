# Greenhollow

**Type:** Agricultural Town / Regional Market Hub  
**Continent:** [[Valecaryn]]  
**Region:** Central Lowlands (Edgemoor Basin)  
**River / Coast:** [[Lake Edgemoor]] (connected to Virelois watershed)  
**Population:** ~28,000  
**Elevation:** Lowland basin, slightly above lake level  
**Government:** Crown-aligned local council with strong guild influence  
**Founded:** Early Post-Unification Expansion (c. 900–1000)  
**Strategic Role:** Agricultural anchor and inland supply node supporting central Valecaryn

---

# Overview

Greenhollow is a quiet but indispensable settlement nestled along the shores of [[Lake Edgemoor]], serving as one of the most stable agricultural and provisioning centers in central Valecaryn.

Unlike the great cities of trade or power, Greenhollow exists to **produce and sustain**. It feeds nearby regions, supports inland trade routes, and provides a reliable source of grain, livestock, and freshwater resources. Its importance is not immediately visible—but it is deeply felt across the systems it supports.

To outsiders, Greenhollow is often described as unremarkable—orderly fields, calm waters, and steady routines. But that simplicity is deceptive. The town operates with quiet efficiency, and its output flows outward into larger networks tied to [[Geldmere]] and the Virelois corridor.

Greenhollow does not seek influence.

> It ensures that influence can exist elsewhere.

---

# Physical Geography

## Regional Context

Greenhollow sits within the Edgemoor Basin, a fertile lowland region formed by long-term water flow patterns feeding into the greater [[Virelois River]] system.

It lies:

• east of the Black Oak Forest  
• west of major inland trade routes leading toward [[Geldmere]]  
• centered on [[Lake Edgemoor]], which provides water stability and local transport

The city developed here because of:

• rich soil conditions  
• reliable freshwater access  
• natural protection from extreme terrain

It occupies one of the most **naturally stable agricultural zones** in Valecaryn.

---

## Immediate Terrain

Greenhollow is built on gently sloping land that rises slightly from the shores of Lake Edgemoor.

• soft, loamy soil ideal for cultivation  
• gradual elevation preventing severe flooding  
• wide, open terrain with minimal natural obstruction  
• shallow depressions used for irrigation and water retention

The settlement itself avoids the lowest ground near the lake, instead occupying slightly elevated terrain that balances access with safety.

---

## Hydrology

Water defines Greenhollow.

• Lake Edgemoor provides a consistent freshwater source  
• small tributaries feed into and out of the lake  
• irrigation channels extend into surrounding farmland  
• controlled drainage systems prevent waterlogging

Seasonal variation is moderate:

• spring runoff increases lake levels  
• summer irrigation draws from lake reserves  
• autumn stabilizes water flow  
• winter sees reduced surface movement

Flooding is rare but managed through careful land planning.

---

## Climate

Greenhollow experiences a temperate inland climate with predictable seasonal cycles.

### Spring

• mild temperatures with increasing rainfall  
• strong growth period for crops  
• occasional lake fog in early mornings

---

### Summer

• warm and stable conditions  
• moderate rainfall supporting sustained agriculture  
• long daylight hours maximizing productivity

---

### Autumn

• cool, dry air  
• primary harvest season  
• stable weather with minimal storm disruption

---

### Winter

• cold but not extreme  
• occasional frost and light snowfall  
• reduced agricultural activity but continued livestock care

Wind patterns are generally mild, with occasional stronger gusts coming off the lake.

# Urban Structure

## City Layout

Greenhollow is organized around **function and access**, rather than defense or prestige.

The city develops outward from the lake’s edge and central market areas:

• a loose radial layout centered on trade and gathering points  
• roads extending outward into farmland and connecting routes  
• low-density expansion with clear separation between urban and agricultural zones

There are no walls—only defined boundaries created by land use and natural geography.

---

## Architecture

Greenhollow’s architecture is practical and resource-driven.

• timber and stone construction  
• low-rise buildings with wide spacing  
• pitched roofs suited to seasonal weather

Structures are built to last—but not to impress.

The city reflects its purpose:

• efficient

• enduring

# Infrastructure

## Roads and Transportation

Greenhollow is defined by **overland and lake-based movement**, rather than major river or maritime systems.

• well-maintained dirt and stone roads connecting to inland trade routes toward [[Geldmere]]  
• cart paths extending outward into surrounding farmland  
• lakeside transport using small barges and fishing vessels on [[Lake Edgemoor]]  
• limited long-distance caravan traffic passing through rather than originating here

Movement is steady and practical, designed to support **continuous agricultural output** rather than rapid transit.

---

## Water Systems

Water management is one of Greenhollow’s greatest strengths.

• direct access to [[Lake Edgemoor]] for freshwater supply  
• irrigation channels feeding surrounding fields  
• drainage ditches preventing soil saturation  
• communal wells supplementing lake access

Water is not scarce—it is **carefully managed and distributed**.

---

## Food Supply

Greenhollow is largely self-sufficient and produces surplus.

• extensive grain production (wheat, barley)  
• livestock from surrounding pastures (cattle, sheep)  
• freshwater fish from Lake Edgemoor  
• preserved goods stored for winter and export

Surplus is regularly transported to:

• [[Geldmere]]  
• nearby inland settlements  
• trade routes feeding larger urban centers

---

## Defenses

Greenhollow has minimal formal defenses.

• no city walls  
• small local watch presence  
• reliance on distance from major conflict zones  
• informal militia drawn from local population if needed

Its protection comes from:

• stability  
• lack of strategic aggression  
• integration into larger Crown systems

• [[Geldmere]] (financial and distribution center)

## Labor and Guilds

Labor in Greenhollow is practical and organized.

• farming families forming the backbone of production  
• guild oversight in milling, storage, and distribution  
• seasonal labor cycles tied to planting and harvest

Guild presence exists—but is less dominant than in major cities.

Work is defined more by **necessity than ambition**.

# Society and Culture

## Cultural Identity

Greenhollow is defined by **stability, routine, and quiet competence**.

Its people value:

• consistency over innovation  
• reliability over prestige  
• community over individual recognition

There is little interest in political maneuvering or cultural influence.

Instead, the prevailing mindset is simple:

> Do the work.  
> Keep things running.

---

## Social Hierarchy

The social structure is relatively flat compared to larger cities.

• landholding families and established farmers hold informal influence  
• guild-affiliated workers (millers, traders) maintain economic roles  
• laborers and seasonal workers form the broader population

There is no dominant noble presence—authority is **localized and practical**.

---

## Clothing

# Religion

Greenhollow follows the broader traditions of **The Rite of Tor**, but in a quieter, more practical form than seen in cities like [[High Tor]].

There are no grand temples or monumental structures. Instead, religious life is integrated into daily existence:

• small shrines dedicated to continuity, harvest, and stewardship  
• seasonal observances tied to planting and harvest cycles  
• blessings of land, water, and livestock

Faith here is not ceremonial—it is **functional**.

The people of Greenhollow do not seek to interpret the will of the Rite.

They seek to **live in alignment with it**.

Traveling clergy occasionally pass through, particularly during major seasonal events, but permanent religious authority is minimal.

# Government and Politics

## Political Structure

Greenhollow is governed through a **Crown-aligned local council**.

• council composed of landholders, guild representatives, and senior figures  
• decisions focused on land use, water management, and trade coordination  
• limited direct interference from major noble houses

The Crown’s authority is present—but distant.

Governance is **practical, not political**.

---

## Power Centers

Power in Greenhollow is distributed across function rather than hierarchy.

Key influences include:

• landholding families controlling large agricultural output  
• milling and storage guilds managing processing and distribution  
• trade coordinators linking Greenhollow to [[Geldmere]]

Unlike larger cities:

• noble houses have limited direct presence  
• influence is tied to **what you produce, not who you are**

---

## Law and Order

Law enforcement is minimal but effective.

• small town watch maintaining order  
• disputes resolved through council mediation  
• strong reliance on social accountability

Crime is rare, and when it occurs, it is typically:

• theft of goods or livestock  
• disputes over land or water usage

Order is maintained through **community structure rather than force**.

---

# Military

## Garrison

Greenhollow does not maintain a formal standing military force.

• small local militia drawn from the population  
• basic training for defense and emergency response  
• limited access to arms and equipment

In times of need:

• support can be requested from nearby Crown forces  
• regional defense networks provide backup

---

## Strategic Importance

Greenhollow holds **low direct military value**, but high logistical importance.

• supports food supply chains across central Valecaryn  
• stabilizes surrounding regions through consistent output  
• acts as a fallback provisioning center during disruptions

It is not defended because it is not contested.

Its value lies in being **kept intact**.

# Demographics

Greenhollow’s population is predominantly human, with a stable and locally rooted community.

• majority Valecaryni agricultural families  
• small number of traveling merchants and laborers

Population characteristics:

• multi-generational households  
• strong community familiarity  
• low population turnover

Foreign presence is minimal and usually temporary.

The people of Greenhollow are not transient.

They are **established**.

# History

## Founding

Greenhollow was established during the early period of post-unification stabilization, when the restored Crown sought to secure reliable food production across central Valecaryn.

The Edgemoor Basin, with its fertile soil and stable water supply from [[Lake Edgemoor]], was identified as an ideal agricultural zone. Initial settlement began as a series of organized farming communities, supported by Crown-backed infrastructure projects focused on irrigation and land division.

Unlike many settlements born from conflict or trade opportunity, Greenhollow was created with a clear purpose:

**to produce consistently, and at scale.**

---

## Growth

Greenhollow expanded gradually and deliberately.

• early irrigation systems allowed for sustained agricultural output  
• consolidation of farmland into coordinated production zones  
• development of a central market to distribute surplus goods  
• integration into inland trade routes connecting to [[Geldmere]]

Over time, smaller surrounding settlements were absorbed into the growing town, forming a unified agricultural hub.

Its growth was not rapid—but it was **stable and continuous**.

---

## Wars and Conflicts

Greenhollow has largely avoided direct involvement in major conflicts.

During the later stages of the Crown Crisis:

• the region remained relatively insulated from large-scale warfare  
• agricultural output was intermittently disrupted but never fully collapsed

In the modern era:

• the town has not been a target of military action  
• its role as a supply center has made it valuable—but not contested

Conflict here has been indirect:

• disputes over land ownership  
• water rights tensions  
• occasional bandit activity along trade routes

---

## Recent Developments

In recent years, subtle pressures have begun to emerge.

• increased demand from larger cities has strained production expectations  
• minor fluctuations in [[Lake Edgemoor]]’s water levels have raised concern  
• outside interest from merchant groups has begun to influence local trade structures

Nothing has broken.

But the system is beginning to feel **pressure**.
