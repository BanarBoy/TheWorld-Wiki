**Continent:** [[Valecaryn]]
**Nation / Realm:** [[Valecaryn]]

**Climate Zone:** Temperate  
**Primary Terrain:** Rolling upland terraces, river-fed slopes, cultivated hills  
**Population:** ~400,000
**Major Cities:** (None) — administered from [[Merrowfen]]  
**Primary Economy:** Viticulture (high-quality red wine production)  
**Strategic Importance:** High-value luxury production tied to noble markets and long-distance trade

---

# Overview

The Merrowfen Vineyards are a renowned agricultural region located northwest of [[Merrowfen]], occupying elevated terrain along the upper Virelois basin. The region takes its name from [[House Merrowfen]], whose early estates shaped its development, though the land today is divided among numerous vineyard holdings of varying size and prestige.

Over centuries, the region expanded beyond its founding estates into a broader network of vineyards, manors, and agricultural communities. While House Merrowfen retains some of the oldest and most respected holdings, it no longer exerts total control. Instead, the region functions as a loosely connected economic zone defined by shared practices, competition, and reputation.

Today, the Merrowfen Vineyards are one of the most important wine-producing regions in Valecaryn. Their output supports both noble consumption and wider trade, making the region a cornerstone of the kingdom’s agricultural economy as well as a center of refinement and rivalry.

---

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Merrowfen Vineyards lie northwest of [[Merrowfen]], positioned along the transitional zone between inland riverlands and the expanding Virelois Delta.

They are situated:

• upstream from the delta region centered on [[Merrowfen]]  
• along elevated terrain overlooking stretches of the Virelois basin  
• between more intensive agricultural regions and less developed uplands

This placement allows access to river systems while avoiding the instability and congestion of the delta.

---

## Terrain

The region is defined by:

• **rolling upland hills** shaped into vineyard terraces  
• **moderate elevation changes** creating varied sun exposure  
• **well-drained soils** ideal for grape cultivation  
• **long, sloping ridgelines** used for estate development

The land is heavily shaped by human intervention, with natural contours refined over generations to support viticulture.

---

## Hydrology

• fed indirectly by branches and channels connected to the [[Virelois River]]  
• smaller tributaries and controlled irrigation systems distribute water across estates  
• natural drainage prevents oversaturation, critical for vine health

Water management is subtle but highly controlled.

---

## Climate

The region benefits from slightly drier and more stable conditions than the delta below.

**Spring**  
• moderate rainfall supports early vine growth

**Summer**  
• warm, consistent sunlight  
• minimal extreme weather

**Autumn**  
• dry, stable conditions ideal for harvest

**Winter**  
• cool and mild, allowing proper seasonal dormancy

The balance between warmth and dryness is key to the quality of the grapes.

# Government and Control

The Merrowfen Vineyards are not governed by a single authority.

• [[House Merrowfen]] holds the oldest and most prestigious estates  
• numerous independent and lesser noble estates operate across the region  
• informal influence is shaped by reputation, quality, and trade relationships  
• coordination occurs through trade networks centered on [[Merrowfen]]

House Merrowfen’s power lies less in direct control and more in **legacy, scale, and influence**.
