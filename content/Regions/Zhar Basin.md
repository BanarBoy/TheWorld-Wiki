# Overview

The Zhar Basin is the fertile heart of Thalassar—a vast network of river-fed floodplains carved from an otherwise unforgiving landscape. Fed by the life-giving waters of the Zhar River and its tributaries, the region produces much of the continent’s staple food supply, along with a number of high-value agricultural exports.

Yet the Basin is not simply fertile—it is **controlled fertility**. The seasonal flooding that sustains the land is carefully managed through a system of irrigation channels, levees, and ritual observances overseen by the Azhariel priesthood. Agriculture here is not merely labor—it is doctrine.

To outsiders, the Zhar Basin appears paradoxical: a place of abundance governed by strict religious authority. Its people are not impoverished, but neither are they free. The river gives—but only through those who claim to speak for it.

Historically, the Basin has been the foundation upon which multiple Thalassari powers have risen and fallen. Whoever controls its waters controls its people—and by extension, much of the continent’s stability.

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Zhar Basin lies in central Thalassar, stretching along the course of the [[Zhar River]] from its inland tributaries toward southern trade corridors.

It is bordered by:

- the [[Glasswaste Basin]] to the west
- the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]] to the east
- the [[Verdant Expanse]] to the east
- dry transitional uplands to the north

The Basin acts as a natural corridor between inland Thalassar and coastal trade systems, making it a critical link in overland and river-based trade.

---

## Terrain

The region is dominated by:

- wide, flat floodplains
- sediment-rich agricultural fields
- irrigation-cut channels and controlled basins
- raised settlement mounds to avoid flooding

Beyond irrigated land, the terrain quickly transitions into dry scrubland and encroaching desert.

---

## Hydrology

The Zhar River defines the region.

- seasonal flooding deposits fertile silt
- tributaries expand farming zones
- irrigation canals distribute water across controlled land

Water flow is regulated through canal gates, levees, and temple-controlled systems.

Control of water is control of life.

---

## Climate

Spring  
Flood season replenishes soil.

Summer  
Hot and humid within irrigated zones; peak agricultural activity.

Autumn  
Harvest season with stable river levels.

Winter  
Dry and cooler; irrigation maintenance and religious observances.

Rainfall is minimal—agriculture depends almost entirely on river cycles.

# Human Geography

## Major Settlements

- [[Zharakel]] — religious and administrative heart of the Basin, seat of Azhariel authority and center of flood ritual governance
- [[Port Iskene]] — primary river port and trade outlet, where agricultural goods are consolidated and shipped toward coastal markets
- [[Tarzhir]] — major inland distribution hub overseeing irrigation networks and coordinating grain and spice movement between regions
- [[Tal'mir Fields]] — vast agricultural zone and population center, representing the largest continuous stretch of cultivated land in the Basin
- [[Sarith Fen]] — wetland settlement along the outer floodplain, known for reed cultivation, medicinal plants, and difficult-to-govern terrain
