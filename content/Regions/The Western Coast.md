# Western Coast

**Continent:** [[Aurelia]]  
**Nation / Realm:** [[Valecaryn]]  
**Region Type:** Coastal Highlands / Maritime Coast  
**Climate Zone:** Temperate Maritime  
**Primary Terrain:** Rugged coastline, cliffs, rocky bays, and coastal forests  
**Population:** ~420,000  
**Major Cities:** [[Westport]], [[Driftwood]]  
**Primary Economy:** Fishing, coastal trade, shipbuilding, timber export  
**Strategic Importance:** Western maritime gateway of Valecaryn and staging ground for long-distance sea travel

---

# Overview

The **Western Coast** stretches along the windswept western shoreline of Valecaryn, where rugged cliffs and deep bays meet the open sea. Unlike the bustling commercial harbors of the Coinward Coast, this region developed more gradually as a collection of fishing towns and hardy maritime settlements.

The largest city of the region is **Westport**, a major harbor built around a natural bay protected from the harshest ocean storms. Westport serves as the primary port on Valecaryn’s western frontier and a launching point for ships traveling to distant western waters.

Further north lies **Driftwood**, a smaller but culturally distinctive coastal city known for its shipwrights and maritime traditions. The town grew around sheltered coves where fishermen and traders established early settlements.

The Western Coast has long been associated with seafaring independence. Sailors from the region are known for venturing far into the western ocean, and many of Valecaryn’s most experienced navigators hail from these coastal towns.

To visitors, the region appears dramatic and austere — a landscape of storm-lashed cliffs, grey seas, and hardy ports built to withstand the power of the ocean.

---

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Western Coast lies along the western edge of Valecaryn.

The region borders:

• the **Black Oak Forest** to the north  
• the **Crownlands** further northeast  
• the open western ocean to the west

Because of its position facing the open sea, the region historically developed as a maritime frontier connecting Valecaryn to distant ocean routes.

---

## Terrain

The terrain of the Western Coast is rugged and dramatic.

Major landscape features include:

• high coastal cliffs overlooking the ocean  
• rocky shorelines and narrow beaches  
• deep natural bays suitable for harbors  
• forested hills rising inland

Many settlements developed in sheltered coves where ships could anchor safely away from storms.

The coastline itself is highly irregular, with numerous inlets and promontories.

---

## Hydrology

Several small rivers descend from inland highlands and empty into the sea.

Important hydrological features include:

• coastal streams cutting through rocky valleys  
• natural harbors formed by river mouths  
• freshwater springs along cliffside formations

These water sources allowed early fishing settlements to develop along the coast.

---

## Climate

The Western Coast experiences a **cool maritime climate** dominated by ocean weather patterns.

### Spring

Cool and wet conditions dominate as coastal storms gradually weaken.

### Summer

Summers are mild and windy, with frequent sea breezes and fog rolling inland.

### Autumn

Autumn storms become more common as ocean winds intensify.

### Winter

Winters are harsh along the coast, with powerful storms and rough seas battering the shoreline.

Strong winds and heavy surf shape the region’s dramatic coastline.

# Ecology

## Flora

### Native Vegetation

The natural ecosystems include:

• coastal pine forests  
• wind-shaped oak trees  
• heathland grasses  
• moss and lichen along cliff faces

The harsh maritime climate limits large-scale agriculture.

---

### Agricultural Landscape

Agriculture is limited but present in sheltered valleys.

Typical farmland includes:

• small grain fields  
• livestock pastures  
• orchard groves protected from coastal winds

Fishing and maritime trade remain the dominant livelihoods.

---

### Economically Important Plants

Important plant resources include:

• coastal pine timber used in shipbuilding  
• hardwood transported from inland forests  
• flax used for sailcloth

Timber harvested inland is often shipped from western ports.

---

## Fauna

### Common Wildlife

Wildlife along the coast includes:

• seabirds nesting on cliffs  
• seals and sea lions along rocky shores  
• deer in inland forests  
• foxes and coastal scavengers

---

### Predators and Dangerous Creatures

Predators include:

• wolves in inland forests  
• large seabirds attacking fishing catches

Sailors occasionally tell stories of strange creatures seen far out at sea.

---

### Domesticated Animals

Common livestock includes:

• sheep grazing along coastal hills  
• cattle in sheltered valleys  
• pigs  
• chickens

Horses are used primarily for coastal transport.

# Human Geography

## Major Settlements

**[[Westport]] — Western Harbor City**

The largest city on the western shoreline and a major maritime port.

**[[Driftwood]] — Shipwright Town**

A smaller coastal settlement famous for its shipbuilders and fishing fleets.

Numerous fishing villages dot the coastline between these cities.

---

## Trade and Transportation

Transportation relies heavily on maritime routes.

Important connections include:

• coastal shipping lanes linking western settlements  
• trade roads connecting Westport to inland regions  
• maritime routes leading into the western ocean

Ships from Westport often travel far beyond Valecaryn’s traditional trade routes.

---

## Infrastructure

Important infrastructure includes:

• harbor docks and shipyards in Westport  
• coastal lighthouses guiding ships through dangerous waters  
• fishing ports along the coastline  
• rugged coastal roads connecting settlements

These systems support the region’s maritime economy.

---

# Economy

## Major Industries

The region’s economy centers on maritime activities.

Major industries include:

• fishing fleets  
• shipbuilding and ship repair  
• coastal trade  
• timber export from inland forests

Many ships built here are designed to withstand rough ocean conditions.

---

## Major Exports

Exports include:

• fish and preserved seafood  
• timber transported from inland forests  
• ships constructed in regional shipyards

These goods support trade with other coastal regions.

---

## Major Imports

Imports include:

• grain from the Gold Marches  
• wine from [[Allacroix Vineyards]] and [[Ambervale Downs]]
• manufactured goods from inland cities

Trade connections tie the region closely to the rest of Valecaryn.

---

# Society and Culture

## Cultural Identity

The people of the Western Coast are known for their independence and maritime skill.

Common cultural traits include:

• resilience in harsh conditions  
• strong seafaring traditions  
• pride in shipbuilding craftsmanship

Many families have long histories of sailing and fishing.

---

## Population Distribution

Population is concentrated in the ports of Westport and Driftwood.

Smaller fishing villages dot the coastline.

---

## Traditions and Festivals

Important traditions include:

• sea blessing ceremonies before major voyages  
• seasonal fishing festivals  
• ship launching celebrations in Driftwood

These traditions emphasize the region’s deep connection to the ocean.

---

# Government and Control

The Western Coast falls under the authority of the Valecaryni crown but maintains a strong local maritime culture.

Port authorities and merchant captains often wield significant influence in regional affairs.

---

# Military Importance

The region serves as Valecaryn’s western maritime frontier.

Strategic advantages include:

• deep natural harbors suitable for fleets  
• access to open ocean routes  
• rugged coastline difficult for invasion

The ports can also serve as staging points for naval patrols.
