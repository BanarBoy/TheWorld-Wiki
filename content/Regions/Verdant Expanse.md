# Overview

The Verdant Expanse is a vast and densely overgrown jungle region stretching across northeastern Thalassar, where life grows thick, fast, and often beyond control. Fed by branching river systems and sustained by constant humidity, the Expanse produces an abundance of valuable organic resources—many of which rival or complement those found in the Masari Archipelago.

Unlike the islands, however, the Verdant Expanse is anchored to the mainland, making it both more accessible and more contested. It represents opportunity not only for trade, but for expansion, settlement, and control. As a result, it has become a region of growing tension between local powers, frontier communities, and the outward reach of established authorities.

The cities of [[Irathel]] and [[Ilvara]] act as the primary footholds within this overwhelming environment. From these centers, trade routes push into the jungle, extracting spices, medicinal plants, rare woods, and other valuable materials from territory that is only partially understood—and never fully controlled.

To outsiders, the Verdant Expanse is a place of abundance.  
To those who enter it, it is a place where the land itself resists being claimed.

---

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Verdant Expanse lies northeast of the Zhar Basin and east of central Thalassar, extending toward the outer reaches of the continent.

It borders:

- the Zhar Basin to the southwest
- the Khaldren Spine Highlands to the south
- eastern wildlands and lesser-known territories beyond
- inland river systems that feed into larger trade routes

The region acts as a transitional zone between structured civilization and untamed wilderness.

---

## Terrain

The Expanse is dominated by:

- dense, multi-layered rainforest canopy
- thick undergrowth and tangled vegetation
- lowland basins and gently rolling jungle terrain
- hidden clearings and natural corridors

Visibility is limited, and movement through the jungle is slow and difficult without established paths.

---

## Hydrology

Water defines the region:

- numerous rivers and tributaries crisscross the jungle
- seasonal flooding creates temporary wetlands
- natural waterways serve as primary travel routes

Settlements form along rivers, which act as lifelines for transport and survival.

---

## Climate

Spring  
Hot and humid; rapid plant growth begins.

Summer  
Heavy rainfall and intense humidity; jungle becomes nearly impenetrable.

Autumn  
Slightly reduced rainfall; peak harvesting season.

Winter  
Warm with intermittent rains; minimal seasonal variation overall.

The climate supports constant biological growth.
