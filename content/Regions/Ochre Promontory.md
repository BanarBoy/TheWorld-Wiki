---

# Overview

The Ochre Promontory is a rugged and wind-swept region stretching from the inland waters of [[Lake Jevora]] to the coastal stronghold of [[Varkesh]], forming a natural bridge between Thalassar’s interior and its southwestern maritime domains. Defined by its distinctive red stone terrain and harsh climate, the Promontory is a place where no single power holds complete control—and where all seek influence.

Unlike the structured systems of the Zhar Basin or the centralized authority of Ashur-Taal, the Ochre Promontory is a region of shifting alliances, fortified settlements, and competing interests. Caravan routes cross its dry plateaus carrying goods between inland regions and coastal trade networks, while local powers carve out territory where they can.

The inland anchor of the region, Lake Jevora, provides a rare and vital freshwater source, supporting settlement and limited agriculture. From there, trade routes stretch westward toward Varkesh, a hardened coastal city that serves as both a trade outlet and a defensive stronghold.

To outsiders, the Ochre Promontory is unstable and dangerous.  
To those who operate within it, it is opportunity—unclaimed, contested, and constantly changing.

# Physical Geography

## Regional Position

The Ochre Promontory lies between central Thalassar and the southwestern coast, acting as a transitional region between inland systems and maritime trade zones.

It connects:

- the Glasswaste Basin to the west
- the Zhar Basin and Verdant Expanse to the east and northeast
- the Glasswater Reach to the southwest
- inland territories through Lake Jevora

Its position makes it a natural corridor for overland trade moving toward coastal export hubs.

---

## Terrain

The region is characterized by:

- elevated red-rock plateaus
- steep coastal cliffs
- dry valleys and erosion-carved basins
- scattered inland water bodies

The terrain is:

- harsh and exposed
- difficult to traverse outside established routes
- defensible when fortified

---

## Hydrology

Water is limited but critical:

- **Lake Jevora** serves as the primary freshwater anchor
- seasonal streams flow outward from the lake
- small reservoirs and wells support settlements

Control of water sources determines settlement viability.

---

## Climate

Spring  
Dry and windy; moderate temperatures.

Summer  
Hot, with intense sun exposure and minimal rainfall.

Autumn  
More stable conditions; peak caravan activity.

Winter  
Cooler but still dry; strong coastal winds.

The region experiences constant wind and low precipitation.
