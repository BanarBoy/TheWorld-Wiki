# House Korsyne

**Realm / Nation:** [[Valecaryn]]  
**House Type:** Noble / Industrial House  
**Seat of Power:** [[Stonebridge]] — The Foundry Court  
**Founding Era:** Late Crown Crisis (industrial consolidation period)  
**Sigil / Symbol:** A black anvil beneath a rising gold arc on a slate-gray field  
**Motto:** _“What is made, endures.”_

---

# Overview

House Korsyne is the engine of Valecaryn’s material power.

Where other houses move wealth, command armies, or shape perception, Korsyne transforms raw resources into the infrastructure that makes all of it possible. Steel, stone, engineered works, and large-scale construction all pass through their systems. They do not extract the world—they **shape it**.

The house rose during the final century of the Crown Crisis, when fractured warlords needed fortifications, roads, and weapons at scale. Korsyne unified scattered workshops, quarry guilds, and forge networks into a coordinated industrial machine. In doing so, they became indispensable to every faction—and survived the transition into Crown rule stronger than most.

Today, House Korsyne stands as a quiet pillar of the realm. They do not dominate court politics, but nearly every major structure, fleet, or army depends on their output.

---

# Historical Role

House Korsyne’s origins lie in the need for **scale**.

During the Crown Crisis, production was fragmented—small forges, isolated quarries, and inconsistent supply. Korsyne consolidated these into standardized networks, introducing:

• coordinated forge production across regions  
• large-scale quarry and stone transport systems  
• standardized fittings, tools, and construction methods  
• contract-based supply agreements with warlords and early Crown forces

They played a decisive role in:

• enabling prolonged campaigns through reliable arms production  
• constructing fortified positions that stabilized contested regions  
• rebuilding critical infrastructure after the fall of Aureth Caldras  
• formalizing industrial partnerships with [[Khordun-Zhal]]

Unlike many houses, Korsyne did not rise through conquest—but through **necessity**.

# Domains of Influence

• large-scale metallurgy and refined steel production  
• stone quarrying and structural construction  
• infrastructure development (roads, bridges, fortifications)  
• engineered components for ships, artillery, and tools  
• industrial standardization and production systems

They do not control raw extraction.

They control **what raw materials become**.

---

# Core Assets

• continent-spanning forge and workshop networks  
• long-term industrial contracts with major houses  
• standardized production methods across multiple regions  
• deep working partnerships with dwarven smiths of [[Khordun-Zhal]]  
• skilled labor forces trained in repeatable, scalable production

Their greatest asset is **consistency at scale**.

---

# Key Holdings

• **The Foundry Court ([[Stonebridge]])** — primary seat and administrative hub of industrial operations  
• **Blackspan Works** — major steelworks near the Blackspine foothills  
• **The Grey Yards** — large quarry and stone-processing complexes  
• **Bridgehold Stations** — distributed construction depots along major routes  
• **Korsyne Exchange Houses** — contract offices in [[Geldmere]] and [[Lethryn Prime]]

Korsyne holdings are functional, expansive, and built to operate continuously.

---

# Economy and Markets

House Korsyne generates wealth through:

• steel and refined metal goods  
• construction and infrastructure contracts  
• engineered components for military and maritime use  
• large-scale public works commissioned by the Crown  
• long-term supply agreements with other houses

Primary relationships:

• [[House Merrow]] — financing and contract structuring  
• [[House Voss]] — transport and logistical distribution  
• [[House Draeven]] — military equipment and fortification supply  
• [[House Brannor]] — tools, fittings, and equipment for frontier operations

Korsyne does not speculate.

They produce—and are paid to keep producing.

---

# Military Power

House Korsyne maintains limited direct military forces:

• 500–800 industrial guards protecting key facilities  
• fortified production sites capable of defensive holdout  
• contracted security from allied houses when required

Their indirect influence is far greater:

- they supply arms, armor, and fortifications
    
- they determine production capacity during wartime
    

They do not win wars.

They determine how long wars can be fought.

---

# Religious or Arcane Alignment

Minimal formal religious alignment.

However, Korsyne maintains:

• ritualized forge traditions (often influenced by dwarven practices)  
• craftsmanship rites tied to quality and durability  
• limited arcane enhancement in metallurgy and structural integrity

Their philosophy is practical:

> what matters is whether it holds.

---

# Political Position

House Korsyne is a stabilizing industrial power within Valecaryn.

They maintain strong working relationships with:

• the Crown (infrastructure and public works)  
• [[House Merrow]] (financial structuring and large contracts)  
• [[House Voss]] (distribution networks)  
• [[House Draeven]] (military supply)  
• [[Khordun-Zhal]] (technical expertise and metallurgy exchange)

They are rarely opposed directly, as disruption to Korsyne affects the entire system.

---

# Strategic Strengths

• control over large-scale industrial output  
• indispensable role in infrastructure and military supply  
• strong cross-house relationships  
• ability to operate continuously across political changes  
• deep technical knowledge and refinement processes

---

# Structural Weaknesses

• dependence on raw material supply (external sources)  
• limited political leverage in court environments  
• vulnerability to coordinated economic pressure (through Merrow)  
• heavy infrastructure makes rapid relocation impossible  
• exposure to disruption in transport networks
