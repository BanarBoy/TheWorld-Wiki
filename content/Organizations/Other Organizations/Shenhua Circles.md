# Shenhua Circles

**Type:** Other (Scholarly / Alchemical Network)  
**Headquarters:** [[Jinshan]] (central oversight), major presence in [[Minghu]] and [[Fenghai]]  
**Sphere of Influence:** National → Limited International  
**Founded:** Early Kiyoran consolidation period  
**Leader(s):** No single leader; senior authority held by the **High Refiners of Jinshan**  
**Affiliated Groups:** [[Zhilan Paper]] guilds, agricultural stewards, state bureaus of [[Jinshan]], licensed alchemical workshops  
**Known For:** Medicine, alchemical refinement, controlled substances, integration of natural and material knowledge

---

# Overview

The Shenhua Circles are a decentralized but highly respected network of practitioners dedicated to the study, refinement, and application of natural and alchemical materials. They operate throughout Kiyora, bridging agriculture, medicine, and controlled experimentation, and are responsible for many of the region’s advancements in healing and material science.

Rather than a single unified institution, the Circles function as an interconnected web of knowledge—linking rural herbalists, urban refiners, and state-sanctioned alchemists. Their influence is felt in everyday life through medicines and tonics, but also at the highest levels of governance through their involvement in regulated substances and strategic materials.

They matter because they transform Kiyora’s raw resources—[[Shencha]], Lotus Root, [[Hezhu Bamboo]], and even restricted materials like [[Shenjin Glass]]—into refined, usable, and often powerful forms. Without the Shenhua Circles, many of Kiyora’s defining advantages would remain unrealized.

In the modern era, they occupy a delicate position: essential to the state, but never fully controlled by it.

---

# Origins and Founding

The Shenhua tradition began as a collection of localized practices among early Kiyoran agricultural communities. Farmers, herbalists, and early scholars experimented with plants, roots, and minerals to improve health, preserve food, and stabilize production systems.

As irrigation networks expanded and crops like [[Lianmi]] became central to Kiyoran life, the need for more consistent and reliable knowledge grew. Informal practices began to consolidate into shared methods, recorded and distributed through early uses of paper.

The rise of [[Jinshan]] as a central authority marked a turning point. Recognizing the value of controlled knowledge, the state began licensing practitioners and encouraging standardization—particularly for medicines and volatile substances.

What emerged was not a single founding moment, but a gradual convergence:

• local tradition → shared methodology  
• shared methodology → recorded knowledge  
• recorded knowledge → regulated practice

---

# Structure and Hierarchy

The Shenhua Circles are loosely structured, with authority based on expertise rather than strict hierarchy.

Core tiers include:

• **Apprentices** — trainees learning foundational techniques  
• **Field Practitioners** — rural herbalists and local specialists  
• **Refiners** — skilled processors of compounds and materials  
• **Master Practitioners** — recognized experts with authority and influence  
• **High Refiners** — senior figures, often based in [[Jinshan]], who guide standards and complex work

There is no single ruler, but:

• [[Jinshan]] exerts influence through licensing and regulation  
• knowledge authority is respected across all regions

---

# Domains of Activity

The Shenhua Circles operate across several key domains:

• **Medicine** — tonics, treatments, and preventative care  
• **Alchemy** — refinement of plant and mineral compounds  
• **Agricultural Enhancement** — improving yields and stability  
• **Material Processing** — working with substances like [[Shenjin Glass]]  
• **Controlled Substances** — oversight of volatile materials (including limited interaction with [[Huoyan Powder]])

They function at the intersection of:

> science, craft, and controlled experimentation

---

# Resources and Assets

The power of the Shenhua Circles comes from:

• deep knowledge of natural and refined materials  
• access to Kiyora’s core resources (Shencha, Lotus Root, Hezhu)  
• networks of practitioners across all major regions  
• recorded knowledge preserved on [[Zhilan Paper]]  
• state recognition and partial protection

They do not rely on armies or wealth alone, but on:

> expertise that cannot easily be replicated

# Cultural Identity and Ideology

The Shenhua Circles believe:

> **All things can be refined.**

Their philosophy emphasizes:

• transformation over preservation  
• knowledge through application  
• balance achieved through adjustment

They reject both:

• uncontrolled experimentation  
• blind adherence to tradition

Instead, they pursue:

> disciplined, repeatable mastery
