# Ministry of Industrial Allocation

**Type:** Government Body / Economic Planning Authority  
**Headquarters:** [[Aureth Caldras]] — Ministry Complex (Allocation Hall)  
**Sphere of Influence:** National (strongest within Caldras-controlled systems)  
**Founded:** Post-Consolidation Era (~170 years ago)  
**Leader(s):** High Allocator of Industry  
**Affiliated Groups:** [[House Merrow]], [[Korsyne]] forge guilds, Foundry Courts, military procurement offices, Capital Trade Council  
**Known For:** Resource apportionment, industrial prioritization, strategic allocation of ore, steel, and production capacity

---

# Overview

The Ministry of Industrial Allocation is the central authority within [[Aureth Caldras]] responsible for determining how critical resources are distributed across Valecaryn’s industrial and military systems.

Where the Capital Trade Council aligns interests and the Foundry Courts ensure quality, the Ministry answers a more immediate question:

> **Who gets what—and when?**

It oversees the allocation of raw materials such as ore, refined metals like Blackspine Steel, and the industrial capacity required to turn those materials into weapons, infrastructure, or trade goods. Requests from cities, military commands, and merchant houses are evaluated, prioritized, and either fulfilled—or delayed.

The Ministry does not produce resources. It controls their **flow at the point of decision**.

> A forge may burn.  
> But only if the Ministry feeds it.

---

# Origins and Founding

The Ministry emerged during a period of increasing strain on Valecaryn’s industrial base, when demand for metal production began to exceed available supply.

This imbalance created:

• competition between military and commercial needs  
• inefficient distribution of ore and refined materials  
• political conflict over access to strategic resources

To stabilize the system, Caldras established a centralized authority to manage allocation decisions based on **priority, necessity, and strategic value**.

Initially a logistical office, the Ministry rapidly expanded as its role proved essential. Over time, it became a permanent institution with the authority to direct the flow of industrial resources across the continent.

---

# Structure and Hierarchy

The Ministry operates as a layered bureaucratic system with centralized oversight.

### Leadership

• **High Allocator of Industry** — ultimate authority over allocation policy and priority decisions  
• **Council of Ledgers** — senior officials responsible for major sectors (military, infrastructure, trade)

### Operational Structure

• **Allocation Officers** — review requests and assign resource quotas  
• **Procurement Analysts** — track supply levels and production capacity  
• **Liaison Officials** — coordinate with Korsyne, merchant houses, and military authorities  
• **Clerical Networks** — maintain records, contracts, and distribution schedules

Authority flows from data, but decisions are ultimately **discretionary at the highest levels**.

---

# Domains of Activity

The Ministry operates in the domain of industrial distribution:

• allocation of ore shipments from Blackspine routes  
• distribution of refined materials (including Blackspine Steel)  
• prioritization of forge output  
• coordination of large-scale production efforts  
• management of resource shortages

It also influences:

• military readiness  
• infrastructure development  
• economic balance between regions

---

# Resources and Assets

The Ministry’s power is derived from **control over allocation decisions**.

• centralized records of supply and demand  
• authority to approve or deny large-scale resource requests  
• integration with Caldras financial systems (via [[House Merrow]])  
• coordination with Korsyne production capacity

Its greatest asset:

> Scarcity exists.  
> The Ministry decides how it is felt.

---

# Methods and Operations

### Request and Allocation

• cities, guilds, and military bodies submit formal requests  
• requests are evaluated based on urgency, importance, and political weight  
• quotas are assigned and scheduled for delivery

### Priority Systems

• military and strategic infrastructure typically receive priority  
• trade-related requests are evaluated based on economic return  
• emergency reallocations can override existing plans

### Coordination

• works closely with Korsyne to align production output  
• interfaces with Voss systems to ensure transport  
• adjusts allocations based on changing conditions

### Enforcement (Indirect)

• denial of allocation halts production  
• delays can cripple projects or weaken military readiness  
• compliance is enforced through dependency on supply

# Geographic Reach

The Ministry’s authority extends across:

• [[Aureth Caldras]] and surrounding inland systems  
• Korsyne industrial networks  
• Blackspine resource flows  
• major military and infrastructure projects across Valecaryn

Its influence is strongest where Caldras financial authority is dominant.

**[[Korsyne]] / Foundry Courts**

**Military Authorities (e.g., [[Fort Asten]])**  
Primary recipients of prioritized allocation.

**Capital Trade Council**  
Coordinates broader economic policy that shapes allocation priorities.

**[[House Voss]]**  
Handles transport of allocated materials through established trade routes.

# Role in Major Events

• prevented industrial collapse during periods of resource scarcity  
• prioritized military production during major conflicts  
• managed allocation during the [[Crown Crisis]] to maintain stability  
• balanced competing demands between trade and warfare

# Reputation

**Nobility:**  
Seen as powerful and sometimes dangerous—controls access to critical resources.

**Merchants:**  
Frustrating but necessary; access often depends on influence.

**Common People:**  
Largely invisible, though its decisions shape availability of goods and employment.

**Foreign Powers:**  
Viewed as a sign of Caldras’ centralized control and efficiency.

---

# Cultural Identity and Ideology

The Ministry operates under a core principle:

> **Resources must serve the system, not the claimant.**

It values:

• efficiency  
• prioritization  
• systemic stability

It rejects:

• waste  
• uncoordinated demand  
• uncontrolled competition

Its culture is bureaucratic, analytical, and quietly political.
