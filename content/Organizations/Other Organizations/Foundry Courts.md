# Foundry Courts

**Type:** Industrial Authority / Regulatory Tribunal / Trade Consortium  
**Headquarters:** [[Korsyne]] — Foundry District (High Crucible Hall)  
**Sphere of Influence:** National / International (through trade contracts)  
**Founded:** Early Industrial Consolidation (~220 years ago)  
**Leader(s):** High Arbiter of the Courts (Korsyne-appointed)  
**Affiliated Groups:** Korsyne forge guilds, Blackspine suppliers, merchant houses, military contracts  
**Known For:** Material standards enforcement, ore grading, weapon certification, industrial arbitration

---

# Overview

The Foundry Courts are the central authority governing material quality and industrial standards across Valecaryn’s metallurgical economy. Based in the forge-city of [[Korsyne]], they exist to ensure that every ingot, weapon, and structural component entering major trade or military use meets **strict, verifiable standards**.

Originally formed to combat the widespread problem of inferior metals and fraudulent trade during the rise of industrial production, the Courts evolved into a system of **certification, arbitration, and enforcement**. Today, their mark determines whether material is trusted—or rejected.

Their authority extends far beyond Korsyne. Merchant houses, military forces, and infrastructure projects rely on Court-certified materials to ensure reliability and consistency. Without their approval, even the finest-looking weapon or shipment of ore is treated with suspicion.

> A blade may shine.  
> But without the Court’s mark, it is only metal.

---

# Origins and Founding

The Foundry Courts were established during a period of rapid industrial expansion, when increased demand for metal production led to a surge in:

• inconsistent alloy quality  
• fraudulent ore grading  
• catastrophic structural failures in weapons and construction

These issues threatened both military effectiveness and economic stability. In response, Korsyne’s forge guilds, backed by powerful industrial and merchant interests, created a centralized authority to **define and enforce standards**.

Initially limited to local oversight, the Courts quickly gained broader recognition as their certification became synonymous with reliability. Over time, they absorbed smaller guild authorities and established themselves as the **final arbiter of material legitimacy** across Valecaryn.

---

# Structure and Hierarchy

The Foundry Courts are organized as a hybrid of guild authority and judicial system.

### Leadership

• **High Arbiter** — supreme authority, responsible for maintaining the integrity of the system  
• **Council of Crucibles** — senior masters representing major forge traditions and industrial sectors

### Operational Structure

• **Court Examiners** — specialists who test and certify materials  
• **Forge Inspectors** — embedded within production centers to ensure compliance  
• **Adjudicators** — preside over disputes involving contracts, quality, and liability  
• **Registered Forges** — certified producers operating under Court oversight

Authority is rooted in expertise, but enforced through institutional backing.

# Domains of Activity

The Courts operate across several critical domains:

• ore grading and verification  
• alloy composition standards  
• weapon and armor certification  
• industrial material approval (beams, fittings, reinforcements)  
• arbitration of production and contract disputes

They also regulate:

• acceptable forging practices  
• quality thresholds for military supply  
• consistency across large-scale production

---

# Resources and Assets

The Foundry Courts derive power from control over **trust in materials**.

• exclusive certification authority for high-grade metals  
• centralized testing facilities and forge laboratories  
• records of production quality and forge performance  
• influence over military and merchant procurement  
• integration with Korsyne’s industrial infrastructure

Their greatest asset:

> If the Courts do not approve it, no one relies on it.

---

# Methods and Operations

### Certification

• materials undergo rigorous stress testing and composition analysis  
• certified goods receive a **Court Mark** indicating quality tier  
• substandard materials are rejected or downgraded

### Inspection

• forge inspectors monitor production processes  
• random audits ensure compliance  
• repeated violations result in sanctions or loss of certification

### Enforcement

• uncertified materials are excluded from major contracts  
• merchants risk liability if using unapproved goods  
• military procurement requires Court approval

### Arbitration

• disputes over faulty materials are resolved through formal hearings  
• liability is assigned based on certification records and production logs

While centered in [[Korsyne]], the Courts’ influence extends across:

• major industrial cities in Valecaryn

Their mark is recognized wherever Valecaryn metal is traded.

Require Court-certified materials for weapons and fortifications.

**[[House Voss]]**
