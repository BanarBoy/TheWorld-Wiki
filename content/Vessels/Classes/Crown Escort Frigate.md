# Crown Escort Frigate

**Type:** Military / Defensive Warship  
**Primary Use:** Convoy protection, sustained engagement, and maritime presence  
**Associated Regions:** [[Valecaryn]] (primary), extended operations along major trade corridors  
**Associated Routes / Currents:** [[Gilded Corridor]], [[Crown Current]], conditional deployment in [[Eastwind Passage]]  
**Typical Operators:** Valecaryn Royal Navy, Crown convoy command, joint operations with [[House Voss]]  
**Defining Trait:** A durable, heavily armed warship designed to maintain control over time, ensuring that once security is established, it **cannot be easily broken**

---

# Overview

The Crown Escort Frigate is the stabilizing force of Valecaryn’s navy—a warship built not for speed or pursuit, but for **presence and endurance**. Where Interceptors resolve engagements quickly, the Frigate exists to ensure those outcomes **hold**.

Developed alongside the rise of structured trade under King Halvair Drosen, the Escort Frigate reflects a shift from opportunistic piracy to sustained control. It is designed to accompany high-value convoys, protect critical vessels such as Voss Carracks, and maintain security across long stretches of ocean where threats may emerge gradually rather than suddenly.

Unlike smaller, faster ships, the Frigate does not rely on timing windows or fleeting advantages. It establishes a zone of control and **maintains it**, forcing adversaries to either commit fully or disengage entirely.

> If the Interceptor wins the moment, the Frigate secures the outcome.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Crown Escort Frigate is built on a single principle:

> **control must be sustained, not assumed**

This results in:

• emphasis on durability and combat endurance  
• balance between offensive and defensive capability  
• design for prolonged engagement rather than rapid resolution  
• integration into larger operational systems (convoys, patrol lines)

It is not built to chase.

It is built to:

> **remain, resist, and reinforce**

---

# Structural Characteristics

• Broad, reinforced hull capable of absorbing sustained damage  
• Multi-deck structure with dedicated combat and command levels  
• Reinforced sides and critical sections for defensive resilience  
• Balanced sail configuration allowing steady, controlled movement  
• Expanded deck space for weapon systems and boarding defense

The ship is built to:

> **withstand pressure and continue operating**

# Relationship to Currents

The Crown Escort Frigate is designed for **stability within structured movement**.

• uses the [[Crown Current]] for consistent deployment and coordination  
• operates heavily along the [[Gilded Corridor]]  
• can traverse the [[Eastwind Current]] under convoy conditions  
• avoids reliance on high-volatility current exploitation

In Eastwind operations:

• maintains formation integrity rather than chasing flow  
• protects slower vessels from disruption  
• acts as an anchor within unstable conditions

Unlike faster ships:

> **it resists the current rather than riding it**
