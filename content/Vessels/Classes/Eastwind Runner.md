# Eastwind Runner

**Type:** Merchant / Corsair Hybrid  
**Primary Use:** High-risk, high-speed transit along the Eastwind Passage  
**Associated Regions:** Eastern [[Valecaryn]] coast, Sapphire Reach waters  
**Associated Routes / Currents:** [[Eastwind Passage]], [[Eastwind Current]]  
**Typical Operators:** Independent merchants, corsairs, private contractors, opportunistic captains  
**Defining Trait:** A fast, maneuverable vessel built to exploit the Eastwind Current, thriving in contested waters where **speed and decision-making outweigh structure**

---

# Overview

The Eastwind Runner is the dominant vessel class of the Eastwind Passage, designed not for stability or scale, but for survival and success in one of the most dangerous maritime environments in the world.

Unlike the Sandrunner—whose power comes from system and origin—the Eastwind Runner is defined by **adaptation**. It operates in waters where control is fragmented, threats are constant, and no single authority can guarantee safety. In this environment, ships must rely on speed, timing, and the judgment of their captain.

These vessels are typically smaller and more agile than long-haul trade ships, allowing them to enter and exit the Eastwind Current with precision. They are capable of riding the current for rapid southbound movement, but—critically—are also designed to disengage when necessary. This flexibility is what separates them from more specialized designs.

To sail an Eastwind Runner is to accept uncertainty:

> **you are not guaranteed arrival—only opportunity**

---

# Design Philosophy

The Eastwind Runner is built around a different principle than most vessels:

> **maximize survivability within chaos**

This results in:

• emphasis on maneuverability over raw speed  
• balance between structure and flexibility  
• design choices that favor recovery from mistakes  
• acceptance of risk as part of operation

It is not optimized for perfect conditions—it is optimized for **unpredictable ones**

---

# Structural Characteristics

• Sleek, narrow hull for speed and responsiveness  
• Reinforced lateral structure to handle sudden directional stress  
• Flexible rigging systems allowing rapid sail adjustment  
• Moderate cargo capacity—favoring value over volume  
• Minimal excess weight to maintain agility

The ship is built to:

> **enter, ride, and escape the current**

---

# Performance Profile

• Fast when aligned with the Eastwind Current  
• Highly maneuverable compared to most trade vessels  
• Capable of rapid course correction  
• Performs well in unstable or contested environments  
• Less efficient in long-haul, stable routes

At peak use:

> **it trades efficiency for control**

---

# Operational Role

Eastwind Runners are used for:

• high-value, time-sensitive trade  
• contract-based transport through dangerous waters  
• corsair activity and interception  
• opportunistic runs exploiting gaps in protection

They operate:

• independently or in small groups  
• rarely as part of large convoys  
• often based on timing windows rather than fixed schedules

---

# Strengths

• high maneuverability  
• strong performance in contested waters  
• ability to disengage from currents when needed  
• adaptable to changing conditions  
• effective for both trade and conflict

---

# Limitations

• limited cargo capacity  
• vulnerable if caught in sustained engagement  
• less efficient on stable, long-distance routes  
• dependent on captain skill more than structure

> A poor captain makes an Eastwind Runner useless

---

# Relationship to Currents

The Eastwind Runner is designed specifically for the Eastwind Current—but not bound to it.

• can enter and ride the current for speed  
• can exit earlier than most vessels  
• can operate along the edges rather than the center  
• performs best when shifting between current and free navigation

Unlike the Sandrunner:

> **it uses the current—it is not defined by it**

---

# Crew and Operation

• smaller, highly skilled crews

• emphasis on awareness, timing, and adaptability

# Economic Role

• facilitates high-risk, high-reward trade

• extreme maneuverability

• reinforced hull and weaponry  
• used for escort or interception  
• common among private security groups
