# Seluqar Glassmarks

**Currency Name:** Seluqar Glassmarks  
**Type:** Commodity / Crafted Token (Glass-Based)  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Thalassar]] — primarily [[Seluqar]] and surrounding coastal trade cities  
**Issuing Authority:** Master Glasswright Guilds of [[Seluqar]] (loosely regulated)  
**Backing System:** Craftsmanship / Material Quality / Origin Authenticity  
**Denominations:** Shards, Marks, High Marks (based on size, clarity, and craftsmanship)  
**Relative Value:** Variable → High-Value (exceptional pieces)  
**Convertibility:** Exchangeable in Thalassari ports; often converted into Valecaryni Crowns for international trade

---

# Overview

Seluqar Glassmarks are a unique form of currency crafted from refined crystal glass produced by the artisans of [[Seluqar]]. Unlike traditional coinage or paper systems, Glassmarks derive their value from the quality of their material, the skill of their creation, and the reputation of their origin.

Each piece is individually formed, often bearing subtle variations in clarity, color, and internal structure. While standardized shapes and sizes exist, no two Glassmarks are perfectly identical. This gives them a dual identity: they function as currency within certain regions, but also as crafted objects of inherent aesthetic and material value.

Within Thalassar, Glassmarks are widely accepted in coastal trade networks, particularly in cities that maintain strong ties to Seluqar. However, their acceptance is not universal. In some regions, they are treated as trade goods rather than currency, valued based on negotiation rather than fixed denomination.

To outsiders, Glassmarks are intriguing but unreliable. To those within Seluqar’s sphere of influence, they represent a different philosophy of value—one rooted in craftsmanship, origin, and the visible quality of the object itself.

# Form and Design

Seluqar Glassmarks are:

• crafted from refined crystal glass  
• shaped into discs, shards, or geometric forms  
• polished to varying degrees of clarity

Design features include:

• internal refractive patterns unique to each piece  
• subtle coloration (clear, pale blue, amber, or green tones)  
• etched or fused maker’s marks from glasswright guilds

Higher-value Glassmarks may include:

• near-perfect clarity  
• complex internal light refraction  
• identifiable signatures from renowned artisans

Unlike minted coins:

• they are not perfectly uniform  
• slight variation is expected—and valued

---

# Issuance and Control

Glassmarks are not centrally issued in the same way as coins or notes.

• produced by master glasswright guilds in [[Seluqar]]  
• distributed through trade, commission, and artisan networks  
• loosely regulated through guild reputation rather than state control

Currency enters circulation through:

• direct trade of crafted Glassmarks  
• payment for goods and services within Seluqar networks  
• export through maritime trade routes

Control mechanisms include:

• guild authentication marks  
• reputation of producing workshops  
• informal verification by experienced traders

---

# Circulation and Use

Seluqar Glassmarks are used for:

• trade within Seluqar and nearby coastal cities  
• artisan transactions and luxury markets  
• medium-value exchanges where trust in origin exists

They are used by:

• artisans and craftsmen  
• coastal merchants  
• regional traders

They are accepted:

• widely in [[Seluqar]]  
• variably across [[Thalassar]]  
• selectively in foreign ports

In many cases:

• value is negotiated rather than fixed

---

# Economic Role

Glassmarks function as a **hybrid between currency and trade good**.

They:

• facilitate trade within artisan and coastal economies  
• represent portable crafted wealth  
• bridge the gap between commodity and currency

They are:

> not a universal standard, but a flexible medium of exchange

---

# Relationship with Other Currencies

Seluqar Glassmarks interact closely with other systems.

• often exchanged for Valecaryni Crowns in major trade hubs  
• rarely accepted in Kiyora without conversion

Trade dynamics:

• Glassmarks → Crowns for international trade  
• Crowns → Glassmarks within Seluqar networks

Compared to other currencies:

• less stable than Crowns  
• less scalable than Zhilan Notes  
• more culturally distinct than both

---

# Backing and Trust System

Glassmarks are backed by:

• the quality of the crystal material  
• the skill of the artisan  
• the reputation of Seluqar craftsmanship

Trust is maintained through:

• recognizable maker’s marks  
• experience of traders  
• regional familiarity with the system

If trust fails:

• Glassmarks revert to value as crafted goods  
• their worth becomes subjective and negotiated

---

# Regulation and Enforcement

Formal enforcement is limited.

Instead, control relies on:

• guild reputation systems  
• artisan verification  
• market experience

Anti-fraud measures include:

• maker’s marks embedded in the glass  
• identifiable crafting techniques  
• visual inspection by trained traders

Penalties for fraud:

• loss of guild standing  
• exclusion from trade networks  
• potential local punishment

# Cultural Significance

Seluqar Glassmarks represent:

• craftsmanship as value  
• beauty intertwined with utility  
• identity through creation

They are seen as:

> wealth shaped by human hands, not mined or recorded

Within Seluqar, possessing high-quality Glassmarks reflects:

• taste  
• connection to artisan networks  
• appreciation of refinement
