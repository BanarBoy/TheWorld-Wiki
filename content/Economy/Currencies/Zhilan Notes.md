# Zhilan Notes

**Currency Name:** Zhilan Notes  
**Type:** Paper / Credit  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Kiyora]]  
**Issuing Authority:** Imperial Ledger Bureau / State of [[Jinshan]]  
**Backing System:** State Authority / Ledger-Based Credit System  
**Denominations:** Standardized note tiers (low, mid, high value), trade notes, high-value ledger credits  
**Relative Value:** Stable → High-Value  
**Convertibility:** Convertible with Valecaryni coinage at regulated exchange points; internally dominant

---

# Overview

Zhilan Notes are the primary currency of Kiyora, representing a sophisticated paper-based financial system built on state authority and precise record-keeping. Produced using refined [[Zhilan Paper]], these notes function not as intrinsic stores of value, but as certified representations of value recognized and enforced by the Kiyoran state.

Unlike metal coinage such as Valecaryni crowns, Zhilan Notes derive their worth from trust—specifically, trust in the accuracy of records maintained by the Imperial Ledger Bureau and the stability of the institutions behind them. Each note corresponds to a recorded value within a broader system of taxation, storage, and allocation.

This system allows Kiyora to operate a flexible and scalable economy. Wealth can be transferred without moving physical metal, large transactions can be conducted efficiently, and credit can be extended through administrative systems rather than physical reserves.

To outsiders, Zhilan Notes may appear abstract or risky. Within Kiyora, however, they are the foundation of economic life—proof that value can be managed, recorded, and controlled.

Zhilan Notes are:

• printed on high-grade [[Zhilan Paper]]  
• marked with official seals of [[Jinshan]]  
• encoded with denomination indicators and issuing marks

Design features include:

• layered ink patterns to deter forgery  
• official stamps and calligraphic identifiers  
• durable paper treated for longevity

Higher-value notes may include:

• additional markings  
• more complex designs  
• restricted issuance identifiers

---

# Issuance and Control

Zhilan Notes are issued through a centralized and tightly controlled process.

• printed in authorized facilities in [[Minghu]] (Minghe basin)  
• issued under authority of [[Jinshan]] (Jinghe basin)  
• recorded and tracked by the Imperial Ledger Bureau

Currency enters circulation through:

• state expenditures  
• payment for goods and services  
• distribution through trade and taxation systems

Control mechanisms include:

• strict tracking of issued notes  
• reconciliation with ledger records  
• audits and inspections

# Circulation and Use

Zhilan Notes are used for:

• daily market transactions  
• taxation and state obligations  
• merchant trade and contracts  
• large-scale financial exchanges

They are used by:

• common citizens (low denominations)  
• merchants and traders (mid-tier notes)  
• state and elite actors (high-value notes and ledger credit)

They are accepted:

• throughout [[Kiyora]]  
• in all major cities and trade centers  
• selectively by foreign merchants operating within Kiyora

# Economic Role

Zhilan Notes are the **dominant economic medium** within Kiyora.

They:

• enable large-scale trade without physical coin transport  
• support centralized taxation and redistribution  
• allow credit-based transactions and deferred payment  
• unify multiple regional economies under one system

They function as:

> both currency and infrastructure

---

# Relationship with Other Currencies

Zhilan Notes coexist with foreign currencies, particularly Valecaryni crowns.

• foreign coin is accepted within Kiyora  
• however, it is often converted into Zhilan Notes for ease of use

Exchange practices:

• conducted in major trade hubs such as [[Laoshan]]  
• regulated to maintain stability  
• influenced by state policy

Outside Kiyora:

• Zhilan Notes have limited acceptance  
• must often be converted back into metal currency

# Backing and Trust System

Zhilan Notes are backed by:

• state authority of [[Jinshan]]  
• recorded value within the Imperial Ledger system  
• taxation and controlled economic output

Their stability depends on:

• accuracy of records  
• strength of governance  
• enforcement of legitimacy

If trust fails:

• notes lose value rapidly  
• trade systems destabilize  
• regional economies may fracture
