# Overview

Ceyvar is a hardy, starchy root crop that forms the dietary foundation of Thalassar. It thrives in humid, flood-prone environments where other staple crops fail, making it the most reliable large-scale food source across the region’s river basins and coastal lowlands.

While ceyvar requires processing to remove naturally occurring toxins, its exceptional yield and resilience make it indispensable. Once properly prepared, it becomes a stable, calorie-dense food that can be stored and transported over long distances. This durability has elevated ceyvar beyond a subsistence crop into a cornerstone of trade and maritime logistics.

Over time, the labor of processing ceyvar has shifted from household necessity to organized industry. Large-scale processing yards now handle the bulk of preparation, producing dried ceyvar, flour, and ration blocks for distribution across Thalassar and beyond.

> Ceyvar is not chosen because it is easy.  
> It is relied upon because it does not fail.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Ceyvar grows primarily in:

• the floodplains of the [[Zhar River]]  
• coastal delta regions  
• humid inland basins with soft, nutrient-rich soils

These environments provide:

• consistent moisture  
• fertile sediment deposits from seasonal flooding  
• warm conditions for rapid growth

Ceyvar’s deep, stabilizing root system allows it to survive unstable soils and fluctuating water levels, making it uniquely suited to Thalassar’s environment.

---

# Production

Ceyvar is cultivated at large scale across river-fed agricultural zones.

Production methods include:

• planting cut root segments rather than seeds  
• staggered planting cycles for continuous yield  
• coordinated irrigation and floodplain management

The crop is highly productive, with reliable yields even under poor conditions. This consistency allows Thalassar to sustain dense populations and maintain steady food output despite environmental variability.

---

# Processing and Transport

Raw ceyvar is toxic and must be processed before consumption.

Processing typically involves:

• peeling and cutting the root  
• soaking or leaching in water to remove toxins  
• pressing and drying to create a stable product

While this process was historically performed at the household level, it is now largely centralized.

Major processing occurs in:

• river-adjacent processing yards  
• controlled drying facilities  
• trade-aligned production centers

These operations produce:

• ceyvar flour  
• dried ration cakes  
• preserved strips for transport

Transport is conducted through:

• river barges along the [[Zhar River]]  
• caravan routes linking inland farms to ports  
• maritime trade networks operated by Seluqar interests

Dried ceyvar is a standard provisioning good for ships, making it essential to long-distance trade.

# Economic Importance

Ceyvar is one of the most critical resources in Thalassar.

It:

• sustains the majority of the population  
• supports urban labor and industrial activity  
• enables large-scale maritime trade through reliable provisioning

Its role extends beyond subsistence:

• fleets depend on it for long voyages  
• trade networks rely on its stability  
• cities depend on its consistent supply

Without ceyvar, Thalassar’s economic and logistical systems would rapidly destabilize.

# Controlling Powers

Control of ceyvar is distributed but strategically significant.

Key actors include:

• river authorities controlling fertile floodplains  
• agrarian collectives managing cultivation  
• licensed processing yards controlling refinement  
• Seluqar trade networks managing distribution

Power lies less in growing ceyvar and more in **processing and movement**, where value is concentrated.

# Cultural Significance

Ceyvar is deeply embedded in Thalassari life.

It represents:

• resilience in harsh environments  
• shared knowledge and survival practices  
• the foundation of daily sustenance

While large-scale processing has industrialized production, knowledge of proper preparation remains culturally significant.

Improperly prepared ceyvar is associated with inexperience or neglect, while properly made ceyvar reflects competence and care.
