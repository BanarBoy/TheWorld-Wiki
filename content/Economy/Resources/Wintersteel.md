# Overview

Wintersteel is the finest forged metal in Eryndor, produced deep beneath the [[Crown of Winter]] in the vast forge complexes of [[Khaz Drunir]]. Renowned for its strength, consistency, and resistance to stress and fracture, it represents the pinnacle of dwarven metallurgical craft.

Unlike mass-produced steels or even high-quality foreign alloys, Wintersteel is defined not only by its materials but by its process. Forged under tightly controlled conditions—cold-stable environments, precise heating cycles, and disciplined refinement—it achieves a grain structure that is both dense and resilient.

Weapons made from Wintersteel hold their edge longer, resist warping, and perform reliably under extreme conditions. Armor and structural components forged from it are prized for their durability and longevity.

It is not merely stronger.

> It is **more certain**.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Wintersteel production is confined almost entirely to:

• the deep subterranean regions beneath the [[Crown of Winter]]  
• forge networks centered on [[Khaz Drunir]]  
• adjacent dwarven hold complexes connected through underground infrastructure

These regions provide:

• stable, cold ambient temperatures  
• access to high-quality ore sources (notably Fangiron)  
• controlled forge environments insulated from surface variability

The depth and isolation of these forges allow:

• precise thermal regulation  
• long, uninterrupted forging cycles  
• consistent output quality

True Wintersteel cannot be produced outside these conditions.

# Production

Wintersteel production is a highly controlled, multi-stage process requiring both specialized materials and expert knowledge.

---

### Raw Materials

• **Fangiron ore** — high-quality iron with strong carbon affinity  
• refined carbon sources  
• flux materials (including high-grade mineral fluxes such as Spineflint equivalents)

---

### Forging Process

• ore is carefully smelted to remove impurities  
• carbon content is precisely controlled during refinement  
• metal is repeatedly heated, folded, and worked

Key characteristics of the process:

• slow, deliberate forging cycles  
• strict temperature control  
• minimal tolerance for variation

The cold ambient environment of the deep forges allows smiths to:

• regulate cooling rates precisely  
• prevent structural weaknesses  
• achieve uniform grain density

Production is:

• slow  
• resource-intensive  
• limited in scale

# Economic Importance

Wintersteel is one of the most strategically important exports of Eryndor.

It:

• equips elite military forces  
• supports high-end craftsmanship industries  
• generates significant trade value

Its production is intentionally constrained, ensuring:

• high demand  
• controlled distribution  
• sustained prestige

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Wintersteel is tightly held.

Primary actors include:

• dwarven forge guilds of [[Khaz Drunir]]  
• master smith lineages  
• governing authorities within the deep holds

Access is restricted through:

• control of forge infrastructure  
• knowledge of forging techniques  
• access to high-quality raw materials

Unlike many other resources:

> Wintersteel is not widely shared

---

# Trade Networks

Wintersteel is exported selectively to:

• Eryndor’s military forces  
• Valecaryn elites and noble houses  
• select foreign powers through controlled trade

It is especially valued for:

• weapons and armor  
• engineering components  
• prestige craftsmanship

Most trade occurs through:

• negotiated contracts  
• long-standing alliances
