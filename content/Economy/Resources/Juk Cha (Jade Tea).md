# Overview

Juk cha is the primary tea cultivated throughout Kiyora, forming a central pillar of daily life, cultural practice, and refined trade. Grown on carefully managed hillsides and terraces, it reflects the same principles that define Kiyoran agriculture: precision, balance, and controlled cultivation.

Unlike staple crops such as [[Lianmi]], which sustain the body, Juk cha occupies a broader role. It is consumed daily across all classes, used medicinally within apothecary traditions, and processed into durable forms for long-distance trade. Its versatility allows it to exist simultaneously as a common good and a refined commodity.

The character of juk cha is shaped heavily by its processing. While all forms originate from the same plant, variations in treatment—drying, fermenting, compressing—produce distinct types with different uses and values. This has led to a tiered system of tea culture within Kiyora, where preparation and purpose define status as much as origin.

As both a cultural and economic resource, juk cha represents Kiyora’s identity outwardly: not abundance or force, but refinement, discipline, and mastery of process.

# Production

Juk cha production is divided into cultivation and processing.

---

### Cultivation

• tea plants are grown in maintained rows along terraces  
• leaves are harvested multiple times per season  
• quality depends on timing, leaf selection, and care

---

### Harvesting

• younger leaves produce higher-quality juk cha  
• harvesting is done by hand for precision  
• lower-grade leaves are used for bulk production

---

### Processing

The defining stage of juk cha production:

• leaves are dried, heated, or fermented  
• different methods produce distinct tea types  
• skill in processing determines final value

Production is:

• scalable but skill-dependent  
• heavily influenced by processing expertise

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Juk cha is processed into three primary forms:

---

## **Common Juk Cha (Everyday Tea)**

• lightly processed and dried  
• consumed daily across all social classes  
• widely available and produced in large quantities

---

## **Medicinal Juk Cha**

• blended or prepared with herbs and compounds  
• used in apothecary and healing practices  
• distributed through controlled medicinal networks

---

## **Pressed Juk Cha (Aged / Compressed Tea)**

• compacted into bricks or cakes  
• aged over time to develop depth and complexity  
• highly valued for trade and long-distance transport

---

### Transport

Juk cha moves through:

• overland routes from [[Tianzhou]] to [[Fenghai]] within the **Fenghe basin**  
• distribution networks centered in [[Laoshan]] (Laohe basin)  
• export channels through coastal trade systems linked to the **Fenghe corridor**

Pressed juk cha is:

• the primary export form  
• durable and easily transported  
• widely traded beyond Kiyora
