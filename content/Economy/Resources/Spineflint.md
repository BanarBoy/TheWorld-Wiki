# Overview

Spineflint is a pale, dense metamorphic stone found within the jagged ridgelines of the [[Khaldren Spine]]. Though visually unremarkable, it is one of the most strategically important industrial resources in the known world due to its role as a **high-efficiency smelting flux**.

When introduced during smelting, Spineflint binds aggressively with impurities in raw ore, drawing them into slag and leaving behind a cleaner, more stable metal bloom. This function is essential for high-grade steel production, but becomes absolutely critical in the forging of **Deepsteel**, where even minor contamination compromises structural integrity.

What sets Spineflint apart is not just its composition, but its consistency. Deposits from the Khaldren Spine produce **predictable, repeatable results**, allowing smiths to refine metal beyond what is possible with common flux materials.

> Ore gives strength.  
> Spineflint decides its purity.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Spineflint occurs exclusively within the [[Khaldren Spine]], particularly in exposed ridge bands where ancient geological pressure has compressed mineral layers into dense, uniform stone.

Major deposits are concentrated in:

• high ridge faces above [[Talresh Point]]  
• eastern escarpments feeding into the [[Kelresh River]] headwaters  
• fractured southern slopes overlooking [[Dreshkul]]

These zones share:

• extreme pressure-formed metamorphic layers  
• high calcium density with trace stabilizing elements  
• low impurity variance across deposits

Unlike most flux materials, Spineflint from different sections of the Spine remains **remarkably consistent**, making it uniquely suited for precision metallurgy.

---

# Production

Spineflint extraction is dangerous, slow, and highly controlled.

Quarry operations are carved directly into:

• vertical cliff faces  
• narrow ridge shelves  
• unstable fracture zones

Extraction involves:

• cutting stone blocks from exposed strata  
• breaking and grading material on-site  
• discarding unstable or inconsistent sections immediately

Operations are limited by:

• sheer terrain and fall risk  
• narrow access routes along the Spine  
• seasonal weather shifts

Only a fraction of extracted stone meets the standard required for advanced forging, particularly for Deepsteel.

---

# Processing and Transport

Spineflint is processed close to extraction sites to reduce transport weight and ensure quality control.

Processing includes:

• breaking into standardized flux fragments  
• grading by density, color, and fracture pattern  
• pre-conditioning (drying or firing) for stability in smelting

---

### Transport Routes

Movement from the Khaldren Spine follows tightly controlled paths:

• southern descent routes into [[Dreshkul]]  
• eastern routes feeding into [[Kelresh River]] transport systems  
• overland caravan paths linking to Seluqar trade networks

High-grade Spineflint is:

• pre-contracted before extraction  
• tracked through its entire journey  
• often escorted due to its value

---

# Economic Importance

Spineflint is not a resource that creates value directly—it **enables all higher-value metallurgy**.

It is essential for:

• producing consistent high-quality steel  
• removing impurities at scale  
• enabling the creation of Deepsteel

Without Spineflint:

• metal quality becomes inconsistent  
• high-end forging becomes unreliable  
• Deepsteel production becomes effectively impossible

This makes it a **choke point resource** in the global materials economy.

# Controlling Powers

Control of Spineflint is geographically local but economically leveraged outward.

Primary control lies with:

• Khaldren ridge clans controlling quarry territory  
• quarry guilds managing extraction standards  
• Seluqar brokers controlling contracts and distribution

While Thalassar owns the source, its power comes from:

> who it chooses to supply

# Trade Networks

Spineflint moves outward from the Khaldren Spine into broader trade systems via:

• overland routes into Thalassar’s interior  
• river-linked systems via the [[Kelresh River]]  
• maritime export through Seluqar-controlled ports

It is rarely traded on open markets.

Instead:

• contracts are negotiated in advance  
• shipments are allocated to specific buyers  
• supply is often intentionally limited
