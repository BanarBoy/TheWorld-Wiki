# Ironstep Courser

**Resource Type:** Livestock  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Eastwatch]] and the surrounding eastern highlands of Valecaryn  
**Major Production Centers:** [[Eastwatch]]  
**Controlling Groups:** [[Eastwatch Cavalry Houses]] / [[Ironstep Breeders’ Guild]]  
**Primary Uses:** War mounts, courier service, prestige riding animals  
**Trade Value:** Strategic

---

# Overview

The Ironstep Courser is a powerful, high-endurance breed of warhorse native to the windswept highlands surrounding [[Eastwatch]]. Bred for strength, stamina, and sure-footedness over broken terrain, these horses are instantly recognizable by their dense musculature, iron-dark hooves, and steady, deliberate gait from which their name is derived.

Unlike lighter cavalry breeds favored in coastal or lowland regions, Ironstep Coursers are built for resilience rather than speed. They excel in long-distance marches, steep inclines, and rough ground where other mounts falter. This makes them indispensable to eastern Valecaryn’s military forces and highly sought after by commanders who value reliability over flash.

The breed’s value extends beyond warfare. Their endurance and temperament make them ideal for long-haul courier networks, particularly across inland trade routes where terrain and weather can be unpredictable. As a result, control over Ironstep breeding has become both an economic and strategic priority, tightly managed by Eastwatch’s ruling powers.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Ironstep Coursers are almost exclusively bred in the highland belts east and northeast of [[Eastwatch]], where rocky soil, sparse grasslands, and sharp elevation changes create the conditions necessary for developing their signature traits.

The region’s harsh winds, mineral-rich ground, and uneven terrain naturally select for stronger hooves, thicker bones, and greater lung capacity. Attempts to breed Ironsteps outside this environment—particularly in the softer river valleys of central Valecaryn—have consistently resulted in weaker stock lacking the defining endurance of the original line.

Small satellite breeding efforts exist in nearby frontier settlements, but true Ironstep lineage is still closely associated with Eastwatch itself.

---

# Production

Ironstep breeding is a slow and deliberate process, emphasizing lineage purity and physical conditioning over volume. Foals are raised in open highland pastures, where they are exposed early to the terrain that will shape their strength and balance.

Training begins young. Rather than stabling, young Coursers are driven across long distances and steep gradients to build endurance naturally. Breeders cull aggressively, selecting only those that demonstrate exceptional stamina, calm temperament, and structural soundness.

Production remains relatively limited by design. The [[Ironstep Breeders’ Guild]] enforces strict breeding quotas to maintain quality and prevent dilution of the bloodline. As a result, Ironstep Coursers are never common, even within Valecaryn.

---

# Processing and Transport

Unlike most resources, Ironstep Coursers require minimal “processing,” but significant preparation before sale. Horses are trained, conditioned, and often partially broken to saddle before being deemed market-ready.

Transport is conducted primarily overland:

- escorted caravans for bulk movement
    
- individual delivery riders for high-value sales
    
- military requisition routes during wartime
    

River transport is rare due to the stress it places on the animals, though it is occasionally used for long-distance export under controlled conditions.

Because of their value, shipments are heavily guarded, and routes between [[Eastwatch]] and major inland cities are well-established and patrolled.

---

# Economic Importance

Ironstep Coursers are a cornerstone of Eastwatch’s regional influence.

- Military forces across Valecaryn depend on them for heavy and sustained cavalry operations
    
- Courier networks rely on them for reliable long-distance communication
    
- Noble houses prize them as symbols of strength and prestige
    

While not a mass-market commodity, their strategic importance far outweighs their volume. A single Ironstep Courser can be worth several times that of a standard riding horse, placing them firmly in the upper tier of military and noble assets.

Cities with strong military traditions or frontier responsibilities are the primary consumers.

During the later centuries of the Crown Crisis, Ironstep Coursers became one of the most contested military resources in eastern Valecaryn. Cavalry superiority often determined the outcome of prolonged inland campaigns, and access to Eastwatch’s breeding stock gave certain factions a decisive advantage in mobility and endurance.

House Draeven, bound by oath to uphold the crown, maintained especially strong ties to Eastwatch and relied heavily on Ironstep-mounted forces during their campaigns against the rebel houses.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of the Ironstep Courser trade is tightly held.

- The [[Ironstep Breeders’ Guild]] regulates breeding standards, lineage records, and sale approvals
    
- The [[Eastwatch Cavalry Houses]] maintain the finest bloodlines and often reserve the best stock for their own forces
    
- Eastwatch’s governing authority oversees export permissions, particularly during times of conflict
    

This layered control ensures that no single external power can easily gain access to large numbers of Ironstep mounts without negotiation.

During the Crown Crisis, the [[Ironstep Breeders’ Guild]] operated under constant pressure from competing factions. While officially neutral, in practice the guild favored long-standing agreements with crown-aligned forces, particularly those of House Draeven and their allies among [[House Mailleux]] and [[House Allacroix]].

Several attempts were made by Houses Corvane and Thale to seize or replicate Ironstep breeding operations, though none succeeded in producing stock of comparable quality.

---

# Trade Networks

Ironstep Coursers are exported selectively rather than widely.

Primary destinations include:

- inland military cities requiring durable cavalry mounts
    
- frontier settlements needing reliable transport
    
- noble courts seeking prestige animals
    

Trade routes extend westward from [[Eastwatch]] into central Valecaryn, often following established overland corridors rather than river systems.

Exports beyond Valecaryn are rare and typically restricted, though occasional high-value trades reach foreign powers through carefully negotiated agreements.

---

# Cultural Significance

In Eastwatch, the Ironstep Courser is more than a resource—it is a symbol of endurance and discipline.

Ownership of a true Ironstep is often seen as a mark of status and capability. Among cavalry units, riders are judged as much by their mount as by their skill. Certain bloodlines are even associated with historic battles and renowned commanders.

Festivals and trials are occasionally held to test young stock, where breeders, soldiers, and nobles gather to evaluate new generations. These events reinforce both the cultural and practical importance of the breed.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

Several factors limit the expansion of Ironstep Courser production:

- strict breeding controls restrict supply
    
- environmental dependency makes relocation difficult
    
- high cost limits accessibility
    

Additionally:

- caravans transporting Ironsteps are prime targets for bandits
    
- attempts to counterfeit lineage or pass inferior stock as true Ironsteps are an ongoing issue
    
- overreliance on the breed could create vulnerabilities if supply is disrupted
