# Greyrock

![[Pasted image 20260323203424.png]]

**Resource Type:** Mineral (Construction Stone)  
**Primary Region(s):** Across Eryndor — highland ridges, valley quarries, and mountain foothills, particularly along the [[Crown of Winter]] and western uplands  
**Major Production Centers:** Distributed quarry networks near [[Karndale]], [[Durgrath]], [[Brom Khazad]], and across rural highland settlements  
**Controlling Groups:** Quarry guilds, dwarven stonecutting orders, regional authorities, local settlement cooperatives  
**Primary Uses:** Construction, fortifications, roadwork, infrastructure  
**Trade Value:** Common → Strategic (region-dependent)

---

# Overview

Greyrock is the most widely used construction stone in Eryndor, forming the structural foundation of its cities, fortifications, and roads. Dense, durable, and resistant to weathering, it is valued not for rarity, but for its reliability.

Quarried from highland formations and mountain foothills, Greyrock is characterized by its muted gray coloration, fine grain, and exceptional resistance to cracking under cold conditions. It holds shape well under stress, making it ideal for load-bearing construction and long-term infrastructure.

Nearly every permanent structure in Eryndor—from dwarven holds to human settlements—is built from Greyrock in some form. It defines not only how the region is built, but how it endures.

> It does not stand out.  
> It simply stands.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Greyrock is found across much of Eryndor, particularly in:

• highland ridgelines and exposed stone formations  
• foothills of the [[Crown of Winter]]  
• upland valleys and quarry basins  
• regions adjacent to the [[Ironfang Peaks]]

These formations are the result of:

• long-term geological compression  
• cold-weather fracturing patterns  
• mineral stabilization under pressure

Because of its widespread availability, Greyrock is:

• accessible to most settlements  
• locally sourced in many regions  
• rarely dependent on long-distance transport

---

# Production

Greyrock extraction is a well-established and widely practiced industry.

---

### Quarrying Methods

• open-face quarrying along exposed ridges  
• stepped extraction in valley basins  
• controlled splitting using wedges and tools

Dwarven operations often employ:

• precision cutting techniques  
• deep quarry shafts for higher-quality stone

Human operations tend toward:

• surface extraction  
• scalable, labor-driven quarrying

---

### Material Characteristics

Greyrock is:

• dense and fine-grained  
• highly resistant to frost damage  
• structurally stable under load

It is especially valued for:

• resistance to cracking in cold climates  
• long-term durability  
• ease of shaping relative to harder stones

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Greyrock is:

• cut into blocks, slabs, or shaped stone  
• smoothed or finished depending on use  
• occasionally reinforced or fitted with metal supports

Higher-quality stone is:

• more uniform  
• easier to work  
• more desirable for large structures

---

### Transport

Greyrock is transported via:

• short-range cart and caravan systems  
• river transport in select regions  
• local distribution networks

Due to its weight, it is typically:

• sourced locally when possible  
• moved in bulk only for major projects

---

# Economic Importance

Greyrock is foundational to Eryndor’s infrastructure.

It supports:

• city construction  
• fortifications and defensive works  
• roads, bridges, and storage structures

While individually low in value, its scale of use makes it:

> one of the most economically significant materials in the region

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Greyrock is localized.

Primary actors include:

• quarry guilds  
• dwarven stonecutting orders  
• regional authorities overseeing large works  
• settlement-level cooperatives

Control is determined by:

• access to quality quarries  
• labor availability  
• proximity to construction sites

---

# Trade Networks

Greyrock is primarily used locally, but is transported for:

• major construction efforts  
• urban expansion projects  
• strategic fortifications

Long-distance trade is limited due to:

• weight  
• transport cost

However, high-quality Greyrock may be moved to:

• major cities  
• dwarven holds  
• important military sites

---

# Cultural Significance

Greyrock represents:

• permanence  
• resilience  
• practicality

It is associated with:

• enduring structures  
• generational building  
• the idea that what is built should last

In many communities, a structure built of Greyrock is assumed to be:

> meant to stand for generations

# Dangers and Limitations

• heavy and difficult to transport  
• quarrying can destabilize terrain  
• lower-quality deposits may fracture  
• labor-intensive extraction

Additionally:

• over-quarrying can damage local environments  
• poor construction practices reduce effectiveness
