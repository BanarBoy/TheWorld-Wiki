# Overview

Keshar Pepper is the primary heat-bearing spice of the subcontinental region, forming a central component of its culinary identity. Grown in humid lowlands and coastal plains, the plant produces small, deep red pods that are harvested, dried, and used in a wide range of dishes.

What distinguishes Keshar Pepper from harsher spices is its **progressive heat profile**. It begins with a mild, almost sweet flavor before gradually intensifying, creating a sustained warmth that enhances rather than overwhelms other ingredients. This makes it particularly well-suited to layered cooking, where multiple flavors are developed over time.

It is most commonly used in conjunction with **Suryan Seed oil** and **Masira Rice**, forming the backbone of everyday cuisine. While widely available, higher-grade Keshar—especially from coastal regions—commands greater value due to its stronger and more balanced flavor.

# Production

Keshar Pepper is produced at scale but requires careful handling.

### Cultivation

• plants are grown in dense, low-lying fields  
• supported by trellises or natural structures  
• require consistent watering and humidity

---

### Harvesting

• pods are picked at full maturity  
• harvested by hand to avoid damage  
• sorted by size, color, and quality

---

### Drying

• peppers are dried in sunlight or shaded racks  
• drying method affects final flavor and intensity  
• premium grades are dried slowly for consistency

Production is:

• widespread  
• labor-intensive  
• sensitive to climate variation
