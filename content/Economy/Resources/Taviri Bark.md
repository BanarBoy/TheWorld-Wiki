# Overview

Taviri Bark is a warm, aromatic spice harvested from the inner bark of trees native to the forested foothills of the Chandral Range. Carefully stripped, dried, and curled into thin segments, it produces a deep, woody fragrance with a subtle underlying sweetness.

Unlike sharper or more dominant spices, Taviri Bark serves as a **foundation ingredient**. It is rarely the defining flavor of a dish, but instead provides the structural base upon which other elements—such as the heat of **Keshar Pepper** or the richness of **Suryan oil**—are built. Its presence stabilizes and binds flavors, allowing complex combinations to develop without becoming harsh or unbalanced.

Because of this role, Taviri is nearly universal in regional cooking. It appears in both everyday meals and refined cuisine, making it one of the most consistently used and economically stable spices in the region.

# Geographic Distribution

Taviri trees grow in:

• forested foothills of the [[Chandral Range]]  
• upland regions between [[Varanji]] and [[Sarajpur]]  
• protected woodland zones with moderate elevation

They require:

• well-drained soil  
• stable seasonal rainfall  
• partial canopy cover

The best-quality bark comes from:

• slow-growing trees in higher elevations  
• undisturbed forest zones

Overharvesting in easily accessible areas has led to increased reliance on managed woodland cultivation.

---

# Production

Taviri Bark production relies on careful harvesting rather than large-scale farming.

---

### Cultivation and Management

• trees are either wild-harvested or lightly cultivated  
• forest areas are managed to prevent depletion  
• growth cycles are monitored over years

---

### Harvesting

• outer bark is removed to access the inner layer  
• inner bark is carefully stripped in thin sheets  
• improper harvesting can kill the tree

---

### Drying

• bark strips are dried in shaded environments  
• natural curling occurs during drying  
• quality depends on uniformity and aroma retention

Production is:

• steady but limited by tree growth  
• dependent on sustainable practices  
• regionally controlled

### Processing

Taviri Bark is prepared as:

• dried bark strips  
• ground powder  
• infused flavor base for oils and broths

It is often:

• lightly toasted before use  
• combined with other spices in blends  
• used whole for slow cooking applications

Taviri Bark is transported via:

• upland trade routes descending into [[Varanji]]

• bundled bark strips

• prepared blends
