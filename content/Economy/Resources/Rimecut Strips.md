# Overview

Rimecut Strips are thin-cut, salt-cured and air-dried meat strips that serve as the most widespread preserved food in Eryndor. Produced using the principles of the Frostcure tradition, they are designed for durability, portability, and reliability rather than luxury.

Made from a variety of meats—most commonly sheep, goat, or lowland cattle—Rimecuts are heavily salted with Glacier Salt and dried in cold, moving air until dense and shelf-stable. The result is a tough but chewable strip with a concentrated, savory flavor and a clean, mineral finish.

They are not considered refined, but they are trusted. Across Eryndor, Rimecut Strips are as common as bread in warmer lands, forming a core part of daily life for travelers, laborers, and soldiers.

> When fresh food fails, Rimecut remains.

Rimecut Strips are produced throughout Eryndor’s highland regions, particularly in:

• the pastoral zones surrounding [[Lake Morra]]  
• upland grazing regions in central and western Eryndor  
• lower elevations of the [[Crown of Winter]]

These areas provide:

• steady livestock supply  
• cold, dry air ideal for curing  
• access to Glacier Salt

Unlike more specialized Frostcure products, Rimecuts are:

• widely produced  
• regionally adaptable  
• not tied to a single location

# Production

Rimecut production is straightforward but requires consistency.

---

### Meat Preparation

• meat is sliced into thin, uniform strips  
• excess fat is trimmed to prevent spoilage  
• cuts vary depending on local livestock

---

### Curing Process

• strips are packed or rubbed with **Glacier Salt**  
• moisture is drawn out over several days  
• meat is hung in open-air racks or sheltered drying frames

---

### Drying

• cold, circulating air completes the process  
• no heavy smoking is used (unlike other Frostcure meats)  
• drying continues until strips are firm and stable

Production is:

• scalable  
• widely practiced  
• dependent on environmental conditions

# Dangers and Limitations

• over-salting can reduce palatability  
• improper drying can lead to spoilage  
• lower-quality cuts reduce durability  
• dependent on access to Glacier Salt

Additionally:

• excessive reliance can lead to dietary imbalance  
• long storage can harden strips excessively
