# Overview

Fellhide is the primary leather used across Eryndor, produced from the hides of Ridgeback Sheep and Broadhorn Cattle and treated through cold-environment tanning processes that emphasize durability, flexibility, and resistance to harsh conditions.

Unlike softer leathers from warmer climates, Fellhide is engineered for survival. It remains supple in freezing temperatures, resists cracking under repeated stress, and provides reliable protection against wind, moisture, and abrasion. It is not decorative by default, but it is exceptionally dependable.

Used in everything from boots and cloaks to saddles and reinforced armor, Fellhide is one of the most widely relied-upon materials in daily life across Eryndor.

> It is not worn for comfort.  
> It is worn because it works.

# Production

Fellhide production is a skilled, multi-stage craft that balances preservation with structural integrity.

---

### Raw Materials

• Ridgeback Sheep hides (lighter, more flexible Fellhide)  
• Broadhorn Cattle hides (thicker, more durable Fellhide)  
• Glacier Salt (for curing)  
• plant-based tanning agents (barks, roots, or local compounds)

---

### Curing

• hides are cleaned and heavily salted using **Glacier Salt**  
• moisture is drawn out to prevent decay  
• initial stabilization occurs before full tanning

---

### Tanning Process

• hides are soaked and treated with tanning agents over extended periods  
• worked repeatedly to maintain flexibility  
• stretched, scraped, and conditioned

Cold climate conditions allow:

• slower, more controlled tanning  
• tighter fiber structure  
• improved long-term durability

---

### Finishing

• leather is cut, shaped, and sometimes layered  
• treated with oils, waxes, or resin blends (often derived from Frostpine)  
• prepared for specific uses (gear, clothing, armor, etc.)
