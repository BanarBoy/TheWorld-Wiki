# Overview

Duskbloom Teak is a prized hardwood harvested from the upland river jungles where the descending waters of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]] meet the dense growth of the [[Verdant Expanse]] and the outer fringes of Masari influence. These massive, buttressed trees grow in humid transitional zones, fed by mineral-rich runoff and shaped by competition within dense jungle canopies.

The wood is immediately recognizable: a golden-brown heartwood marked by dark, ink-like radial streaks that spread organically across each cut surface. When freshly exposed, the sapwood emits a faint amber glow at dawn and dusk—an effect that gives the wood its name and contributes to its mystique among craftsmen and traders alike.

Duskbloom occupies a unique position in Thalassar’s material economy. While Crestpine defines structural strength and naval reach, Duskbloom defines finish, identity, and prestige. Its natural resistance to rot and shipworm makes it indispensable for hull planking, while its beauty ensures its dominance in high-end architecture and crafted goods.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Duskbloom Teak grows in a narrow ecological band where multiple environments overlap:

- upland river jungles of the [[Verdant Expanse]]
- transitional zones along Khaldren tributaries descending into lowland forests
- Masari-facing mainland jungle fringes

Key concentrations occur along:

- river systems feeding into [[Irathel]]
- deeper jungle territories accessible from [[Ilvara]]
- elevated jungle corridors where water flow and soil stability balance

It requires:

- constant humidity
- mineral-rich runoff from highlands
- stable terrain above floodplains

This combination is rare, making Duskbloom geographically constrained and difficult to cultivate elsewhere.

---

# Production

Duskbloom logging is decentralized, dangerous, and highly specialized.

Production zones include:

- frontier logging networks centered on [[Irathel]]
- deeper, less stable extraction routes extending from [[Ilvara]]
- tributary corridors descending from the Khaldren Spine

Harvesting involves:

- clearing dense jungle growth around target trees
- cutting massive buttressed trunks in confined conditions
- managing unpredictable terrain and environmental hazards

[[Irathel]] serves as the primary coordination hub for organized logging, while [[Ilvara]] pushes deeper into the Expanse, supplying rarer and often higher-quality timber at greater risk.

Unlike Crestpine, Duskbloom cannot be harvested in uniform operations—each tree is effectively an individual prize.

---

# Processing and Transport

Duskbloom must be processed close to its source to preserve quality.

Processing includes:

- cutting into planks near logging sites
- controlled drying to prevent warping
- polishing to enhance natural figuring

Transport follows river systems:

- timber is moved downstream from jungle camps to [[Irathel]] and [[Ilvara]]
- consolidated into larger shipments
- transported via river and caravan toward Seluqar-controlled trade routes

Final export typically occurs through:

- the [[Glasswater Reach]]
- [[Seluqar]] shipyards and merchant districts

Due to its value, even small shipments are tracked and protected.

---

# Economic Importance

Duskbloom Teak is a **prestige-defining material**.

It is essential for:

- outer hull construction of high-performance Seluqar vessels
- elite architecture in Seluqar and Kelresh
- high-end furniture and interior craftsmanship

Key dependencies:

- Seluqar shipbuilders rely on it for speed and durability
- merchant elites use it to display wealth and refinement
- Verdant frontier economies depend on its extraction

It does not sustain life—but it defines status, identity, and economic ambition.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Duskbloom is fragmented:

- jungle logging syndicates control extraction zones
- frontier guilds manage labor and risk
- river authorities influence movement through key corridors
- Seluqar merchant houses dominate refinement and export

[[Irathel]] represents structured control, while [[Ilvara]] represents expansion and risk, creating ongoing tension between stability and opportunity.

---

# Trade Networks

Duskbloom flows through layered trade routes:

- harvested in upland jungles
- transported to [[Irathel]] and [[Ilvara]]
- moved along river systems into broader trade networks
- exported via Seluqar maritime systems

From there, it reaches:

- shipyards across Thalassar
- elite construction markets
- foreign buyers seeking high-quality timber

“Duskbloom planks” are widely recognized as a mark of prestige.

---

# Cultural Significance

Duskbloom is deeply tied to craft and ritual.

Notably:

- carpenters burn Duskbloom sawdust with Masari resin during keel-layings
- the smoke is believed to bind the wood’s spirit to the sea

The wood represents:

- transformation from jungle to vessel
- individuality through unique grain patterns
- the merging of natural chaos and human refinement

Ownership and use of Duskbloom is often seen as a sign of discernment and status.
