# Overview

Kharzul Sheep are the primary livestock of the steppe peoples of northern Kiyora, forming the foundation of their material economy and daily survival. Adapted to harsh climates and constant movement, these animals provide a reliable and renewable source of essential resources across multiple regions of the steppe.

While found throughout the plains, the greatest concentrations of Kharzul Sheep are maintained in the fertile grazing basins of [[Sujun]] and the wide open expanses of [[Tsetsui]]. These regions support large, stable herds, allowing clans to build long-term strength and resource security. From these centers, herds are moved seasonally through corridors such as [[Uluur]] and into harsher environments like [[Khordai]].

Unlike livestock raised in settled regions, Kharzul Sheep are bred for resilience rather than size or yield. They can survive on sparse grazing, endure extreme seasonal changes, and travel long distances with migrating clans. This mobility allows herds to be redistributed across regions like [[Tarkhul]], where animals are often gathered and processed to support large-scale movement or conflict.

Their importance lies in versatility. From a single animal, the steppe peoples derive wool for **Kharu Felt**, meat for both fresh consumption and preservation (including **Sarnak Strips**), and fat used for fuel and basic processing. Even bones and sinew are repurposed for tools and composite materials, including components used in **Khagan Bows**.

In every region of the steppe, Kharzul Sheep are not merely livestock—they are the foundation upon which life is sustained.

# Geographic Distribution

Kharzul Sheep are herded across a wide regional network:

• the expansive grazing plains of [[Tsetsui]]  
• the dense herd basins of [[Sujun]]  
• the highland and colder zones of [[Khordai]]  
• migration corridors through [[Uluur]]  
• seasonal gathering and processing regions near [[Tarkhul]]

These regions provide:

• varied grazing environments for seasonal movement  
• natural distribution of herd pressure across the land  
• resilience against environmental fluctuation

Because of this system:

• herds are constantly in motion  
• no single region fully contains production  
• stability comes from regional balance rather than fixed control

# Production

Kharzul Sheep are raised through coordinated nomadic herding practices across multiple regions.

---

### Herding

• flocks are maintained in large, mobile groups  
• major herd concentrations are managed in [[Sujun]] and [[Tsetsui]]  
• clans migrate through [[Uluur]] to maintain grazing balance

---

### Breeding

• selective breeding emphasizes hardiness and wool quality  
• strong bloodlines are preserved within specific clan territories  
• regional variation exists between lowland and highland herds

---

### Resource Harvesting

• wool is sheared regularly, especially in [[Sujun]]  
• animals are slaughtered in greater numbers near [[Tarkhul]] for provisioning  
• fat, bone, and sinew are processed across all regions

Production is:

• continuous and renewable  
• dependent on coordinated regional movement  
• shaped by both environment and tradition

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Kharzul Sheep yield multiple integrated resources:

• **Wool** → processed heavily in [[Sujun]] into **Kharu Felt**  
• **Meat** → prepared in regions like [[Tarkhul]] and preserved as **Sarnak Strips**  
• **Fat** → used locally as fuel and processing material  
• **Bone & Sinew** → used in tools and composite crafting (including bowmaking in [[Ordugar]])

---

### Transport

Kharzul Sheep are:

• moved continuously across regional routes  
• redistributed seasonally between grazing zones  
• rarely confined or transported in fixed systems

This allows:

• flexible response to environmental conditions  
• sustained production across large territories

# Economic Importance

Kharzul Sheep are the **foundation of the steppe economy**.

They:

• supply raw materials for multiple key industries  
• sustain food production and preservation  
• enable textile, craft, and military production

Regional roles include:

• [[Sujun]] — primary herd concentration and wool production  
• [[Tsetsui]] — expansive grazing and herd expansion  
• [[Tarkhul]] — provisioning and large-scale processing  
• [[Khordai]] — highland adaptation and seasonal resilience

They are:

> the resource that makes all other resources possible

---

# Controlling Powers

Control is decentralized but regionally influenced.

Primary actors include:

• clans dominant in [[Sujun]] controlling large herds  
• migrating groups managing routes through [[Uluur]]  
• provisioning groups operating in [[Tarkhul]]  
• highland herders in [[Khordai]]

Power is determined by:

• herd size and health  
• control of migration routes  
• access to key regional zones

# Trade Networks

Kharzul Sheep and their products move through:

• internal steppe networks connecting all major regions  
• trade routes into settled areas of [[Kiyora]]  
• limited external export in processed forms

Trade characteristics:

• raw animals rarely leave the steppe  
• value increases through regional processing  
• movement follows seasonal patterns

# Dangers and Limitations

• disruption of migration routes through [[Uluur]]
