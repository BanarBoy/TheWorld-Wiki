# Overview

Kelresh Coffee is widely regarded as the finest coffee in the known world, cultivated in the misted highlands east of [[Kelresh]] where elevation, climate, and soil combine to produce exceptionally dense and aromatic beans.

Unlike lower-grown varieties, Kelresh beans develop slowly under cool nights and filtered sunlight, resulting in complex flavor profiles and high oil content. This makes them uniquely suited to controlled roasting, which has evolved into a defining craft of the region.

Coffee in Kelresh is not merely an agricultural product—it is a **cultural institution**, shaping daily life, trade, and social interaction. From common roadside brews to rare Highcrest servings in noble courts, Kelresh coffee exists as a tiered system of quality, ritual, and prestige.

> In Kelresh, coffee is not consumed quickly.  
> It is prepared, served, and remembered.

# Geographic Distribution

Kelresh Coffee is grown exclusively in the eastern highlands surrounding [[Kelresh]].

Key cultivation zones include:

---

### 🌄 Highcrest Ridges (Elite Zone)

• highest elevations  
• slowest growth cycles  
• lowest yield, highest quality

👉 Source of **Highcrest-grade beans**

---

### 🌿 Mid-Slope Terraces (Core Production)

• terraced farming systems  
• balanced climate and soil  
• consistent, scalable output

👉 Backbone of Kelresh coffee trade

---

### 🌱 Lower Highland Edges

• faster growth cycles  
• higher yield, lower complexity  
• often used in blends

---

The region’s defining conditions:

• mineral-rich soils  
• heavy morning mist  
• cool night temperatures  
• gradual sunlight exposure

---

# Production

Kelresh Coffee is cultivated through labor-intensive, high-skill farming.

Methods include:

• terrace farming along steep slopes  
• hand-picking of ripe cherries  
• selective harvesting to ensure consistency

Production is:

• slow  
• seasonal  
• highly dependent on environmental stability

Higher-grade beans require:

• stricter selection  
• smaller yields  
• more precise handling

---

# Processing and Transport

Coffee processing is a defining stage of its identity.

---

### Processing

• cherries are cleaned and dried shortly after harvest  
• beans are separated, cured, and graded  
• roasting occurs primarily in [[Kelresh]]

---

### Roasting Tradition — **Kelresh Roast**

Kelresh Roast is the standard processing style:

• slow, controlled roasting  
• deep caramelization  
• rich, oil-forward finish

This creates:

• bold flavor  
• lasting aroma  
• high consistency

---

### Transport

Coffee is exported via:

• overland routes from [[Kelresh]]  
• Seluqar-controlled maritime trade networks

Higher grades are:

• transported in smaller, secured shipments  
• often pre-contracted
