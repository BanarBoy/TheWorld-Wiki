# Overview

Brindlegrain is the primary staple crop of Eryndor, a hardy cereal grown in cold, wind-exposed environments where more delicate grains cannot survive. Named for its mottled, dark-streaked husk, it is known for its resilience, reliability, and consistent—if modest—yield.

Unlike the abundant grains of warmer lands, Brindlegrain does not produce excess. It grows slowly, tolerates poor soils, and withstands harsh seasonal shifts, making it uniquely suited to Eryndor’s demanding climate.

It forms the backbone of daily life, used in simple breads, dense porridges, and as a key input for brewing. Nearly every household, settlement, and caravan depends on it in some form.

> It does not flourish.  
> It endures—and feeds those who do the same.

# Geographic Distribution

Brindlegrain is cultivated across:

• upland valleys with partial wind shelter  
• basins and lowland pockets between highland ridges  
• marginal farmland unsuitable for more delicate crops  
• regions surrounding the [[Lake Morra]] basin and beyond

It thrives in:

• cold, short growing seasons  
• rocky or nutrient-poor soils  
• irregular rainfall patterns

Farmers favor locations that offer:

• minimal wind protection  
• workable soil depth  
• reliable seasonal thaw

Because of its adaptability, Brindlegrain is:

• grown across nearly all settled regions of Eryndor  
• rarely dependent on a single geographic center

# Production

Brindlegrain farming emphasizes reliability over yield.

---

### Cultivation

• sown early in short growing seasons  
• grown in tightly managed fields or terraces  
• often rotated with grazing land to preserve soil

Farmers prioritize:

• crop survival over output  
• soil preservation  
• consistent yearly harvests

---

### Harvesting

• harvested late in the season before frost return  
• manually or with simple tools  
• carefully dried and stored immediately

Yield is:

• moderate to low per acre  
• highly consistent compared to other grains

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Brindlegrain is:

• threshed and separated from husks  
• ground into coarse flour  
• sometimes cracked or boiled whole

It is used to produce:

• dense breads  
• porridge and stews  
• brewing mash for Stonebrew

---

### Transport

Brindlegrain is transported via:

• caravan networks  
• river routes (where available)  
• local trade systems

It is typically moved as:

• raw grain  
• milled flour (less stable, higher value)

Because of its importance, it is:

• widely stored  
• carefully rationed in lean seasons

# Economic Importance

Brindlegrain is one of the most critical resources in Eryndor.

It:

• sustains the majority of the population  
• feeds livestock such as Ridgeback Sheep and Broadhorn Cattle  
• fuels brewing traditions

Its importance makes it:

> both common and indispensable

A failed Brindlegrain harvest would:

• destabilize food systems  
• disrupt trade  
• threaten entire regions

# Dangers and Limitations

• vulnerable to extreme seasonal disruption  
• low yield compared to warmer-climate crops  
• dependence on careful land management  
• storage risks (moisture, pests)

Additionally:

• over-farming can degrade already poor soils  
• failed harvests have severe consequences
