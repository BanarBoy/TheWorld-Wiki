# Overview

Sarnak Strips are dried meat rations produced by the steppe peoples of northern Kiyora, designed for long-term storage and easy transport across vast distances. Thinly cut and carefully dried, these strips provide a dense, reliable source of nutrition that can be carried and consumed with minimal preparation.

In a culture defined by movement, Sarnak Strips are essential. Riders traveling for days or weeks rely on them as a primary food source, allowing sustained activity without the need for cooking or resupply. They are eaten during travel, between engagements, and in environments where other forms of food are impractical.

The preparation of Sarnak Strips emphasizes preservation over flavor. While lightly seasoned in some regions, the primary goal is durability. Properly prepared strips can last for extended periods without spoiling, making them invaluable for both daily life and organized military campaigns.

Regional specialization gives Sarnak Strips distinct identities. The large-scale processing grounds of [[Tarkhul]] produce the most consistent and durable rations, while strips made in [[Khordai]] tend to be drier and harder due to colder, harsher conditions. In contrast, smaller-scale production in [[Sujun]] and [[Tsetsui]] is more varied, reflecting local herd conditions and clan traditions.

# Geographic Distribution

Sarnak Strips are produced across a coordinated regional system:

• the large-scale provisioning zones of [[Tarkhul]]  
• the herd-rich plains of [[Sujun]]  
• the expansive grazing lands of [[Tsetsui]]  
• the central steppe near [[Harakum]]  
• the highland drying regions of [[Khordai]]  
• migration and transport routes through [[Uluur]]

They depend on:

• meat from **Kharzul Sheep** and, less commonly, horses  
• dry, wind-exposed environments—especially in [[Tarkhul]] and [[Khordai]]  
• access to herds and coordinated slaughter during key periods

These regions support:

• steady livestock supply  
• ideal natural drying conditions  
• integration of food production into seasonal cycles

Because of this system:

• production scales with herd movement  
• quality varies by region and climate  
• supply can be concentrated before major campaigns

# Production

Sarnak Strips are produced through efficient but disciplined methods, often coordinated at a regional level.

---

### Butchering

• animals are slaughtered as needed, with large-scale processing in [[Tarkhul]]  
• meat is selected and trimmed carefully  
• excess fat is removed to prevent spoilage

---

### Cutting

• meat is sliced into thin, uniform strips  
• consistency ensures even drying and preservation

---

### Drying

• strips are hung or laid out in open air  
• strong winds in [[Tarkhul]] and [[Khordai]] accelerate curing  
• drying time varies by region and season

---

### Finishing

• strips are inspected and sorted  
• some are lightly salted or treated for extended storage  
• finished bundles are prepared for transport or stockpiling

Production is:

• highly scalable in regions like [[Tarkhul]]  
• environmentally dependent  
• integrated into both daily life and military preparation

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Sarnak Strips vary by regional preparation:

• **Standard Strips** — common across [[Tsetsui]] and [[Sujun]]  
• **Tarkhul Cuts** — highly durable, mass-produced for campaigns  
• **Highland Strips** — extremely dry, produced in [[Khordai]]  
• **Fine Cuts** — thinner, higher-quality strips for trade

---

### Transport

Sarnak Strips are:

• bundled and stored in saddlebags  
• carried across migration routes such as [[Uluur]]  
• stockpiled in regions like [[Tarkhul]] before major movements

Because of their durability:

• they withstand long-distance travel  
• they require minimal storage conditions
