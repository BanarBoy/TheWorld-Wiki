# Overview

Kharu Felt—known to outsiders as “Steppefelt”—is the most essential material produced by the steppe peoples of northern Kiyora. Made from compressed and treated wool, it forms the foundation of daily life, enabling shelter, clothing, and protection in an environment defined by movement and environmental extremes.

Unlike woven textiles, Kharu Felt is created by matting wool fibers together through moisture, pressure, and motion. The result is a dense, flexible material that resists wind, sheds water, and retains heat—qualities critical for survival across regions ranging from the open plains of [[Tsetsui]] to the colder highlands of [[Khordai]].

Its versatility is unmatched. Entire dwellings—most notably yurts—are constructed using layered Kharu Felt, allowing them to be assembled, disassembled, and transported across migration routes such as those passing through [[Uluur]]. It is also used in garments, saddle padding, blankets, and as reinforcement in armor and equipment.

Though common in form, high-quality Kharu Felt reflects regional expertise. Felt produced in [[Sujun]] is often denser and more uniform due to large, stable herds, while pieces finished near [[Tarkhul]] are more refined and prepared for trade or long-distance use.

# Geographic Distribution

Kharu Felt is produced across an interconnected regional system:

• the dense herd and wool regions of [[Sujun]]  
• the expansive grazing plains of [[Tsetsui]]  
• the central processing zones near [[Harakum]]  
• mobile production routes through [[Uluur]]  
• highland refinement areas in [[Khordai]]  
• redistribution and finishing regions near [[Tarkhul]]

It depends on:

• wool from [[Kharzul Sheep]], especially abundant in [[Sujun]]  
• access to water sources for processing  
• open terrain for rolling, compressing, and drying

These regions support:

• large-scale wool production  
• continuous, decentralized manufacturing  
• integration of production into migration cycles

Because of its nature:

• nearly every clan produces Kharu Felt  
• quality and style vary by region

# Production

Kharu Felt is produced through physical, labor-intensive, and communal methods.

---

### Wool Collection

• wool is gathered from [[Kharzul Sheep]], primarily in [[Sujun]] and [[Tsetsui]]  
• fibers are cleaned and sorted based on quality

---

### Layering

• wool is spread into thick, even sheets  
• fibers are aligned to ensure structural strength

---

### Compression

• layers are moistened and tightly rolled  
• pressure is applied through repeated motion—often during travel across [[Uluur]]  
• in some cases, rolls are dragged behind animals to accelerate bonding

---

### Finishing

• felt is shaped, trimmed, and reinforced  
• higher-quality pieces are refined near [[Harakum]] or [[Tarkhul]]  
• drying conditions vary by region, affecting final texture

Production is:

• labor-intensive but widely accessible  
• scalable across clan structures  
• influenced by movement and environment

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Kharu Felt is produced in several regionally influenced forms:

• **Structural Felt** — thick, durable layers used for yurts, common in [[Tsetsui]]  
• **Garment Felt** — lighter, more flexible, often produced in [[Harakum]]  
• **Refined Felt** — smoother, higher-quality material finished near [[Tarkhul]] for trade

---

### Transport

Kharu Felt is:

• rolled and bundled for transport  
• carried along migration routes such as [[Uluur]]  
• distributed through trade networks when surplus exists

Because of its durability:

• it withstands long-distance travel  
• remains usable even after repeated movement

# Economic Importance

Kharu Felt is a **foundational material resource**.

It:

• enables mobile housing and infrastructure  
• supports clothing, equipment, and insulation  
• provides a tradable good in refined form

Regional roles include:

• [[Sujun]] — primary wool production and raw material base  
• [[Tsetsui]] — large-scale production through herd access  
• [[Harakum]] — central processing and cultural use  
• [[Tarkhul]] — refinement and redistribution for trade

It is:

> the material that allows movement without losing stability

# Controlling Powers

Control is decentralized but regionally influenced.

Primary actors include:

• herding clans in [[Sujun]] controlling wool supply  
• production groups across [[Tsetsui]]  
• artisan families refining felt near [[Harakum]] and [[Tarkhul]]

Power is determined by:

• herd access  
• production capacity  
• craftsmanship quality

---

# Trade Networks

Kharu Felt is traded:

• between clans across the steppe  
• through redistribution points such as [[Tarkhul]]  
• to settled regions of [[Kiyora]] in refined form

Trade characteristics:

• common locally  
• more valuable when refined  
• valued for durability and practicality

# Cultural Significance

Kharu Felt represents:

• self-sufficiency  
• resilience  
• continuity through movement

It is:

• present in every aspect of daily life
