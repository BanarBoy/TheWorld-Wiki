# Stonebrook White

**Resource Type:** Agricultural Product / Commercial Wine  
**Primary Region(s):** River valleys and lowland vineyards across western [[Valecaryn]]  
**Major Production Centers:** Independent vineyard holdings along the Stonebrook River and surrounding lowlands  
**Controlling Groups:** Local growers, vintners, merchant cooperatives, minor landholders  
**Primary Uses:** Tavern consumption, merchant trade, everyday households, ship provisions  
**Trade Value:** Common / Affordable

---

# Overview

Stonebrook White is one of the most widely consumed white wines in [[Valecaryn]], valued for its crisp character, affordability, and consistent drinkability. Produced across the fertile lowlands surrounding the Stonebrook River, it serves as a cornerstone of everyday wine consumption throughout the western regions of the continent.

Unlike the carefully managed vineyard systems of the [[Ambervale Downs]], Stonebrook White is not tied to a single noble house or tightly controlled estate network. Instead, it is produced by a patchwork of independent growers, small estates, and cooperative vintners. This decentralized production gives the wine a slightly varied character from batch to batch, though its overall profile remains reliably bright and refreshing.

While it lacks the prestige of wines such as [[Amberfall Reserve]] or the refined whites of Thornweave estates, Stonebrook White has earned a different kind of reputation—one built on accessibility and honest quality. It is the wine most commonly found in taverns, aboard merchant vessels, and in the homes of working and middle-class households.

For many across Valecaryn, Stonebrook White is not merely an alternative to finer wines—it is the standard by which everyday wine is judged.

---

# Geographic Origin

Stonebrook White originates from vineyards spread along the banks and surrounding lowlands of the Stonebrook River, a slow-moving waterway that nourishes a broad agricultural region in western [[Valecaryn]].

The area is defined by:

• fertile alluvial soils deposited by seasonal river flooding  
• moderate rainfall supporting consistent vine growth  
• gentle terrain allowing for widespread small-scale cultivation  
• a temperate climate producing steady, predictable harvests

Unlike the elevated, carefully selected slopes of the Ambervale Downs, the Stonebrook region is expansive and accessible, allowing a large number of independent growers to cultivate vines.

This abundance of viable land is a key factor in the wine’s widespread production and availability.

---

# Production

Stonebrook White is produced through a decentralized but well-understood process shared among regional vintners.

Grapes are harvested during the late growing season, typically by small crews working individual plots. The fruit is then brought to local wineries or cooperative pressing houses, where fermentation is conducted in large batches.

The focus of production is efficiency and consistency rather than refinement. Fermentation is managed to preserve freshness and clarity, resulting in a wine that emphasizes brightness over complexity.

Aging is minimal, with most batches prepared for distribution within a year of harvest. While individual producers may introduce slight variations in technique, the overall goal remains the same: a reliable, refreshing wine suitable for immediate consumption.

---

# Processing and Storage

After fermentation, Stonebrook White is stored in large barrels or casks before distribution.

Because it is not intended for long-term aging, the wine is typically shipped soon after production. Merchant cooperatives play a major role in aggregating supply from multiple producers and preparing shipments for transport.

Storage conditions are less stringent than those required for reserve wines, though care is still taken to prevent spoilage during transit.

In most markets, Stonebrook White is sold in barrels or simple bottles designed for practicality rather than presentation.

---

# Economic Importance

Stonebrook White represents a significant component of the regional economy in western [[Valecaryn]].

Its production supports:

• independent vineyard owners and farming families  
• local vintners and cooperative wineries  
• laborers involved in harvesting and processing  
• merchant groups responsible for distribution

Because of its scale and accessibility, Stonebrook White provides stable income across a broad segment of the population, rather than concentrating wealth within a single noble house.

It also serves as a critical trade good in lower and mid-tier markets, ensuring steady demand across the continent.

---

# Trade Networks

Stonebrook White is distributed widely throughout [[Valecaryn]] via both river and overland trade routes.

Major destinations include:

• inland trade hubs such as [[Lethryn Prime]]  
• coastal ports including [[Westport]] and [[Velossa]]  
• smaller market towns supplied by caravan networks

The Stonebrook River itself serves as a primary transport artery, allowing barrels of wine to be shipped efficiently to larger trade centers.

Because of its affordability and popularity, Stonebrook White is a common cargo aboard merchant vessels and caravans alike.

---

# Cultural Significance

Stonebrook White is deeply embedded in the everyday culture of Valecaryn.

It is the wine of:

• taverns and dockside inns  
• merchant crews and caravan guards  
• family meals and casual gatherings

Unlike prestige wines, it carries little symbolic weight—but significant familiarity. Its presence signals comfort, routine, and shared experience rather than status.

Many who can afford finer wines still choose Stonebrook White in informal settings, valuing its drinkability and reliability over prestige.

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some estate vintners in the [[Ambervale Downs]] dismiss Stonebrook White as inconsistent or inferior, though its popularity suggests otherwise.

There are occasional disputes between independent growers and merchant cooperatives over pricing and distribution rights.

Rumors also persist that certain producers have begun experimenting with higher-quality batches, attempting to elevate Stonebrook beyond its traditional role.
