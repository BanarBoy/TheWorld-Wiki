---

# Overview

Almaraine Cream is a soft, high-fat cheese made from Broadhorn cattle milk in the fertile lowlands of southern Eryndor. Known for its smooth texture, mild tang, and rich body, it represents the most refined expression of the region’s dairy culture.

Unlike the hard, durable cheeses of the north such as Rimewheel, Almaraine Cream is made to be consumed fresh or lightly aged. It is softer, more delicate, and more responsive to handling, requiring skill and attention rather than endurance to produce well.

Its character reflects the land it comes from: fuller, steadier, and less defined by scarcity. It is not extravagant—but it is undeniably good.

> It does not need to last.  
> It only needs to be right.

# Geographic Distribution

Almaraine Cream is produced throughout:

• the river-fed pastures of the **Almaraine Basin**  
• dairy regions surrounding [[Gildhall]] and [[Belthorn]]  
• fertile lowlands near [[Alcienne]]  
• lower reaches of [[Ulthridge]]

These regions provide:

• abundant Broadhorn milk  
• stable grazing conditions  
• access to clean water and processing space

Because of its delicate nature, production is:

• localized  
• tied closely to dairy regions  
• less viable in harsher northern climates

---

# Production

Almaraine Cream production balances simplicity with precision.

---

### Milk Collection

• fresh Broadhorn milk is used immediately  
• high fat content is essential  
• milk is handled carefully to preserve quality

---

### Curd Formation

• gently heated and cultured  
• allowed to form soft curds  
• minimal pressing to retain moisture

---

### Setting and Aging

• lightly drained and shaped  
• aged briefly (days to weeks) depending on style  
• sometimes wrapped or stored in cool environments

The process emphasizes:

• control  
• timing  
• restraint

---

### Variants

Almaraine Cream appears in several forms:

• **Fresh-set** — soft, spreadable, very mild  
• **Table-set** — slightly firmer, sliceable  
• **Light-aged** — deeper flavor, still creamy

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Most Almaraine Cream is:

• produced in small batches  
• handled manually  
• consumed close to its origin

---

### Transport

Due to its softness, it is transported:

• short distances via local trade  
• along the **Almaraine River** in controlled shipments  
• carefully packed to prevent damage

Long-distance trade is:

• limited  
• reserved for higher-quality batches

It:

• supports dairy economies  
• supplies taverns and markets  
• represents regional culinary identity

While not a bulk export like preserved goods, it is:

> a high-value, high-quality product

# Dangers and Limitations

• sensitivity to handling and temperature  
• dependence on consistent milk supply  
• limited scalability

Additionally:

• spoilage risk during transport  
• quality variation between producers
