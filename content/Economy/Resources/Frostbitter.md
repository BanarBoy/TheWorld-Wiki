# Overview

Frostbitter is a sharp, dry ale brewed in the high, wind-scoured regions of the [[Crown of Winter]], where survival depends on clarity, endurance, and discipline. Defined by its intense bitterness and clean, cutting finish, Frostbitter stands apart from the heavier and more structured ales of Eryndor’s settled regions.

It is brewed not for comfort, but for function. Its herbal sharpness and crisp body make it a drink that clears the senses rather than dulls them, favored by those who operate in the most exposed and unforgiving environments.

Unlike Karn Black or Coldfire Ale, Frostbitter is not standardized. Each batch reflects the land it comes from—its herbs, its water, and the hand of the brewer—making it one of the most regionally distinct products in Eryndor.

> It does not warm you.  
> It does not comfort you.  
> It keeps you sharp.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Frostbitter is produced almost exclusively along:

• the eastern slopes of the [[Crown of Winter]]  
• high-altitude ridgelines and windbreak valleys  
• seasonal alpine settlements and frontier outposts

These regions are defined by:

• thin air and low temperatures  
• short growing seasons  
• scattered, resilient plant life

The defining ingredients of Frostbitter—alpine bittering herbs—grow only in:

• exposed, mineral-poor soils  
• high elevations with intense seasonal stress

This gives the brew its signature:

• sharpness  
• dryness  
• lingering bitterness

---

# Production

Frostbitter is brewed in small, localized batches with significant variation.

---

### Ingredients

• lightly roasted highland grains  
• glacial or snowmelt water  
• cold-tolerant yeast strains  
• wild alpine herbs unique to each region

---

### Brewing Process

• grains are prepared for a lighter body  
• herbs are added in multiple stages for layered bitterness  
• fermentation occurs in cold, often improvised conditions  
• minimal filtration preserves sharpness and character

Unlike other Eryndori brews:

• recipes are rarely written down  
• knowledge is passed locally or within small groups  
• no two regions produce identical Frostbitter

Production is:

• small-scale  
• irregular  
• deeply tied to place

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Conditioning

Frostbitter is:

• lightly conditioned  
• rarely aged beyond short periods  
• consumed relatively fresh

Extended aging reduces its defining sharpness.

---

### Transport

Frostbitter is transported in:

• small casks  
• reinforced travel vessels

It moves through:

• limited caravan routes descending from the Crown of Winter  
• supply lines used by scouts and frontier patrols  
• occasional trade through larger hubs like [[Ravenfjord]]

Due to its variability and niche appeal:

• it is rarely mass-exported  
• most is consumed near its place of origin

---

# Economic Importance

Frostbitter plays a specialized but vital role in frontier economies.

It:

• supports isolated brewing communities  
• provides a durable, functional beverage for harsh conditions  
• contributes to localized trade networks

Its value lies not in scale, but in:

> necessity and identity

---

# Controlling Powers

Frostbitter is almost entirely decentralized.

It is produced by:

• independent brewers in alpine settlements  
• small frontier clans  
• scout and ranger groups maintaining their own supply

There is:

• no central authority  
• no unified standard  
• no large-scale control

Reputation is the only currency that matters.

---

# Trade Networks

Frostbitter circulates through:

• local exchange between settlements  
• supply chains used by frontier patrols  
• small-scale trade into Eryndor’s interior

It occasionally reaches:

• niche taverns in [[Ravenfjord]]  
• adventurous markets in Valecaryn

But remains largely:

> a regional product

• independence from centralized systems

• scouts and rangers

# Dangers and Limitations

• highly variable quality  
• dependence on wild alpine herbs  
• difficult and inconsistent production  
• risk of improper brewing in harsh conditions

Additionally:

• misuse of certain herbs can result in toxic batches  
• loss of local knowledge can degrade entire regional styles
