---

# Overview

Paleweave is the finest textile produced in Eryndor, a pale, tightly woven fabric derived from rare high-altitude fleece and refined through advanced weaving techniques. Unlike the rugged durability of Grimfleece, Paleweave represents **control, precision, and restraint**, transforming a harsh environment into something refined and deliberate.

The fiber itself is naturally pale—ranging from bone-white to soft ash—and exceptionally fine, but its true value lies in the weaving. Through careful alignment, tension control, and multi-layered construction, Paleweave achieves a balance of lightness and insulation that is unmatched by other textiles.

Garments made from Paleweave are deceptively simple in appearance. They lack ornamentation, bold color, or overt decoration. Their value is recognized not through display, but through **material quality, structure, and subtlety**.

> It does not draw the eye.  
> It rewards those who know where to look.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Paleweave originates from a narrow range of environments where both the fiber and the craft can exist.

Primary sources include:

• high-altitude grazing zones along the [[Ironfang Peaks]]  
• controlled upland pastures near [[Rhayne]]  
• isolated highland estates bordering the [[Valdar]] region

These regions provide:

• cold but stable climates  
• mineral-rich but sparse grazing  
• low-stress, controlled herd conditions

The animals that produce Paleweave fiber require:

• elevation  
• consistent temperature patterns  
• careful management

Outside these conditions, the fleece loses its fineness and structural integrity.

# Production

Paleweave production is deliberately limited and highly controlled.

---

### Fiber Harvesting

• sourced from select highland breeds  
• fleece is finer, lighter, and more uniform than Grimfleece  
• harvested in small quantities under controlled conditions

---

### Weaving (Defining Stage)

The defining characteristic of Paleweave is its construction:

• fibers are tightly aligned and tensioned during spinning  
• woven in layered structures to trap heat without bulk  
• minimal finishing to preserve natural properties

Weaving is performed by:

• specialized artisan houses  
• trained craftsmen working under strict standards  
• often within noble-controlled workshops

Production is:

• low-volume  
• high-skill  
• time-intensive

---

### Processing Centers

Primary weaving and finishing occur in:

• [[Alcienne]] — aristocratic textile center  
• [[Corvaire]] — refinement and finishing  
• highland estate workshops

---

### Transport

Paleweave is transported as:

• finished garments  
• folded cloth bolts

It moves through:

• controlled caravan routes into southern Eryndor  
• merchant networks tied to Valdar and external trade

Due to its value, it is:

• rarely shipped in bulk  
• often pre-commissioned  
• closely tracked

---

# Economic Importance

Paleweave is not a mass commodity—it is a **prestige material**.

It supports:

• aristocratic economies  
• specialized artisan industries  
• high-value trade networks

Cities like [[Alcienne]] and [[Corvaire]] depend on it as part of their identity as centers of refinement.

Its importance lies in:

> demonstrating mastery, not meeting demand

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Paleweave is concentrated but not absolute.

Primary actors include:

• highland estate owners controlling fiber production  
• aristocratic weaving houses managing craftsmanship  
• Valdar-aligned nobles influencing distribution and standards

Unlike Grimfleece, Paleweave is:

• structured  
• curated  
• intentionally limited

---

# Trade Networks

Paleweave is exported selectively to:

• Valecaryn noble courts  
• Seluqar merchant elites  
• foreign aristocratic markets

It is especially valued in:

• courtly fashion  
• diplomatic gifting  
• elite personal attire

---

# Cultural Significance

Paleweave represents:

• restraint over excess  
• mastery over environment  
• refinement without display

In Eryndor’s southern and eastern courts, it signals:

• status without ostentation  
• discipline and lineage  
• understanding of material value

Bright dyes and heavy ornamentation are often avoided, as they are seen as:

> diminishing the integrity of the fabric

---

# Dangers and Limitations

• extremely limited production capacity  
• dependence on specific environmental conditions  
• reliance on highly skilled artisans  
• vulnerability to loss of technique

Additionally:

• improper weaving ruins material value  
• expansion of production risks degrading quality
