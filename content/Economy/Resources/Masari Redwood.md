# Overview

Masari Redwood is a rare, slow-growing hardwood native to the dense interior jungles of the [[Masari Archipelago]]. Distinguished by its deep crimson grain and natural luster, it is one of the most coveted timber resources in the known world. When cut and polished, the wood reveals flowing patterns of dark red, amber, and black veining, giving each piece a unique and unmistakable character.

Unlike common construction timber, Masari Redwood is valued for **appearance, workability, and longevity** rather than raw strength. It resists warping in humid climates, takes fine carving exceptionally well, and develops a richer tone over time. These qualities make it the material of choice for elite furniture, ceremonial interiors, and high-status architectural elements.

Its rarity is not simply due to geography, but to time. Redwood groves take decades—sometimes centuries—to mature, and cannot be rapidly replaced once harvested. As a result, Masari Redwood exists at the intersection of natural limitation and external demand, making it both a luxury material and a point of economic tension.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Masari Redwood grows exclusively within the **deep interior jungles** of the [[Masari Archipelago]].

It is found in:

• old-growth grove networks far from coastal settlements  
• volcanic soil zones with stable moisture and mineral density  
• dense canopy environments with limited light and slow competition

The trees require:

• consistent humidity  
• nutrient-rich volcanic ground  
• long, uninterrupted growth cycles

Because of these conditions, Redwood cannot be cultivated at scale or transplanted successfully. Attempts to grow it outside Masari have failed, reinforcing its geographic exclusivity.

# Production

Masari Redwood harvesting is **deliberate, controlled, and slow**.

### Logging Practices

• only mature trees are selected  
• cutting is performed manually or with minimal-impact tools  
• surrounding grove health is preserved to prevent ecosystem collapse

Masari clans prioritize:

• long-term grove survival  
• selective harvesting over clear-cutting  
• maintaining ancestral control of logging grounds

### Output Characteristics

Production is:

• extremely limited  
• irregular in yield  
• dependent on decades-long growth cycles

Each harvested tree represents a **significant economic event**, not routine output.

---

# Processing and Transport

### Processing

Initial processing occurs near harvesting zones:

• logs are cut into manageable sections  
• bark is removed and wood is partially cured  
• select pieces are pre-shaped for transport

Further refinement occurs in:

• [[Marakai]] — finishing, carving, and grading  
• Seluqar workshops — high-end furniture production

The wood is often:

• hand-finished  
• oil-treated to enhance color depth  
• polished to reveal natural grain patterns

---

### Transport

Transport is logistically complex:

• jungle extraction routes using sleds or river channels  
• movement to coastal hubs, primarily [[Marakai]]  
• export via Seluqar-controlled shipping networks

Due to its value:

• shipments are small and heavily secured  
• high-grade pieces are often pre-sold before export

---

# Economic Importance

Masari Redwood is a **prestige material**, not a mass commodity.

It supports:

• luxury furniture industries in Seluqar  
• elite architectural projects  
• high-value artisan trades

In markets across [[Valecaryn]] and beyond, it is associated with:

• wealth  
• permanence  
• refined taste

Key reality:

👉 Masari produces the resource  
👉 Seluqar captures most of the value through refinement and trade

---

# Controlling Powers

Control is divided, but uneven.

• Masari clans control grove access and harvesting rights  
• local networks regulate internal movement  
• Seluqar merchant houses dominate export and pricing  
• Marakai authorities enforce trade flow and taxation

This creates a familiar dynamic:

> local ownership, external monetization

---

# Trade Networks

Masari Redwood follows a controlled trade path:

• jungle groves → [[Marakai]]  
• Marakai → Seluqar trade fleets  
• Seluqar → elite markets across Thalassar and [[Valecaryn]]

It is rarely available in open markets and is typically:

• commissioned  
• auctioned among elite buyers  
• traded through established merchant houses

---

# Cultural Significance

In Masari:

• Redwood is tied to **ancestral groves and long-term stewardship**  
• cutting a tree is often accompanied by ritual acknowledgment

In Seluqar and foreign markets:

• it symbolizes **refinement, permanence, and control over distant resources**  
• used in council chambers, noble estates, and diplomatic settings

Owning Masari Redwood is less about utility—and more about what it represents.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

• extremely slow regeneration rate  
• risk of overharvesting under external demand  
• ecological damage if grove systems are disrupted  
• economic imbalance between producers and exporters

Additionally:

• illegal logging and smuggling are increasing  
• loss of mature groves would take generations to recover
