# Zhilan Paper

**Resource Type:** Industrial / Cultural / Intellectual  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Kiyora]] — bamboo-rich lowlands and processing centers within the **Qinghe**, **Minghe**, and **Fenghe** river basins near [[Qinghe]], [[Minghu]], and [[Fenghai]]  
**Major Production Centers:** Pulping and paper mills in [[Minghu]] (Minghe basin), refinement houses in [[Fenghai]] (Fenghe basin), administrative distribution in [[Laoshan]] (Laohe basin)  
**Controlling Groups:** Paper-making guilds, scholarly institutions, state record offices, licensed printers and scribes  
**Primary Uses:** Writing, record-keeping, printing, art, bureaucracy, communication  
**Trade Value:** Common → Valuable (refined forms)

---

# Overview

Zhilan Paper is the primary writing and record material used throughout Kiyora, forming the foundation of its administrative, scholarly, and cultural systems. Produced primarily from processed [[Hezhu Bamboo]], it is known for its smooth texture, durability, and ability to hold ink with exceptional clarity.

Unlike rough or brittle papers found elsewhere, Zhilan is carefully refined through multiple stages of pulping, filtering, and pressing. The result is a material that is both lightweight and resilient, suitable for everything from everyday records to long-term archival documents.

Its importance extends far beyond utility. Zhilan Paper enables the preservation of knowledge, the operation of bureaucracy, and the spread of ideas. Laws, trade records, philosophical texts, and correspondence all depend on its availability and quality.

In Kiyora, paper is not simply a tool—it is infrastructure. It is the medium through which governance functions and memory is maintained.

# Geographic Distribution

Zhilan Paper production is concentrated in:

• bamboo-rich regions within the **Qinghe basin** near [[Qinghe]]  
• water-fed processing hubs along the **Minghe basin** around [[Minghu]]  
• refinement and finishing centers in the **Fenghe basin** near [[Fenghai]]

It depends on:

• abundant [[Hezhu Bamboo]] for pulp  
• clean and consistent freshwater from major river systems  
• skilled labor for refinement and finishing

These regions are ideal because:

• bamboo supply is stable and renewable  
• river basins support large-scale pulping and washing processes  
• proximity to major cities allows rapid distribution

---

# Production

Zhilan Paper production is a multi-stage process requiring precision and consistency.

---

### Pulping

• Hezhu Bamboo is cut, soaked, and broken down into fibrous pulp  
• fibers are repeatedly washed using river-fed water systems  
• impurities are removed to ensure uniform quality

---

### Sheet Formation

• pulp is suspended in water basins  
• thin layers are lifted onto framed screens  
• sheets form through controlled drainage and settling

---

### Pressing and Drying

• sheets are pressed to remove excess moisture  
• dried under controlled conditions  
• flattened and prepared for finishing

---

### Refinement

Higher-grade Zhilan Paper undergoes:

• additional filtering for finer fiber distribution  
• surface treatments to improve ink absorption  
• trimming, inspection, and grading

Production is:

• scalable at lower grades  
• increasingly skill-dependent at higher qualities

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Zhilan Paper is produced in several grades:

• **Common Zhilan** — everyday writing and record use  
• **Refined Zhilan** — smoother sheets for official documents  
• **Archival Zhilan** — treated for long-term preservation  
• **Artisan Zhilan** — used for calligraphy and fine artwork

---

### Transport

Zhilan Paper is transported:

• via river routes along the **Qinghe**, **Minghe**, and **Fenghe** systems  
• through distribution networks centered in [[Laoshan]] (Laohe basin)  
• along overland trade routes to administrative and cultural centers

It is:

• lightweight and efficient to transport  
• vulnerable to moisture if not properly stored
