# Overview

Sunveil Sandstone is a golden-orange construction stone quarried from the dry southern foothills of Thalassar, particularly around [[Dreshkul]] and the upland approaches to [[Kheled-Sar]]. Softer and more workable than basalt or deep stone, it is the material most often used to build the region’s expanding towns, trade routes, and frontier infrastructure.

Its defining characteristic is its **ease of shaping**. Blocks can be cut, carved, and fitted quickly, allowing for rapid construction and simple ornamentation even in remote settlements. While it lacks the long-term durability of harder stone, its accessibility and speed of use make it indispensable to regions where growth and movement matter more than permanence.

The stone’s warm coloration—ranging from pale gold to deep orange—gives southern settlements a distinctive, sun-washed appearance.

> It is not the stone of empires.  
> It is the stone of roads, towns, and movement.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Sunveil Sandstone forms in the dry transitional zones between Thalassar’s river systems and its southern uplands.

Major deposits occur in:

• the sun-exposed foothills surrounding [[Dreshkul]]  
• layered ridges and escarpments near [[Kheled-Sar]]  
• eroded cliff bands along the upper [[Pom River]]

These regions are characterized by:

• sedimentary layering formed from ancient river and wind deposits  
• low vegetation cover exposing quarry faces  
• mineral content that produces its signature golden-orange hue

The “veil” effect—subtle striations and color gradients—comes from fine mineral banding within the stone.

---

# Production

Sunveil Sandstone is extracted through relatively accessible quarry operations.

Methods include:

• cutting blocks along natural sediment layers  
• splitting stone using wedges rather than heavy force  
• shaping near quarry sites to reduce transport weight

Production is:

• high-volume  
• locally managed  
• less specialized than harder stone industries

Because of its softness, smaller settlements and work crews can maintain their own quarries without large-scale infrastructure.

---

# Processing and Transport

Stone is typically shaped at or near the quarry.

Processing includes:

• cutting into standardized blocks  
• rough shaping for construction use  
• finer carving for decorative elements

---

### Transport Routes

Sunveil Sandstone moves through:

• caravan routes radiating from [[Dreshkul]]  
• overland connections between [[Kheled-Sar]] and southern settlements  
• river transport along the [[Pom River]] for bulk movement

Due to its weight and relative abundance, trade is:

• regional rather than global  
• driven by proximity and demand

---

# Economic Importance

Sunveil Sandstone is a **foundational material of expansion**.

It is essential for:

• constructing new settlements  
• building trade roads and waystations  
• supporting regional growth

Its value lies not in rarity, but in:

> availability, speed, and adaptability

---

# Controlling Powers

Control is decentralized but locally important.

Primary actors include:

• quarry guilds around [[Dreshkul]]  
• regional builders and contractors  
• caravan operators managing distribution

While not heavily monopolized, control of high-quality quarry zones can influence regional development.

---

# Trade Networks

Sunveil Sandstone is distributed across southern Thalassar via:

• Dreshkul-centered caravan routes  
• Pom River transport corridors  
• regional construction supply chains

It is rarely exported beyond the region due to:

• transport cost  
• widespread availability of alternative materials elsewhere

---

# Cultural Significance

Sunveil Sandstone defines the architectural character of southern Thalassar.

It is associated with:

• sunlit, warm-toned settlements  
• carved but practical construction  
• frontier expansion and mobility

Where northern regions favor darker, heavier stone, the south builds in:

> light, warmth, and impermanence

# Dangers and Limitations

• less durable than basalt or deep stone  
• prone to erosion in exposed conditions  
• unsuitable for major fortifications or heavy load structures

Additionally:

• requires maintenance over time  
• structural failures can occur if overused in large builds
