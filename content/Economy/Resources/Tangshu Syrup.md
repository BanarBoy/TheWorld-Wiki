# Overview

Tangshu Syrup is a naturally derived sweetener produced from the sap of hardwood trees native to Kiyora’s temperate forest regions. Harvested during a narrow seasonal window, the sap is slowly reduced into a thick, amber-colored syrup known for its layered sweetness and subtle earthy depth.

While not essential like [[Lianmi]], Tangshu Syrup plays a significant role in Kiyoran cuisine. It is used to balance flavors, preserve foods, and enhance both everyday meals and refined dishes. Its ability to store well and maintain consistency makes it especially valuable in regions where fresh ingredients fluctuate seasonally.

Production is inherently limited by climate and timing. This scarcity, combined with labor-intensive processing, elevates higher-quality Tangshu into a refined trade good. In this way, it occupies a middle ground between common utility and culinary luxury.

Within Kiyora, Tangshu represents a quieter form of refinement—one rooted not in strict control like Shenhua practices, but in patience, timing, and careful observation of natural cycles.

• transitional zones between river valleys and forested highlands

They thrive in:

• climates with distinct seasonal temperature shifts

These conditions allow:

• sap to flow during early seasonal thaw periods  
• consistent long-term tree growth

# Production

Tangshu Syrup production is highly seasonal and technique-driven.

---

### Sap Collection

• trees are tapped during peak sap flow periods  
• collection occurs over several days or weeks  
• yield varies depending on weather conditions

---

### Reduction

• raw sap is boiled slowly over controlled heat  
• excess water is evaporated  
• sugars concentrate into syrup

---

### Refinement

• impurities are removed during boiling  
• syrup is filtered and clarified  
• batches are graded based on consistency and flavor

Production is:

• labor-intensive  
• time-sensitive  
• dependent on environmental stability

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Tangshu Syrup is produced in several grades:

• **Common Tangshu** — widely used, lighter flavor  
• **Refined Tangshu** — richer, smoother consistency  
• **Aged Tangshu** — deeper, more complex profile developed over time

---

### Transport

Tangshu Syrup is transported:

• in sealed ceramic or lacquered containers  
• from forest regions along the **Qinghe** and **Laohe** basins  
• to markets in [[Fenghai]] (Fenghe basin) and [[Jinshan]] (Jinghe basin)  
• along established river and overland trade routes from [[Heiwah]] and [[Hang'an]]

It is:

• stable when properly sealed  
• vulnerable to contamination if mishandled
