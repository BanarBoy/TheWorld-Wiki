# Overview

Blackgum is a dense, resinous substance derived from coastal reef organisms and salt-adapted plant life found throughout [[Sapphire Reach]]. Processed into a thick, tar-like sealant, it is widely used in ship construction and maintenance as a superior alternative to traditional pitch.

Unlike standard sealants, Blackgum retains flexibility after application. It expands and contracts with the natural movement of a ship’s hull, preventing the cracking and splitting that commonly occurs under prolonged stress. This makes it especially valuable for vessels operating in warm, high-salinity environments where conventional materials degrade quickly.

Its effectiveness is most pronounced when used in conjunction with materials such as [[Brinewood]], where it forms a durable, adaptive seal that resists both water intrusion and structural fatigue. For experienced shipwrights, Blackgum is not a luxury—it is a performance advantage.

Though widely used within [[Sapphire Reach]], its export has grown steadily as outside regions recognize its value in extending vessel lifespan and reducing maintenance demands.

# Geographic Distribution

Blackgum originates in the coastal ecosystems of [[Sapphire Reach]], particularly where marine and jungle environments intersect.

It is sourced from:

• reef systems containing resin-producing organisms  
• coastal growths exposed to constant saltwater and tidal flow  
• mangrove-like environments where organic compounds accumulate

These environments produce the dense, oil-rich compounds that give Blackgum its defining properties. The combination of heat, salinity, and biological activity is difficult to replicate elsewhere, limiting production to specific regions.

---

# Production

Production of Blackgum involves both harvesting and extraction.

• raw material is collected from reef structures or coastal growths  
• material is broken down and processed into usable resin  
• impurities are removed through heating and separation

Harvesting is:

• labor-intensive and environmentally sensitive  
• dependent on access to viable reef systems  
• often conducted by specialized crews

Production levels are moderate:

• overharvesting reduces long-term yield  
• environmental damage can disrupt supply  
• access to high-quality sources is limited

---

# Processing and Transport

Blackgum must be refined before use.

Processing includes:

• heating raw material into a workable state  
• separating usable resin from organic waste  
• stabilizing the compound for storage and transport

It is typically stored in:

• sealed barrels  
• hardened blocks for later reheating

Application requires reheating into a viscous state before use.

Transport occurs primarily by sea:

• bulk shipments move through [[Corsair’s Rest]]  
• refined, higher-grade Blackgum is exported through [[Bluewake]]

Its dense, compact form makes it efficient to transport relative to timber.

# Economic Importance

Blackgum is a critical component of maritime infrastructure.

It is essential for:

• maintaining watertight hulls  
• reinforcing structural seams  
• extending operational lifespan of vessels

Its use reduces:

• repair frequency  
• material degradation  
• long-term maintenance costs

For fleets operating across long distances, Blackgum provides a significant logistical advantage.

Control of Blackgum is decentralized but closely monitored.

• harvesting zones are tied to access to reef and coastal systems  
• processing operations are controlled by specific crews and intermediaries  
• distribution is influenced by trade networks and faction presence

Among the Five:

• [[Albrecht Lanmast]] — controls access to major processing and harbor facilities  
• [[Cirel Redmark]] — influences trade agreements and distribution  
• [[Rathen Gustmore]] — pressures extraction zones  
• [[Niamh Auldrahm]] — intervenes selectively in disputes or disruptions  
• [[Seressa Fairwind]] — maintains strategic use within her fleet

No single faction holds full control, but all maintain a vested interest.

# Trade Networks

Blackgum is exported through established maritime routes.

• [[Bluewake]] — legitimate trade and refined product  
• [[Corsair’s Rest]] — bulk movement and unregulated distribution

Key destinations include:

• [[Valecaryn]] shipyards

• independent maritime operators

Its use is most common in regions with heavy maritime traffic.

Blackgum carries little symbolic meaning, but strong practical reputation.

Among shipwrights and sailors:

• it is associated with well-maintained vessels  
• its presence indicates investment in durability  
• its absence is often noted more than its use

It is a material of function rather than display.
