# Ironwood Timber

**Resource Type:** Strategic Timber / Heavy Construction Material  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Regions/Blackspine Mountains]]  
**Major Production Centers:** [[Iron Hollow]], [[Khordun-Zhal]]  
**Controlling Groups:** Mountain foresters, dwarven timber guilds, regional trade houses  
**Primary Uses:** Fortress construction, siege engines, naval reinforcement, heavy structural beams  
**Trade Value:** Rare / Strategic

---

# Overview

Ironwood is one of the rarest and strongest natural materials found in Valecaryn. The wood is incredibly dense and durable, often described as “hard as iron,” which is the origin of its name.

Unlike the vast hardwood forests of Black Oak, Ironwood trees grow only in the harsh highland environments of the **[[Regions/Blackspine Mountains]]**. These trees grow slowly in rocky soil and thin mountain air, producing extremely dense wood that is prized for its strength and resistance to damage.

Because Ironwood is far more difficult to harvest and transport than other timber, it is rarely used for everyday construction. Instead it is reserved for projects requiring exceptional durability such as fortress gates, siege engines, naval reinforcements, and massive structural beams.

The primary center of Ironwood harvesting and trade is the mountain settlement of **[[Iron Hollow]]**, which sits along key trade routes descending from the Blackspine Mountains.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Ironwood trees grow almost exclusively in the high forests of the **[[Regions/Blackspine Mountains]]**.

The species thrives in:

• rocky mountain slopes  
• cold alpine valleys  
• high-altitude forests where other hardwoods struggle to survive

The largest known Ironwood groves exist near:

• the mountain settlement of [[Iron Hollow]]  
• remote valleys surrounding [[Khordun-Zhal]]  
• isolated ridges deep within the Blackspine range

Because the trees grow slowly and sparsely, Ironwood is significantly rarer than most other timber.

---

# Production

Harvesting Ironwood is extremely difficult due to the terrain where the trees grow.

Logging crews must travel deep into mountainous forests to locate mature Ironwood trees. Once found, the trees require specialized tools to cut because of the wood’s density.

After being felled, the logs must be transported down steep mountain slopes using sled systems, reinforced wagons, or controlled log slides.

Because of these challenges, Ironwood production is limited and carefully managed.

---

# Processing and Transport

Ironwood logs are typically transported to processing yards near **[[Iron Hollow]]**, where craftsmen cut them into usable beams and reinforced timber.

Processing includes:

• shaping heavy beams for fortress gates  
• reinforcing hull structures for warships  
• crafting structural supports for large buildings

Transporting Ironwood to major cities requires complex logistics. Caravans often move the timber through mountain passes before reaching lower trade routes that lead toward coastal ports and major construction centers.

---

# Economic Importance

Ironwood is considered one of the most valuable construction materials in Valecaryn.

Its primary uses include:

• fortress gates and defensive structures  
• siege engines and military equipment  
• reinforced naval vessels  
• major architectural projects requiring extreme durability

Because of its strength and rarity, Ironwood is often reserved for strategic or military purposes.

---

# Controlling Powers

Several groups control Ironwood harvesting and trade.

**Mountain Forester Guilds**

Local foresters responsible for managing Ironwood groves and overseeing harvesting operations.

**Dwarven Timber Guilds**

Dwarven craftsmen from settlements such as [[Khordun-Zhal]] are known for their expertise in shaping Ironwood beams.

**Mountain Trade Houses**

Merchant families who organize the transport of Ironwood from the mountains to major cities.

---

# Trade Networks

Ironwood is transported from the Blackspine Mountains to construction centers across the continent.

Major destinations include:

• fortress cities requiring defensive reinforcements  
• naval shipyards building warships  
• major urban construction projects

Because of the difficulty of transporting Ironwood, it is typically shipped in small but extremely valuable quantities.

---

# Cultural Significance

Ironwood has become associated with strength, endurance, and permanence.

Many legendary fortress gates and ancient structures built with Ironwood have stood for centuries without significant deterioration.

Among dwarven craftsmen, working with Ironwood is considered a mark of exceptional skill.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

Ironwood harvesting faces several challenges.

The trees grow slowly and sparsely, making large-scale logging difficult. Overharvesting could quickly deplete the limited groves.

The mountainous terrain also makes logging operations dangerous, as crews must work on steep slopes and transport massive logs down unstable paths.

Because of its high value, Ironwood shipments are occasionally targeted by bandits attempting to steal valuable cargo.

---

# Notable Locations

Important locations tied to Ironwood production include:

**[[Iron Hollow]]**

The primary settlement supporting Ironwood harvesting and processing operations.

**[[Khordun-Zhal]]**

A dwarven stronghold known for skilled craftsmanship and heavy timber construction.

---

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some mountain foresters claim that deeper regions of the Blackspine Mountains contain ancient Ironwood groves far larger than those currently harvested.

Others whisper that certain merchant houses may be secretly cutting trees beyond the officially sanctioned harvest zones.

There are also rumors that dwarven craftsmen possess techniques for strengthening Ironwood even further through specialized treatments.
