# Whitebridge Chalk

![[Pasted image 20260324191714.png]]

**Resource Type:** Geological Resource / Construction Material  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Stonebridge]] and surrounding chalk hills  
**Major Extraction Sites:** Whitebridge Quarries near [[Stonebridge]]  
**Controlling Groups:** Quarry guilds, lime burners, construction merchants  
**Primary Uses:** Lime mortar, plaster, whitewash, masonry work  
**Trade Value:** High / Essential Building Material

---

# Overview

Whitebridge Chalk is a pale sedimentary stone quarried from the rolling hills surrounding [[Stonebridge]]. Though softer than mountain stone, it plays an essential role in construction across Valecaryn.

When burned in large lime kilns, the chalk produces lime used to create mortar, plaster, and whitewash — materials necessary for nearly every masonry structure built throughout the continent.

Because of this, Whitebridge Chalk is one of the most widely used construction resources in Valecaryn. While heavy basalt from the [[Blackspine Mountain Foothills]] forms the structure of walls and fortifications, lime derived from Whitebridge Chalk binds the stone together and seals the surfaces of buildings.

The resource has made [[Stonebridge]] an important industrial town supplying materials to builders, masons, and royal construction projects.

---

# Geographic Origin

Whitebridge Chalk deposits lie in the low hills and exposed cliffs surrounding the settlement of [[Stonebridge]]. These chalk layers were formed millions of years ago from compressed marine sediments that accumulated when much of the region lay beneath ancient seas.

Over time, erosion exposed thick chalk strata close to the surface, making the area ideal for quarrying.

The pale cliffs and quarry faces around Stonebridge are visible from miles away and have long served as a landmark for travelers approaching the region.

---

# Extraction

Chalk is quarried in large open pits carved into the hills around Stonebridge.

Workers cut blocks directly from exposed chalk layers using chisels, saws, and heavy picks. Because chalk is much softer than basalt or granite, extraction is relatively fast compared to mountain quarrying.

Once removed from the quarry face, the chalk is broken into transportable blocks and sent either to lime kilns or directly to building sites.

---

# Processing and Lime Production

The most valuable product derived from Whitebridge Chalk is **lime**.

Chalk blocks are heated in large kilns at extremely high temperatures, producing quicklime. This material is then mixed with water and sand to create mortar and plaster used in masonry construction.

This lime mortar binds stone blocks together and allows buildings to withstand weather and time.

Because nearly every stone structure requires mortar, lime production from Whitebridge Chalk is essential to construction across Valecaryn.

---

# Economic Importance

Whitebridge Chalk supports a large regional industry centered around quarrying, lime burning, and construction supply.

The industry employs:

• quarry workers extracting chalk stone  
• kiln operators producing lime  
• merchants transporting lime and chalk  
• masons and builders across the kingdom

Large shipments of lime regularly travel to cities such as [[Aureth Caldras]], where major construction projects require enormous quantities of mortar and plaster.

---

# Trade Networks

Whitebridge Chalk and lime products move through established trade routes connecting Stonebridge to major cities.

Typical trade routes include:

Stonebridge → [[Aureth Caldras]] → royal construction projects

Stonebridge → [[Geldmere]] → merchant construction contracts

Stonebridge → regional towns and fortifications

Because mortar is needed for nearly all large buildings, demand for lime is constant.

---

# Cultural Significance

The pale color of Whitebridge Chalk has influenced the appearance of many settlements throughout the region.

Buildings coated in lime plaster often take on a bright white appearance, which helps reflect sunlight and protect walls from weather.

This has given rise to the phrase among builders:

> “Every stone stands on Whitebridge.”

---

# Notable Locations

**[[Stonebridge]]**  
The industrial town that serves as the center of chalk quarrying and lime production.

**Whitebridge Quarries**  
Large open-pit chalk quarries carved into the hills surrounding Stonebridge.

**Lime Kiln District**  
A cluster of industrial kilns near Stonebridge where chalk is burned to produce lime.

---

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some quarry workers believe deeper chalk layers beneath the hills may contain rare minerals useful for alchemical or arcane purposes.

Others fear that expanding quarry operations may destabilize nearby land or threaten older tunnels beneath the hills.

There are also occasional disputes between quarry guilds and merchants over control of lime production contracts.
