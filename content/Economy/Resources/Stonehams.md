# Overview

Stonehams are large, bone-in cuts of meat preserved through the Frostcure tradition and aged in cold, stable environments until dense, durable, and deeply flavored. Found across nearly every region of Eryndor, they are one of the most widespread and essential preserved foods on the continent.

Unlike Rimecut Strips, which are produced quickly for portability, Stonehams are made to last. They are cured slowly, aged carefully, and stored as long-term food reserves, forming the backbone of winter stores, tavern supply, and regional trade.

While methods vary by region, the defining characteristics remain consistent: heavy salt curing, cold-air aging, and a dense, sliceable structure that improves over time.

# Geographic Distribution

Stonehams are produced throughout Eryndor wherever three conditions exist:

• reliable livestock populations  
• access to **Glacier Salt**  
• cold, stable environments for aging

This includes:

• central pastoral basins  
• western and northwestern highlands  
• foothills of the [[Crown of Winter]]  
• dwarven strongholds with deep storage vaults

Because these conditions are widespread, Stonehams are:

• produced continent-wide  
• adapted to local environments  
• highly variable in character

# Production

Stoneham production follows a shared framework with regional variation.

---

### Meat Selection

• large cuts, often bone-in  
• sourced from sheep, goat, cattle, or regional livestock  
• selected for structural integrity and fat balance

---

### Curing Process

• heavily treated with **Glacier Salt**  
• cured over extended periods to draw out moisture  
• sometimes exposed to light **Frostpine smoke** depending on region

---

### Aging

• hung or stored in:

- stone vaults
- cellar chambers
- mountain storage halls

• aged for weeks to months

This produces:

• dense, durable texture  
• concentrated flavor  
• long-term stability

Production is:

• moderately scalable  
• dependent on infrastructure  
• shaped by local tradition
