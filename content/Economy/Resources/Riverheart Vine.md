---

# Overview

Riverheart Vine is a semi-aquatic medicinal plant found in the slow, silt-heavy waterways of central Thalassar, most notably within the tangled wetlands of [[Sarith Fen]]. Its hollow, buoyant vines drift partially submerged in still or low-current channels, forming dense, interwoven clusters that are carefully harvested by trained gatherers.

The vine produces a clear, latex-like sap with powerful restorative properties. Applied directly, it accelerates tissue knitting and prevents festering wounds. When prepared as a decoction, it treats intestinal parasites and waterborne illness—making it indispensable in riverine settlements and aboard long-distance vessels.

Not all Riverheart Vine is equal. Specimens harvested from the deep backwaters of Sarith Fen are considered the most potent, while those gathered from managed waterways near [[Tal Mir Fields]] are more common but less effective.

> The river gives life.  
> Riverheart decides if it is kept.

# Geographic Distribution

Riverheart Vine grows in highly specific environments where water movement is minimal and ecological balance is stable.

Primary concentrations occur in:

• the shaded, low-current channels of [[Sarith Fen]]  
• slow-flow bends and split channels around [[Zharakel]]  
• controlled irrigation waterways feeding the [[Tal Mir Fields]]

The vine requires:

• clean, oxygen-rich freshwater  
• low turbulence and consistent water levels  
• dense surrounding vegetation for shade

It does not survive in:

• fast-moving river sections  
• flood-surged channels  
• polluted or sediment-heavy runoff zones

Because of this sensitivity, Riverheart Vine acts as a natural indicator of **healthy water systems**.

---

# Production

Riverheart Vine is not cultivated—it is **selectively gathered**.

Harvesting is concentrated in [[Sarith Fen]], where experienced Fenborn clans navigate narrow, overgrown waterways to locate mature vine clusters.

The process involves:

• cutting vine lengths without uprooting the base network  
• rotating harvest zones to prevent depletion  
• identifying high-sap-yield segments by color and density

Gathering is:

• slow and skill-intensive  
• geographically limited  
• dependent on generational knowledge of Fen pathways

Tal Mir and Zharakel sources provide larger volume, but lower potency.

# Processing and Transport

The value of Riverheart Vine lies in its sap, which begins degrading almost immediately after harvest.

---

### Fresh Sap (High Value)

• extracted by scoring or cutting the vine  
• applied directly to wounds or preserved briefly  
• stored in wax-sealed banar casks for transport

Fresh sap is:

• highly effective  
• extremely perishable  
• reserved for high-priority use (healers, ships, elite buyers)

---

### Dried Form — “Zhar Healer”

• vines are dried in shaded racks  
• sap potency reduced but retained in latent form  
• reactivated through soaking or boiling

This form is:

• far more stable  
• widely traded  
• accessible to common use

---

### Preparation Methods

• sap applied directly to cuts or infections  
• mixed with banar mash for poultices  
• boiled into decoctions for intestinal illness and flux

---

# Economic Importance

Riverheart Vine is a **critical but limited medicinal resource**.

It is essential for:

• treating wounds in river and coastal communities  
• maintaining health aboard ships  
• supporting trade routes where disease risk is high

Unlike staple resources, its importance comes from:

• effectiveness over volume  
• reliability in harsh conditions  
• lack of viable substitutes

---

# Controlling Powers

Control is informal but tightly held.

Primary groups include:

• Fenborn clans of [[Sarith Fen]] controlling high-quality harvest zones  
• healer circles who regulate preparation and use  
• Seluqar traders who control long-distance distribution

Power lies in:

• access to Fen waterways  
• knowledge of harvesting  
• ability to preserve and transport sap

---

# Trade Networks

Riverheart Vine moves outward from central Thalassar through:

• river routes from [[Zharakel]]  
• caravan links from Tal Mir to inland settlements  
• maritime export via Seluqar ports

Trade forms include:

• dried bundles (“Zhar healer”) — common export  
• sealed sap casks — rare and high value

---

# Cultural Significance

Among the Fenborn, Riverheart Vine is more than medicine.

It is believed to:

• “remember” clean water  
• carry the balance of the river itself  
• restore both body and internal harmony

Use of the vine is often quiet and deliberate, reflecting respect for both the plant and the water it grows in.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

• extreme sensitivity to water quality  
• limited growth regions (primarily [[Sarith Fen]])  
• rapid degradation of fresh sap  
• inability to scale production

Additionally:

• disruption of Fen ecosystems can collapse supply  
• overharvesting weakens long-term viability
