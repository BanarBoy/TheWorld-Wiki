# Shenjin Glass

**Resource Type:** Mineral / Volcanic / Industrial / Luxury  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Kiyora]] — volcanic zones surrounding the ruins of [[Old Sylrae]], within the southern interior highlands near the **Laohe basin**  
**Major Production Centers:** Extraction camps near [[Old Sylrae]], refinement and shaping facilities in [[Laoshan]] (Laohe basin)  
**Controlling Groups:** Central state authorities of [[Jinshan]], Imperial resource bureau, licensed glassworkers, restricted research circles  
**Primary Uses:** Blades, precision tools, lenses, alchemical vessels, ceremonial objects  
**Trade Value:** Valuable → Strategic

---

# Overview

Shenjin Glass is a rare volcanic material formed in the active eruption zones surrounding the ruins of [[Old Sylrae]], a site once inhabited by the ancient Sylrae. Created when molten stone cools rapidly under extreme conditions, it produces a dense, glass-like substance capable of taking an exceptionally sharp edge while maintaining unusual clarity.

Unlike common volcanic glass, Shenjin forms in an environment of continuous geological instability. The volcano near Old Sylrae erupts regularly, renewing deposits but making extraction both dangerous and unpredictable. This results in a material that is never scarce in origin, but always limited in safe access.

What distinguishes Shenjin Glass is not only its physical properties, but its association with the ruins themselves. Artisans have observed that glass taken from deeper or older flow layers behaves differently—sharper, more stable, and easier to refine. Many believe this is due to lingering environmental effects tied to Old Sylrae, though such claims remain unverified.

Within Kiyora, Shenjin Glass is valued as a material of precision. It is used where exactness matters—fine tools, delicate instruments, and carefully crafted blades. It is not a replacement for metal, but a complement to it, occupying a niche where sharpness and refinement outweigh durability.

# Geographic Distribution

Shenjin Glass forms in:

• active lava flows surrounding [[Old Sylrae]]  
• cooling zones where molten material solidifies rapidly  
• volcanic ridges and ash-covered slopes in the region

It is found in:

• obsidian-like surface deposits  
• fractured glass fields created by repeated eruptions  
• layered formations from older volcanic activity

These conditions are unique because:

• continuous eruptions renew material supply  
• rapid cooling produces dense, uniform glass  
• proximity to Old Sylrae appears to influence material quality

The highest-quality Shenjin is often recovered from:

• older, partially buried flow layers  
• areas closer to the ruins themselves

# Production

Shenjin Glass is extracted under hazardous conditions.

---

### Extraction

• crews operate near active or recently cooled lava flows  
• glass deposits are identified and broken free from surrounding rock  
• extraction requires timing between eruptions and cooling cycles

---

### Collection

• material is gathered in irregular shards  
• larger, unfractured pieces are significantly more valuable  
• quality varies depending on formation conditions

---

### Risk Factors

• extreme heat and volcanic gases  
• unstable terrain and shifting ground  
• sudden eruptions or collapses

Production is:

• dangerous  
• inconsistent  
• tightly regulated by the state

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Raw Shenjin Glass is:

• cut using specialized techniques  
• shaped into blades, tools, or forms  
• polished for clarity or edge refinement

It is used to create:

• ultra-fine cutting implements  
• precision tools and instruments  
• optical components and lenses  
• alchemical containers resistant to contamination

Some processing methods are:

• restricted to licensed artisans  
• closely guarded as trade secrets

---

### Transport

Due to fragility and value:

• transported in padded and reinforced containers  
• moved from [[Old Sylrae]] through secured routes into the **Laohe basin**  
• refined in [[Laoshan]] before wider distribution  
• distributed through controlled state channels

Higher-grade Shenjin is:

• rarely transported in raw form  
• reserved for specific uses or clients

# Economic Importance

Shenjin Glass is a **specialized high-value resource**.

It:

• enables precision craftsmanship  
• supports advanced tools and instruments  
• contributes to Kiyora’s technological refinement

> it enhances everything built with it

# Controlling Powers

Control of Shenjin Glass is centralized under [[Jinshan]].

Primary actors include:

• **central state authorities of [[Jinshan]]** — regulate access and distribution  
• **Imperial resource bureau** — oversees extraction rights  
• **licensed glassworkers** — permitted to shape and refine the material  
• **restricted research circles** — study advanced applications

Access to raw Shenjin is:

• limited  
• monitored  
• politically controlled

# Trade Networks

• internally to state projects and licensed artisans

• in limited quantities to external markets  
• primarily as finished or semi-finished goods

Trade is:

• restricted  
• high-value  
• tightly controlled

# Cultural Significance

Shenjin Glass represents:

• refinement through danger  
• precision over strength  
• mastery of unstable forces

> shaped not only by fire—but by what once stood there

• a mark of mastery
