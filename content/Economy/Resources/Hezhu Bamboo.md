# Overview

Hezhu Bamboo is one of the most essential and widely used materials in Kiyora, forming the backbone of everyday construction, craft, and industry. Grown in dense, fast-regenerating groves along river systems and lowland regions, it provides a reliable and renewable source of structural material.

Unlike heavier timbers, Hezhu Bamboo combines strength with flexibility. It resists splitting under tension, bends without breaking under stress, and can be processed into a wide range of forms. From housing frames and scaffolding to tools, containers, and woven goods, it is present in nearly every aspect of daily life.

Its true value lies in its versatility. The same plant can be used for construction, broken down into fibers for paper, or split into strips for weaving. This makes Hezhu Bamboo not just a material, but a foundational resource that supports multiple industries simultaneously.

In contrast to refined resources like [[Shencha]] or luxury goods such as Jadefire Silk, Hezhu Bamboo represents practicality, adaptability, and sustained growth—the quiet infrastructure beneath Kiyora’s refinement.

# Production

Hezhu Bamboo is cultivated and harvested on a continuous cycle.

---

### Cultivation

• groves are planted and maintained in managed clusters  
• growth is monitored to ensure consistent yield  
• older stalks are selectively removed to promote regrowth

---

### Harvesting

• stalks are cut at maturity for optimal strength  
• harvesting is cyclical, allowing regrowth between cuts  
• improper cutting can weaken future yields

---

### Processing Preparation

• stalks are sorted by thickness and use  
• outer layers may be treated or stripped  
• material is cut into sections for transport or use

Production is:

• highly sustainable  
• scalable  
• dependent on proper grove management

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Hezhu Bamboo is processed into multiple forms:

• structural poles for construction  
• split lengths for scaffolding and frameworks  
• woven strips for baskets, mats, and containers  
• fiber pulp for paper production

Specialized processing includes:

• curing to improve durability  
• splitting and smoothing for precision use  
• pulping for fine paper materials

---

### Transport

Hezhu Bamboo is transported via:

• river systems across the **Qinghe**, **Minghe**, and **Fenghe** basins  
• canal networks connecting [[Qinghe]] and [[Minghu]]  
• overland routes to cities like [[Fenghai]] and [[Laoshan]]

Because of its abundance:

• raw bamboo is usually transported locally  
• processed forms travel further and hold higher value
