# Overview

Fangiron is a high-grade iron ore drawn from the jagged, mineral-dense formations of the [[Ironfang Peaks]]. Renowned for its density, purity, and exceptional response to refinement, it serves as the foundational material behind Eryndor’s metallurgical strength—most notably in the forging of Wintersteel.

The ore takes its name from the peaks themselves, whose serrated ridgelines resemble a field of fangs breaking through the earth. Extracting Fangiron from these formations is difficult and often dangerous, requiring deep excavation into unstable stone and narrow, pressurized veins.

Fangiron is not valuable as a finished material, but as a beginning. In raw form, it is heavy, unyielding, and unremarkable. Only through disciplined refinement does its true quality emerge.

> In the mountain, it is burden.  
> In the forge, it becomes power.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Fangiron deposits are concentrated within:

• the central ridges of the [[Ironfang Peaks]]  
• deep subsurface veins running beneath the range  
• fractured extensions into surrounding highlands

These deposits form under:

• intense geological pressure  
• mineral-rich compression zones  
• deep, stable but difficult-to-access strata

The highest-quality ore is typically found:

• at depth rather than surface level  
• in narrow, vertical or angled seams  
• in regions prone to collapse or shifting stone

While Fangiron is abundant in total, usable deposits are:

• unevenly distributed  
• dangerous to extract  
• often contested

# Production

Fangiron extraction is one of the most demanding industries in Eryndor.

---

### Mining Operations

• deep shaft mining into mountain cores  
• horizontal tunneling along narrow ore veins  
• structural reinforcement to prevent collapse  
• controlled blasting in dense formations

Mining is:

• heavily dominated by dwarven guilds in deeper operations  
• supported by human highland labor in outer zones

---

### Ore Characteristics

Fangiron ore is:

• exceptionally dense  
• high in iron content  
• low in contaminating impurities

Its defining trait:

• strong affinity for carbon during refinement

This allows it to produce:

• high-quality steels  
• stable, reliable alloys  
• superior forged materials

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Primary Processing

At extraction sites, Fangiron is:

• broken into manageable loads  
• sorted by quality and density  
• sometimes partially refined to reduce waste

---

### Major Processing Centers

Raw ore is transported to major industrial hubs for smelting and refinement:

• [[Durgrath]] — large-scale smelting and primary refinement  
• [[Kargrath]] — heavy industrial processing and bulk output  
• [[Hrothgard]] — fortified processing center near active mining zones  
• [[Thordain]] — regional distribution and mid-tier refinement hub  
• [[Brom Khazad]] — dwarven-controlled processing with emphasis on quality  
• [[Rhayne]] — lower-elevation refinement and trade-linked redistribution

These centers handle:

• impurity removal  
• initial alloy preparation  
• conversion into forge-ready material

---

### Transport

Fangiron is transported through:

• high-risk mountain caravan routes  
• fortified trade corridors  
• underground dwarven transit networks (in select regions)

Transport is:

• logistically complex  
• heavily guarded  
• a frequent target for disruption or theft

---

# Economic Importance

Fangiron is a cornerstone of Eryndor’s industrial and military economy.

It:

• underpins all large-scale metal production  
• enables the creation of Wintersteel  
• supports construction, tooling, and weapon industries

Without Fangiron:

> Eryndor’s ability to produce high-grade metal would collapse

Its importance makes it:

• tightly controlled  
• strategically protected  
• constantly in demand

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Fangiron is shared but contested.

Primary actors include:

• dwarven mining guilds controlling deep extraction  
• highland syndicates managing surface and mid-depth operations  
• city authorities overseeing processing centers  
• transport consortia controlling movement of ore

Power is determined by:

• access to productive veins  
• control of processing infrastructure  
• security of transport routes

---

# Trade Networks

Fangiron moves through a structured internal network:

• Ironfang extraction zones  
→ [[Durgrath]] / [[Kargrath]] / [[Hrothgard]] (primary processing)  
→ [[Thordain]] / [[Rhayne]] (distribution and secondary refinement)  
→ [[Khaz Drunir]] and other forges (final production, including Wintersteel)

Exports exist, but are:

• limited  
• lower-grade  
• carefully controlled

High-grade Fangiron is almost always retained within Eryndor.

# Cultural Significance

Fangiron represents:

• labor and risk  
• raw potential  
• the beginning of transformation

Among dwarves, mining Fangiron is seen as:

> confronting the mountain and taking something from it that must be earned

In highland communities, it is:

• a source of livelihood  
• a measure of endurance  
• a dangerous but respected trade

• dangerous working environments  
• heavy logistical burden

• depletion of key veins disrupts entire regions  
• over-extraction can destabilize mountain structures
