# Overview

Silverthread Zharling is a small, schooling river fish found in immense numbers across the central waterways of Thalassar. Recognizable by the bright silver streaks along its flanks, it is the most widely consumed source of protein in the region.

While individually insignificant, Zharling move in dense, coordinated schools that can be harvested in massive quantities. This makes them uniquely suited to feeding both rural populations and major urban centers, particularly along the Zhar trade corridor.

Their greatest value lies in preservation. When brined in Crownshard-derived salt and dried into **Zharling strips**, they become lightweight, long-lasting, and easily transported—forming a core provisioning food for caravans, labor forces, and maritime fleets.

> Ceyvar feeds the body.  
> Zharling keeps it working.

# Geographic Distribution

Zharling populations are not evenly distributed—they concentrate in specific ecological zones.

Primary concentrations occur in:

• the still, nutrient-heavy waters of [[Sarith Fen]] (primary spawning grounds)  
• the split-flow channels and river convergence at [[Zharakel]]  
• downstream migration routes feeding into coastal waters near [[Irathel]]

They thrive in:

• slow to moderate current zones  
• flood-expanded feeding grounds  
• silt-rich, biologically dense waters

During seasonal floods, Zharling populations surge outward from the Fen, repopulating downstream systems.

# Production

Zharling fishing is a highly organized, high-volume operation.

Primary harvesting zones:

### Sarith Fen (Spawning Harvest)

• dense schools during breeding cycles  
• controlled harvesting to avoid collapse  
• dominated by Fenborn fishing clans

---

### Zharakel (Mass Capture Hub)

• convergence point for migrating schools  
• large-scale netting operations  
• highest volume extraction zone

---

### Downstream Channels

• smaller-scale fishing  
• opportunistic harvesting  
• local consumption focus

---

Fishing methods include:

• wide-drift netting across slow channels  
• fixed trap systems in narrow flows  
• coordinated cooperative sweeps during peak movement

# Processing and Transport

Zharling must be processed rapidly after capture.

---

### Fresh Use

• sold in local markets (especially [[Zharakel]])  
• consumed grilled, stewed, or roasted

---

### Preserved Form — **Zharling Strips**

Primary processing occurs near:

• [[Zharakel]] (bulk processing)  
• coastal curing yards near [[Irathel]]

Process:

• fish are split and cleaned  
• brined using Crownshard salt  
• hung on racks for drying

Result:

• long shelf life  
• compact, high-protein ration  
• durable trade good

---

### Transport Network

Zharling moves along:

• river barges from [[Zharakel]]  
• overland routes from central Thalassar  
• maritime export through Seluqar routes

Zharling strips are a standard provisioning cargo for:

• fleets  
• caravans  
• labor operations

# Economic Importance

Zharling is the **primary protein backbone** of Thalassar.

It:

• feeds the majority of the population  
• supports river-based economies  
• sustains trade and labor systems

Its importance is not in price—but in:

> volume, reliability, and reach

---

# Controlling Powers

Zharling is broadly accessible, but key leverage points exist.

Primary actors:

• Fenborn clans controlling spawning harvest zones in [[Sarith Fen]]  
• Zharakel cooperatives controlling mass-capture operations  
• Seluqar networks controlling large-scale distribution

Control is less about ownership—and more about:

• access to key locations  
• timing of harvest  
• control of processing capacity

---

# Trade Networks

Zharling flows outward from central Thalassar:

• from [[Zharakel]] into inland trade routes  
• from [[Irathel]] into maritime export networks  
• into Valecaryn markets and beyond

Preserved Zharling is especially critical for:

• long-distance shipping  
• military provisioning  
• urban supply chains

# Dangers and Limitations

• dependence on [[Sarith Fen]] spawning cycles  
• overharvesting risks in key zones  
• vulnerability to water contamination  
• spoilage if processing is delayed

A collapse in Fen populations would:

> ripple across the entire food system
