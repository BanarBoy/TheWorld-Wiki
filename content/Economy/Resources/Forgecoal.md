# Forgecoal

**Resource Type:** Industrial / Fuel  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Blackspine Mountain Foothills]]  
**Major Production Centers:** [[Khordun-Zhal]] (deep extraction tiers), surrounding mining outposts  
**Controlling Groups:** Durnak extraction authorities, Zharg forge syndicates, Council of Foundries  
**Primary Uses:** High-temperature fuel for forges, smelting, industrial heat systems, steam pressure generation  
**Trade Value:** Valuable / Strategic

---

# Overview

Forgecoal is a dense, high-energy fuel extracted from deep seams within the [[Blackspine Mountain Foothills]], and serves as the primary power source behind the industrial output of [[Khordun-Zhal]]. Unlike common coal, Forgecoal burns hotter, longer, and more consistently, making it uniquely suited for the extreme temperatures required in Zharg forge systems.

Its defining characteristic is its **energy density**. A relatively small quantity of Forgecoal can sustain temperatures that ordinary fuels cannot reach or maintain. This allows for continuous, large-scale industrial processes without the need for constant refueling or temperature fluctuation.

While it lacks the prestige of Blackspine Steel, Forgecoal is arguably more important. Without it, the deep forges cannot operate, large-scale smelting becomes inefficient, and Khordun-Zhal’s entire production system slows dramatically.

Across [[Valecaryn]], Forgecoal is recognized as the fuel that enables:

• advanced metallurgy  
• industrial-scale production  
• sustained high-heat engineering

It is not a luxury.

> It is the foundation of modern industry.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Forgecoal occurs naturally within deep geological seams of the [[Blackspine Mountain Foothills]].

These seams are characterized by:

• extreme compression over long geological periods  
• mineral infusion from surrounding rock layers  
• proximity to geothermal heat zones

The result is a fuel source that differs significantly from standard coal deposits found elsewhere.

Attempts to locate similar deposits outside the Blackspine range have produced:

• lower-grade fuels  
• unstable burn characteristics  
• significantly reduced heat output

True Forgecoal remains geographically limited, reinforcing Khordun-Zhal’s dominance.

---

# Production

Forgecoal is extracted through deep mining operations.

• Durnak-led teams stabilize and expand extraction tunnels  
• large blocks are removed from compressed seams  
• material is transported via lift systems to mid-tier processing zones

Extraction is dangerous due to:

• structural pressure within deep seams  
• gas pockets and volatile environments  
• proximity to high-temperature zones

Production scale is massive:

• continuous mining operations  
• high labor demand  
• tightly managed output quotas

Forgecoal is one of the few resources where:

> production volume directly limits industrial capacity

---

# Processing and Transport

Once extracted, Forgecoal is:

• cut into standardized blocks or crushed into graded fuel sizes  
• dried and stabilized to ensure consistent burn rates  
• sorted based on energy density and purity

Transport methods include:

• heavy caravan shipments through mountain passes  
• bulk transport under Roskul-managed logistics networks  
• priority allocation to contracted buyers

Due to its weight and value:

• transport is slower than refined goods  
• shipments are large but infrequent  
• loss during transit is costly but less catastrophic than metal shipments

---

# Economic Importance

Forgecoal is a **core industrial resource**.

It is required for:

• Blackspine Steel production  
• Redslag formation and reprocessing  
• all major smelting and forging operations

Cities and industries dependent on Forgecoal include:

• major manufacturing centers in [[Valecaryn]]  
• weapons forges outside Khordun-Zhal  
• large-scale construction and engineering operations

While alternatives exist, they are:

• less efficient  
• more expensive in the long term  
• incapable of matching Forgecoal’s performance

Control of Forgecoal effectively determines:

> who can produce at scale—and who cannot

---

# Controlling Powers

Forgecoal production is controlled within [[Khordun-Zhal]].

Primary authorities include:

• **Durnak Extraction Authorities** — control mining operations and structural safety  
• **Zharg Forge Syndicates** — regulate allocation to internal industrial systems  
• **Council of Foundries** — sets export limits and pricing

Unlike Blackspine Steel, Forgecoal is exported more broadly—but never without control.

---

# Trade Networks

Forgecoal is distributed across:

• [[Valecaryn]]’s industrial cities  
• secondary forge centers  
• engineering and construction hubs

Trade characteristics:

• bulk contracts rather than small-scale trade  
• long-term supply agreements  
• priority given to established partners

Roskul networks handle distribution, ensuring:

• steady supply chains  
• coordination across distant regions

---

# Cultural Significance

Within Khordun-Zhal, Forgecoal represents:

> endurance through fire

It is not celebrated—but it is ever-present.

Among workers:

• it is associated with labor, heat, and survival  
• its presence defines daily life in the forges  
• shortages are feared more than conflict

It is the most common reminder that:

> the city runs on what it burns

---

# Dangers and Limitations

Forgecoal presents several risks:

• extreme heat output leading to uncontrolled fires  
• gas emissions during extraction  
• potential instability in poorly ventilated environments

Limitations include:

• heavy transport requirements  
• reliance on continuous mining  
• vulnerability of supply routes through mountain passes

A disruption in Forgecoal supply would result in:

• reduced production capacity  
• increased strain on existing systems  
• economic pressure across dependent regions
