# Overview

Goldhorn Beef is one of the most important agricultural exports of Valecaryn and forms a major component of the continent’s food supply. Produced from the large herds of [[Goldhorn Cattle]] raised across the fertile grasslands of the [[Gold Marches]], the meat is known for its rich flavor and high quality.

The vast pastures of the Gold Marches support enormous cattle herds, allowing ranchers to produce beef on a scale unmatched in most other regions of the world. As a result, Goldhorn Beef is consumed widely across Valecaryn and exported through merchant networks to foreign markets.

Caravans regularly transport cattle and processed meat from the Gold Marches to major cities and port hubs, where merchants distribute the product across trade routes and maritime shipping lanes.

---

# Geographic Origin

Goldhorn Beef originates primarily from the ranchlands of the [[Gold Marches]], a region known for its fertile soil, abundant grazing land, and strong agricultural traditions.

The wide grasslands of the Marches allow ranchers to maintain enormous herds of cattle, while nearby rivers and seasonal rainfall provide reliable water sources for livestock.

The region’s combination of open pasture and established ranching culture has made it the center of cattle production in Valecaryn.

---

# Production

Goldhorn cattle are raised across large ranchlands and open pastures throughout the Gold Marches.

When cattle reach maturity, they are either driven to livestock markets or slaughtered locally for processing. The meat is then prepared in several forms depending on destination and demand.

Common preparations include:

• fresh cuts sold in nearby cities  
• salted or smoked beef for long-distance trade  
• preserved meat used as ship provisions  
• cured meats and sausages produced by regional butchers

These processing methods allow the beef to be transported over long distances without spoiling.

---

# Processing and Storage

Beef intended for local markets is usually sold fresh, while export shipments are preserved using salting, drying, or smoking techniques.

Salted beef barrels are especially common among merchant ships and naval fleets, as the preserved meat can remain edible for long voyages.

Large storage facilities in cities such as [[Sarnhold]] and [[Geldmere]] handle much of the region’s meat trade before it is distributed further.

---

# Economic Importance

Goldhorn Beef is one of the largest agricultural commodities produced in Valecaryn.

The cattle industry supports a wide range of economic activities including:

• ranching communities across the Gold Marches  
• livestock traders and cattle drivers  
• butchers and meat processors  
• merchant houses transporting preserved meat

Because of the scale of production, the cattle industry forms a crucial foundation of the continent’s agricultural economy.

---

# Trade Networks

Beef from the Gold Marches moves along several major trade routes.

Typical routes include:

Gold Marches → [[Sarnhold]] → [[Aureth Caldras]] → regional markets

Gold Marches → [[Geldmere]] → coastal shipping routes

Preserved beef is often exported by ship from port cities, where it is sold in foreign markets.

---

# Cultural Significance

Beef is a central component of everyday cuisine throughout Valecaryn. Meals featuring roasted or stewed beef are common in both rural and urban households.

Among ranching communities in the Gold Marches, cattle herding and livestock management are deeply rooted traditions passed down through generations.

Large cattle markets and seasonal livestock drives remain important social and economic events in the region.

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some merchants claim that rival regions are attempting to establish competing cattle industries to challenge the dominance of the Gold Marches.

Others worry that drought or disease could threaten the massive herds that sustain Valecaryn’s beef supply.

Occasional disputes also arise between ranchers over grazing land and water access.
