# Overview

Zharvein Wheat is a tall, flood-tolerant grain cultivated along the central waterways of Thalassar. Unlike conventional wheat, it develops deep, anchoring root systems that stabilize it in silt-heavy soils and allow it to survive seasonal flooding.

The grain produces a dense, slightly sweet flour with a faint golden hue, prized for its durability and consistency. Zharvein bread is heavier than most, but keeps longer and sustains better—making it ideal for laborers, travelers, and maritime provisioning.

Grown across multiple regions rather than a single zone, Zharvein Wheat forms a **distributed agricultural system**, ensuring consistent output even when individual areas are disrupted.

> Where ceyvar endures,  
> Zharvein sustains.

# Production

Zharvein Wheat is cultivated through a mix of controlled agriculture and adaptive farming.

### Tal Mir System

• structured irrigation channels  
• planned planting cycles  
• highest yield per acre

---

### Riverbank Farming

• opportunistic planting along receding floodwaters  
• natural fertilization through silt deposits  
• variable but efficient

---

### Fen-Edge Cultivation

• planted in marginal ground  
• slower growth, higher resilience  
• used as supply insurance

---

Its defining advantage:

> it survives where other grains fail

# Processing and Transport

Zharvein Wheat is processed into flour near major collection hubs.

### Processing Centers

• [[Zharakel]] — primary milling and storage  
• Tal Mir — local milling for internal use  
• Tarzhir — bulk grain consolidation

---

### Products

• coarse flour (common use)  
• refined flour (urban and export markets)  
• ration loaves and flatbreads

---

### Transport Network

Zharvein flows outward through:

• river barges from [[Zharakel]]  
• overland routes from [[Tarzhir]]  
• maritime export via Seluqar-controlled ports

It is one of the most widely exported food goods from Thalassar.
