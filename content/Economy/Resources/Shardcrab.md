# Overview

Shardcrab is a hardy, coldwater crustacean found along the coasts of Eryndor, known for its thick, jagged shell and dense, flavorful meat. Its name comes from the sharp, shard-like ridges along its carapace, which make handling it difficult but also provide natural protection in the turbulent coastal waters it inhabits.

Unlike passive shellfish such as mussels, Shardcrab must be actively caught, typically through trapping or line capture in deeper tidal zones. This makes it a more labor-intensive and valuable resource, often forming the backbone of small-scale coastal fishing economies.

Its meat is firm, slightly sweet, and holds well in cooking, making it a staple of coastal cuisine and a frequent feature in tavern fare across Eryndor’s port towns.

> It is not gathered.  
> It is taken.

# Geographic Distribution

Shardcrab is found along:

• rocky coastal shelves and submerged stone fields  
• deep tidal channels and cold coastal waters  
• reef-like formations and broken seabeds

Major concentrations exist around:

• [[Stormhaven]] — exposed coasts with strong tidal movement  
• [[Frostgate]] — deep, cold harbor waters  
• [[Threewaters]] — mixed estuarine and coastal zones

They thrive in:

• cold, oxygen-rich waters  
• areas with strong currents  
• environments with abundant cover and structure

# Production

Shardcrab harvesting is an active and skill-based fishery.

---

### Catching Methods

• baited traps lowered into tidal zones  
• weighted lines in deeper waters  
• hand collection in shallow rocky areas (less common)

Fishing is:

• physically demanding  
• dependent on weather and tides  
• often conducted in small crews

---

### Yield

Shardcrabs provide:

• high-density meat per catch  
• more value per unit than shellfish  
• consistent but variable yields depending on conditions

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Shardcrabs are:

• boiled or steamed shortly after capture  
• broken down into sections for easier use  
• sometimes picked for meat and stored separately

They are used in:

• stews and broths  
• roasted or cracked dishes  
• tavern meals and communal cooking

---

### Preservation

Limited preservation methods include:

• light salting  
• cold storage  
• short-term packing

However, they are primarily:

> consumed fresh or near-coastal

---

### Transport

Shardcrab is transported:

• short to medium distances inland  
• along coastal trade routes  
• packed for durability compared to softer seafood

This makes it:

• more tradable than mussels  
• still regionally bound
