# Overview

Sural Zest is a bright, aromatic spice derived from the dried peel of the sural fruit, a small, thick-skinned citrus grown in the warm groves of [[Jaspar]] and [[Kalore]]. Known for its sharp, fragrant profile, it provides the essential counterbalance to the region’s heavier culinary elements—cutting through oil, spice, and dense grain with clean, citrus clarity.

Unlike fresh citrus, which is limited by shelf life, Sural Zest is processed into a stable, transportable form that retains its intensity over long periods. When dried and prepared correctly, it delivers a concentrated burst of brightness, often used at the final stage of cooking to lift and define a dish.

Its importance lies not in dominance, but in precision. A small amount of Sural Zest can transform a dish, making it one of the most carefully applied and widely respected components in regional cuisine.
