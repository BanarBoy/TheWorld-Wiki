# Vireline (Saltroot)

**Resource Type:** Maritime / Industrial  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Sapphire Reach]]  
**Major Production Centers:** [[Corsair’s Rest]], [[Bluewake]]  
**Controlling Groups:** Fleets of [[Seressa Fairwind]], [[Albrecht Lanmast]], [[Rathen Gustmore]], [[Cirel Redmark]], [[Niamh Auldrahm]] (distributed control)  
**Primary Uses:** Rigging, ropes, netting, tension lines, sail control systems  
**Trade Value:** Strategic

---

# Overview

Vireline, known locally in [[Sapphire Reach]] as _saltroot_, is a fibrous material derived from coastal jungle vines that thrive in salt-heavy environments. Processed into rope and line, it is prized for its durability, flexibility, and resistance to rot in maritime conditions.

Unlike conventional rope materials, Vireline maintains strength under constant exposure to moisture and salt air. It resists fraying, holds tension more reliably, and retains a slight natural elasticity that allows it to absorb stress without snapping. These properties make it particularly valuable for rigging, where failure can be catastrophic.

The name “Vireline” originates from [[Valecaryn]] sailors, who came to associate the material with ships operating along the Virelois routes. Over time, the term became standardized in trade, while “saltroot” remains the common name among those who harvest and process it.

As with other key resources from [[Sapphire Reach]], Vireline has become an integral part of maritime economies. Its use is not universal, but among experienced captains and shipwrights, it is considered a significant advantage.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Saltroot grows in the coastal jungles of [[Sapphire Reach]], particularly in areas where dense vegetation meets salt-laden air.

It is most commonly found:

• along tidal edges and coastal ridges  
• in humid, low-elevation jungle zones  
• in areas exposed to regular sea spray

The plant develops thick, fibrous strands as a response to its environment. Constant exposure to moisture and salt conditions strengthens the fibers, giving them their distinctive durability.

Attempts to cultivate saltroot outside these environments have proven largely unsuccessful, limiting its availability to specific coastal regions.

---

# Production

Saltroot is harvested manually from jungle growth.

• vines are cut, stripped, and separated into usable fibers  
• fibers are cleaned and bundled for processing  
• harvesting crews operate in difficult and often hazardous terrain

Production requires:

• knowledge of viable growth zones  
• careful extraction to preserve regrowth  
• skilled handling to avoid damaging usable fiber

Scale of production is moderate:

• overharvesting reduces yield  
• growth is regionally limited  
• expansion inland reduces quality

Most harvesting is carried out by small crews aligned with larger factions or operating under protection.

---

# Processing and Transport

Raw saltroot must be processed before it becomes usable line.

Processing includes:

• drying and curing fibers  
• twisting and braiding into rope  
• treatment to stabilize and preserve flexibility

Quality varies significantly depending on technique.

Transport is primarily maritime:

• raw fiber is moved to coastal processing points  
• finished Vireline is exported through [[Bluewake]]  
• bulk or unregulated shipments move through [[Corsair’s Rest]]

Due to its flexibility and lower weight compared to timber, Vireline is easier to transport in volume.

---

# Economic Importance

Vireline is a critical material in maritime industries.

It is essential for:

• rigging systems  
• sail control  
• cargo securing  
• fishing and netting

Its durability reduces failure rates and maintenance demands, particularly on long voyages.

While not required for all vessels, its use provides a measurable advantage, making it highly desirable among experienced crews and high-value operations.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Vireline is decentralized.

• harvesting zones fall under shifting territorial influence  
• processing operations are tied to specific crews or brokers  
• distribution is influenced heavily by intermediaries

Among the Five:

• [[Cirel Redmark]] — influences distribution and trade agreements  
• [[Albrecht Lanmast]] — controls access to major processing and export points  
• [[Rathen Gustmore]] — exerts pressure over harvesting regions  
• [[Niamh Auldrahm]] — intervenes selectively when control is contested  
• [[Seressa Fairwind]] — maintains strategic use within her fleet

No single power dominates the resource, but all maintain interest in its flow.

---

# Trade Networks

Vireline is exported widely through maritime trade routes.

Primary channels:

• [[Bluewake]] — formal export and merchant trade  
• [[Corsair’s Rest]] — bulk movement and informal distribution

Key destinations:

• [[Valecaryn]] shipyards and merchant fleets  
• independent maritime operators  
• coastal regions lacking reliable rope materials

The material is especially associated with ships operating along the Virelois, reinforcing its trade name.

---

# Cultural Significance

Among locals, saltroot is viewed as a practical resource rather than a symbol.

Among sailors, however, Vireline carries a reputation:

• reliability under stress  
• reduced risk of catastrophic failure  
• association with well-maintained vessels

Experienced crews often recognize its presence immediately, and its use can indicate a ship of higher quality or preparation.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

• harvesting is physically demanding and dangerous  
• overharvesting can degrade local supply  
• quality varies depending on processing  
• inferior imitations are common in trade

Additionally:

• improper curing weakens the fiber  
• poorly made line can fail under load

Reliability depends heavily on craftsmanship.
