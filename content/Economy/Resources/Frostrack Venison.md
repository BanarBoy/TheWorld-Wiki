# Overview

Frostrack Venison is a premium preserved meat produced from highland deer hunted in the cold forests and ridgewood regions of Eryndor. Known for its deep flavor, lean structure, and exceptional preservation qualities, it represents the highest refinement of the Frostcure tradition.

The name derives from the practice of tracking deer across frost-hardened ground, where skilled hunters read subtle signs left in ice, wind, and snow. Only animals taken cleanly and processed immediately are considered suitable for Frostrack production.

Unlike common preserved meats, Frostrack Venison is not made for necessity—it is made for precision. The resulting product is dense, dark, and intensely flavored, with a clean finish shaped by Glacier Salt, cold air, and often a light touch of Frostpine smoke.

> It is not hunted in haste.  
> It is not cured without purpose.

# Production

Frostrack Venison production is highly selective and skill-dependent.

---

### Hunting

• conducted by experienced trackers  
• relies on reading frost patterns, tracks, and environmental signs  
• prioritizes clean, efficient kills to preserve meat quality

Hunting is:

• seasonal  
• regulated in many regions  
• limited by available game

---

### Preparation

• meat is processed immediately after harvest  
• carefully trimmed to remove excess fat  
• divided into specific cuts suited for curing

---

### Frostcure Process

• treated with **Glacier Salt**  
• hung in cold, circulating air  
• optionally exposed to light **Frostpine smoke**

The process emphasizes:

• precision  
• timing  
• environmental control

The result is:

• dense, dark meat  
• concentrated flavor  
• long shelf stability
