# Overview

Mirebloom is a pale, soft-petaled aquatic flower cultivated in the slow waters and saturated soils of the region’s river basins and coastal marshlands. Known for its powerful calming and sedative properties, it is one of the most essential medicinal resources in the subcontinent’s structured healing practices.

When properly prepared, Mirebloom induces a controlled state of physical stillness and mental calm, allowing for pain suppression, restorative sleep, and medical intervention. Its effects are consistent when handled by trained practitioners, making it indispensable in surgical procedures and long-term treatment.

However, Mirebloom is not inherently safe. Its potency varies based on growing conditions and preparation, and improper dosing can result in deep unconsciousness or dangerous suppression of bodily function. For this reason, its cultivation, refinement, and use are tightly regulated, and its handling is considered a practiced discipline rather than common knowledge.

# Geographic Distribution

Mirebloom grows across three primary environments:

---

### River Lowlands

• slow-moving basins of the [[Ravasha River Lowlands]]  
• controlled floodplains near [[Devora]]

These areas produce:

• stable, predictable Mirebloom  
• consistent potency  
• the majority of medical-grade supply

---

### Managed Wetland Systems

• irrigated basins and enclosed cultivation zones  
• shallow water fields maintained by cultivator collectives

These environments allow:

• controlled growth conditions  
• standardized harvest cycles  
• reliable large-scale production

---

### Coastal Marshes (Sarajpur → Talgarh)

• brackish wetlands and tidal flats between [[Sarajpur]] and [[Talgarh]]  
• estuarine zones influenced by seasonal monsoon flooding

This coastal strain is:

• more mineral-rich  
• slightly stronger in effect  
• less stable without careful processing

# Production

Mirebloom production is a controlled agricultural process.

---

### Cultivation

• grown in shallow, still-water environments  
• water flow is carefully regulated  
• plants are spaced to maintain quality and prevent contamination

---

### Harvesting

• flowers are collected at peak bloom  
• harvesting is done by hand  
• timing directly affects potency and effect

---

### Processing

• petals are dried or processed fresh depending on use  
• preparation methods determine strength and stability  
• coastal variants often require additional refinement

Production is:

• seasonal  
• labor-intensive  
• dependent on water control
