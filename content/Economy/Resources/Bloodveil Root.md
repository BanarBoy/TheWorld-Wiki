**Resource Type:** Medicinal / Agricultural

**Major Production Centers:** [[Marakai]], [[Ilvara]], deep jungle harvesting camps  
**Controlling Groups:** Jungle shamans, Masari harvest clans, frontier gatherers, Seluqar apothecary guilds  
**Primary Uses:** Wound treatment, fever reduction, pain relief, medicinal powders  
**Trade Value:** Rare

---

# Overview

Bloodveil Root is a dense, deep-growing taproot found in shaded jungle clearings across the [[Masari Archipelago]] and the outer jungle frontiers surrounding [[Ilvara]]. When cut, the root releases a vivid red sap that stains everything it touches—an unmistakable sign of its potency and value.

This sap possesses remarkable medicinal properties. It can rapidly staunch bleeding, reduce fever, and dull pain, making it indispensable for both jungle survival and organized medicine. Its effectiveness in treating trauma and tropical illness has made it a critical component of naval and expeditionary supply systems throughout Thalassar.

Unlike most Masari exports, Bloodveil cannot be easily standardized or scaled. Its value lies not only in the plant itself, but in the knowledge required to find, harvest, and prepare it safely. As a result, while Seluqar dominates its refinement and distribution, it remains fundamentally dependent on Masari expertise it cannot fully control.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Bloodveil Root grows in:

- shaded jungle clearings within the [[Masari Archipelago]]
- humid understory regions near [[Ilvara]]
- dense, low-light environments with rich organic soil

It requires:

- constant humidity
- minimal direct sunlight
- deep, undisturbed soil

The most potent roots are typically found in older, untouched jungle zones where growth cycles remain uninterrupted.

---

# Production

Bloodveil Root is not cultivated—it is **harvested from the wild**.

Production involves:

- locating mature plants within dense jungle environments
- carefully excavating deep taproots without damaging the core
- extracting sap quickly before degradation

Key production zones:

- Masari interior jungles feeding into [[Marakai]]
- frontier harvesting regions near [[Ilvara]]

Harvesting is:

- dangerous and labor-intensive
- dependent on experienced gatherers
- limited by environmental conditions and plant scarcity

Knowledge of viable growth zones and safe extraction methods is closely guarded by jungle shamans and local harvest clans, preventing large-scale external control.

---

# Processing and Transport

Bloodveil must be processed rapidly to retain potency.

Processing methods include:

- mashing fresh root into wound paste
- boiling into medicinal decoctions
- drying and grinding into powder

A common preparation mixes the paste with **Crownshard of Ashur** to improve purity and preservation.

Transport methods:

- small, high-value shipments from jungle camps to [[Marakai]] and [[Ilvara]]
- maritime export through Seluqar-controlled routes
- distribution to apothecaries and naval supply chains

Because of its instability and value, Bloodveil is typically transported in sealed, carefully measured forms.

---

# Economic Importance

Bloodveil Root is a **critical medical resource** despite its low volume.

It is essential for:

- battlefield and shipboard trauma treatment
- management of fever and tropical disease
- advanced apothecary practices

Key reality:

- Seluqar depends heavily on Bloodveil for naval medicine
- Masari and Verdant regions control its origin and knowledge

This creates a rare imbalance:

👉 Seluqar controls distribution  
👉 Masari controls access

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Bloodveil is divided—but not equal:

- jungle shamans and Masari clans control knowledge and harvesting
- frontier gatherers expand into new regions near [[Ilvara]]
- Seluqar apothecary guilds control refinement, branding, and export

Seluqar cannot fully monopolize Bloodveil, as it lacks the ecological knowledge required to locate and harvest it reliably.

---

# Trade Networks

Bloodveil moves through specialized and constrained routes:

- harvested in Masari and Verdant jungle zones
- transported to [[Marakai]] and [[Ilvara]]
- shipped through Seluqar-controlled maritime networks

From Seluqar, it is exported as:

- refined medicinal paste
- powdered treatments
- “Masari bloodstop” used in naval and military contexts

Supply remains inherently unstable due to its dependence on skilled harvesters rather than scalable production.

---

# Cultural Significance

Bloodveil Root carries deep spiritual meaning.

Among jungle communities:

- it is believed to carry ancestral blood memory
- improper or excessive use is said to cause visions or “hearing ghosts”

Its use is often ritualized in:

- healing ceremonies
- rites of survival

Outside the jungle, it is both feared and revered—a medicine that borders on the mystical.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

- limited and fragile natural distribution
- dangerous harvesting conditions
- rapid degradation if improperly processed
- dependence on specialized knowledge

Improper use may lead to severe side effects, including hallucinations or prolonged disorientation.
