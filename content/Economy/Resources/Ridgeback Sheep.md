# Overview

Ridgeback Sheep are the primary pastoral livestock of Eryndor’s highland regions, bred for survival in harsh, wind-exposed environments. Stocky, sure-footed, and covered in dense, weather-resistant wool, they are uniquely suited to grazing along rocky ridgelines and thin-soil pastures where other animals struggle to survive.

They are named for the pronounced ridge along their spine—both a physical trait and a reflection of the terrain they inhabit. Unlike lowland breeds, Ridgebacks are not raised for speed or size, but for resilience, consistency, and adaptability.

Nearly every aspect of the animal is used. Their wool produces Grimfleece textiles, their milk feeds regional cheesemaking traditions, and their meat forms a core input for Frostcure systems.

> They do not thrive.  
> They endure—and that is enough.

# Geographic Distribution

Ridgeback Sheep are found across:

• exposed ridge systems and upland pastures  
• foothills of the [[Crown of Winter]]  
• western and central highland regions

These environments are defined by:

• strong, constant winds  
• sparse vegetation  
• rocky, uneven terrain

Ridgebacks are uniquely adapted to:

• graze on low, hardy vegetation  
• navigate steep or unstable ground  
• withstand prolonged cold exposure

Because of this, they are:

• widespread across Eryndor  
• essential to marginal agricultural zones

# Husbandry and Production

Ridgeback herding is a seasonal and labor-intensive practice.

---

### Herding Practices

• free-range grazing along ridge systems  
• seasonal movement between summer and winter pastures  
• reliance on natural terrain rather than enclosed fields

Herders prioritize:

• herd stability over rapid growth  
• survival rates over yield  
• long-term sustainability

---

### Outputs

Ridgeback Sheep provide multiple critical resources:

• **Grimfleece wool** — dense, insulating textile fiber  
• **Milk** — used in cheeses such as Rimewheel  
• **Meat** — processed into Rimecut Strips and Stonehams

This makes them:

> one of the most economically efficient animals in Eryndor

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Wool

• sheared seasonally  
• cleaned and processed into Grimfleece  
• transported to textile centers

---

### Dairy

• processed locally into cheese  
• rarely transported as raw milk

---

### Meat

• consumed locally or preserved via Frostcure  
• transported as Rimecut or Stoneham

Because Ridgebacks are often raised in remote areas:

• most processing occurs near production sites  
• transport focuses on finished goods rather than raw materials

# Economic Importance

Ridgeback Sheep are a cornerstone of Eryndor’s rural economy.

They:

• sustain highland populations  
• support textile industries (Grimfleece)  
• feed preservation systems (Frostcure)

Their adaptability allows communities to:

> survive in regions where conventional agriculture fails
