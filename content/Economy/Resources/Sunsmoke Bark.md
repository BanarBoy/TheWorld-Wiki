# Overview

Sunsmoke Bark is a refined botanical product derived from the inner bark of a slow-growing Masari hardwood, most commonly the **Masari Redwood**. Unlike raw agricultural goods, Sunsmoke Bark is defined as much by its **processing technique** as by the tree itself.

The bark is carefully harvested, cured, and then smoked using controlled, low-heat fires fueled by select island woods. This process produces its distinctive **bitter-citrus profile**, combining sharp aromatic notes with a deep, lingering warmth.

Highly versatile, Sunsmoke Bark is shaved or ground into powders used in sauces, broths, and beverages. It has become especially prized as an additive in bitter drinks—most notably in coffee preparations—where it enhances complexity without overpowering the base flavor.

> Sunsmoke Bark is not simply grown.  
> It is _made_.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Sunsmoke Bark production is limited to the [[Masari Islands]], where the native hardwoods thrive in:

• warm, humid coastal climates  
• mineral-rich volcanic soils  
• dense, mixed forest ecosystems

The Masari Redwood and related species grow slowly in these environments, producing dense, resin-rich inner bark ideal for processing.

Attempts to cultivate similar trees outside the islands have consistently failed to replicate the same flavor profile, likely due to differences in soil composition, climate, and native growth conditions.

---

# Production

Production begins with selective harvesting of the inner bark.

Key steps include:

• careful stripping of bark without killing the tree  
• rotational harvesting cycles to preserve long-term yield  
• selection of mature trees for optimal flavor density

The raw bark is then:

• cleaned and trimmed  
• cut into strips or thin sheets  
• prepared for curing and smoking

Harvesting is controlled and deliberate, as over-harvesting can permanently damage groves.

---

# Processing and Transport

Processing is what defines Sunsmoke Bark.

### Smoking Process

• bark is dried under controlled conditions  
• then placed in low-heat smokehouses  
• smoked over specific wood blends unique to each region

The smoking process can last:

• several days to weeks

This stage develops:

• its citrus-bitter complexity  
• its deep, aromatic finish  
• subtle regional variations in flavor

After smoking:

• bark is hardened and stabilized  
• shaved into flakes or ground into powder  
• packaged for trade

---

### Transport

Sunsmoke Bark is exported via:

• coastal shipping routes  
• Seluqar-managed maritime trade networks

Due to its low weight and high value, it is an ideal export commodity.

# Controlling Powers

Control of Sunsmoke Bark is decentralized but culturally guarded.

Primary control lies with:

• Masari grower-clans managing forest groves  
• smokehouse guilds overseeing processing techniques  
• regional producers maintaining proprietary smoking methods

Seluqar trade networks handle large-scale distribution, but do not control production.
