# Overview

Redwake, known locally in [[Sapphire Reach]] as _reef blood_, is a deep red dye derived from coastal reef organisms found in the warm, shallow waters surrounding the island chain. It is prized for its intensity, durability, and resistance to fading under prolonged exposure to sun and salt.

Unlike most dyes, Redwake binds exceptionally well to treated sailcloth and maritime fabrics. It retains its color even after extended voyages, making it particularly valuable for ships that require visible identification at distance. This has led to its widespread use in sails, banners, and signal markings.

The trade name “Redwake” originates from [[Valecaryn]] sailors, who associated the color with ships operating along the Virelois routes. Over time, the name became standard in trade, while “reef blood” remains the term used by those who harvest and process the material.

Though not essential for ship function, Redwake carries both practical and symbolic value. Its use signals preparation, investment, and often affiliation.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Reef blood is sourced from marine organisms found in the coastal waters of [[Sapphire Reach]].

It is most commonly harvested:

• along shallow reef systems  
• in warm, clear coastal waters  
• in areas with stable tidal flow

These organisms thrive in environments with high sunlight exposure and nutrient-rich currents, which contribute to the richness of the pigment.

The material cannot be replicated inland, and similar organisms elsewhere do not produce the same color stability.

---

# Production

Harvesting reef blood is a marine-based process.

• divers collect reef organisms by hand  
• harvesting is time-sensitive and physically demanding  
• overharvesting can damage reef systems and reduce yield

Production is limited by:

• accessibility of reef zones  
• environmental conditions  
• the need to preserve long-term viability

Most harvesting is conducted by specialized crews familiar with local waters.

---

# Processing and Transport

Raw reef material must be processed quickly to preserve its pigment.

Processing includes:

• extraction of dye compounds  
• concentration and stabilization  
• preparation for application to fabric

The resulting dye is:

• thick and highly pigmented  
• applied through repeated treatment rather than single immersion

Transport occurs primarily through:

• [[Bluewake]] for legitimate trade  
• [[Corsair’s Rest]] for bulk or unregulated movement

Due to its compact form, Redwake is easier to transport than timber or fiber resources.

---

# Economic Importance

Redwake occupies a unique position between utility and luxury.

It is important for:

• sail visibility and identification  
• durable coloration in maritime conditions  
• signaling and recognition at sea

While not required for ship operation, its use provides:

• practical advantages in identification  
• increased prestige among crews and captains

Merchant fleets, private operators, and pirate crews all make use of Redwake to varying degrees.

# Controlling Powers

Control over Redwake is diffuse.

• harvesting zones are tied to access to reef systems  
• processing is handled by specialized crews and intermediaries  
• distribution is influenced by trade brokers and fleet interests

Among the Five:

• [[Cirel Redmark]] — influences distribution and trade agreements  
• [[Albrecht Lanmast]] — controls access to major export points  
• [[Rathen Gustmore]] — pressures harvesting operations  
• [[Niamh Auldrahm]] — intervenes selectively when disputes arise  
• [[Seressa Fairwind]] — uses Redwake for fleet identification and signaling

No single faction dominates the resource, but all maintain a presence in its movement.

---

# Trade Networks

Redwake is exported through established maritime routes.

Primary channels:

• [[Bluewake]] — refined and legitimized trade  
• [[Corsair’s Rest]] — bulk and informal distribution

Key destinations include:

• [[Valecaryn]] merchant fleets  
• naval and private maritime operators  
• distant ports seeking durable dyes

Its association with the Virelois has reinforced its trade identity.

---

# Cultural Significance

Within [[Sapphire Reach]], reef blood is seen as a practical material tied to the sea.

Among sailors, Redwake carries stronger meaning:

• ships bearing Redwake sails are easily recognized  
• color can signal affiliation, reputation, or intent  
• its presence often indicates a well-maintained vessel

Certain captains and fleets are associated with specific shades or patterns, making Redwake part of maritime identity.

# Dangers and Limitations

• overharvesting can damage reef ecosystems  
• production is limited by environmental conditions  
• improper processing reduces color stability  
• inferior substitutes are common in trade

Additionally:

• large-scale use is costly  
• color intensity can vary depending on source and method
