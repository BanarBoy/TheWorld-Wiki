# Overview

Crestpine is a rare high-altitude conifer native to the upper ridgelines of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]], growing in thin soils and constant wind exposure far above most habitable zones. These trees rise tall and unnaturally straight, their growth shaped by relentless mountain winds and slow, decades-long development.

The wood itself is pale, straight-grained, and exceptionally strong for its weight. Its natural resin content renders it highly resistant to water, rot, and insects, while still allowing it to be worked and polished to a near mirror finish. This unique combination of strength, flexibility, and durability makes Crestpine one of the most valuable construction timbers in Thalassar.

Though it has many uses, Crestpine’s greatest importance lies in shipbuilding. Seluqar’s naval dominance depends heavily on Crestpine spars and masts, which allow for taller rigs, greater sail spread, and superior maneuverability. Control over this resource directly translates into control over the seas.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Crestpine grows exclusively in:

- the highest ridgelines of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]]
- wind-exposed slopes above [[Kelresh]]
- remote, difficult-to-access mountain zones

It requires:

- extreme elevation
- constant wind stress
- thin, mineral-poor soil

These harsh conditions slow growth dramatically, resulting in:

- dense, tightly packed grain
- exceptional structural integrity
- naturally straight trunks with minimal warping

Crestpine does not grow in lowlands or controlled forests, making it inherently limited.

---

# Production

Crestpine logging is dangerous and highly specialized.

Harvesting involves:

- identifying suitable trees years in advance
- marking them for future cutting
- carefully felling trees on steep, unstable terrain

Production centers include:

- highland logging zones above [[Kelresh]]
- processing routes through [[Kheled-Sar]]
- heavy-lift operations supported by [[Dreshkul]] engineering

Due to terrain constraints:

- trees are often lowered manually or via controlled slides
- specialized crews operate in extreme conditions
- output is limited and tightly managed

Crestpine cannot be mass-produced—only carefully harvested.

---

# Processing and Transport

Once felled, Crestpine must be processed quickly but carefully.

Processing includes:

- stripping and curing to preserve resin integrity
- shaping into long, unbroken spars or beams
- polishing for high-end applications

Transport is a major challenge:

- logs are moved down mountain slopes to [[Kheled-Sar]]
- river transport via the [[Kheled Flow]] or [[Veldryn River]]
- final shipment through [[Houjoum]] or overland toward the [[Glasswater Reach]]

Seluqar-controlled systems handle final refinement and export.

---

# Economic Importance

Crestpine is a **strategic resource**.

It is essential for:

- Seluqar naval construction
- long-distance maritime trade
- high-performance structural engineering

Key dependencies:

- [[Seluqar]] relies on it for ship masts and fleet superiority
- shipyards across Thalassar depend on limited supply
- elite construction projects require it for load-bearing structures

Its scarcity and importance make it one of the most tightly controlled resources in the region.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Crestpine is layered:

- highland logging guilds manage harvesting rights
- Spine wardens regulate access to high-altitude forests
- dwarven engineers in [[Dreshkul]] assist in extraction logistics
- Seluqar merchant and naval powers control large-scale contracts and export

Seluqar effectively maintains a **naval monopoly** through control of Crestpine distribution.

---

# Trade Networks

Crestpine moves through a controlled trade system:

- harvested in the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]]
- transported through [[Kheled-Sar]]
- shipped via [[Houjoum]] or westward caravan routes
- exported through [[Seluqar]]

“Crestpine spars” are a major export product, with foreign buyers paying premium prices for guaranteed straightness and quality.

---

# Cultural Significance

Crestpine carries deep cultural meaning in the Spine.

Logging traditions include:

- tying red prayer ribbons to selected trees years before felling
- rituals intended to “straighten the grain from within”
- reverence for trees that survive decades of harsh mountain growth

The act of harvesting Crestpine is seen as both labor and rite.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

- extremely slow growth rate
- dangerous harvesting conditions
- limited geographic range
- risk of overharvesting

Loss of Crestpine forests would have long-term consequences for both industry and naval power.
