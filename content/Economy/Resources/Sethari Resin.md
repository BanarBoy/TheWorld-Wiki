# Overview

Sethari Resin is a concentrated psychoactive substance derived from the sap of a hardy upland shrub cultivated along the dry terraces of the Chandral foothills. When processed and prepared correctly, it produces a slow-building state of euphoria, heightened perception, and inward focus.

Unlike crude intoxicants, Sethari does not overwhelm the senses. Its effects are gradual and controlled, often described as _expanding awareness rather than distorting it_. Users report heightened appreciation of sound, color, and thought, along with a sense of calm detachment from immediate physical concerns.

Because of its potency and psychological effects, Sethari Resin is not freely consumed. Its preparation and use are regulated, and it is typically taken in measured doses within structured environments. It exists not as a casual indulgence, but as a **deliberate experience**.

# Geographic Distribution

Sethari shrubs grow in:

• dry, mineral-rich terraces along the [[Chandral Range]]  
• upland zones with strong sunlight and limited rainfall  
• transition regions between river-fed lowlands and interior highlands

The plant requires:

• well-drained soil  
• seasonal dryness  
• controlled exposure to sunlight

These conditions make it unsuitable for the wetter river basins or coastal regions.

---

# Production

Sethari Resin production is careful and time-sensitive.

---

### Cultivation

• shrubs are grown in spaced terraces  
• overwatering reduces potency  
• plants mature slowly and require stable conditions

---

### Harvesting

• resin is collected by scoring the bark  
• sap is allowed to seep and partially cure  
• collected material is refined after extraction

---

### Refinement

• raw resin is filtered and concentrated  
• impurities are removed through controlled heating  
• final product is shaped into small, stable portions

Production is:

• low-yield  
• labor-intensive  
• dependent on environmental stability
