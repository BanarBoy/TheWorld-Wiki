# Overview

Anjara Red is a rare and highly prized dye derived from delicate flowering plants cultivated in the elevated terraces and sheltered valleys of the Chandral foothills. Known for its deep, luminous crimson tone, it stands in contrast to the permanence of **Indrava Blue**, offering instead a color associated with status, ritual, and controlled use.

The dye produces a rich, warm red that appears to shift subtly depending on light and fabric density. When applied to fine materials such as **Velari Weave**, Anjara Red creates a depth of color unmatched by more common pigments. However, unlike Indrava, it is not widely used in everyday textiles. Its scarcity and production difficulty limit it to high-status applications.

Anjara Red is not simply a dye—it is a controlled material. Its cultivation is restricted, its use often regulated, and its presence in garments or objects carries immediate cultural meaning. Where it appears, it signifies intention.

# Geographic Distribution

Anjara-producing plants grow only in:

• the sheltered terraces of the [[Chandral Range]] foothills  
• elevated valleys near [[Devora]]  
• controlled upland estates east of [[Varanji]]

Processing and refinement are concentrated further south and west:

• trade refinement centers in [[Jaspar]]  
• finishing and export preparation in [[Kalore]]

The plant requires:

• moderate elevation  
• stable, filtered sunlight  
• well-drained but mineral-rich soil

It is highly sensitive to:

• excessive heat  
• flooding  
• environmental instability

# Production

Anjara Red production is delicate and tightly controlled.

---

### Cultivation

• plants are grown in small, carefully managed terrace plots  
• often cultivated on elevated slopes  
• yield is low compared to other dye crops

---

### Harvesting

• flowers must be harvested at peak bloom  
• timing is critical for pigment quality  
• collection is done by hand

---

### Extraction

• petals are processed immediately after harvest in [[Devora]] and [[Varanji]]  
• pigment is extracted through controlled soaking and pressing  
• refinement increases color intensity

---

### Stabilization

• dye must be carefully fixed to remain stable  
• final preparation often completed in [[Jaspar]] or [[Kalore]]

Production is:

• limited  
• labor-intensive  
• dependent on specialized knowledge

Anjara is transported:

• from upland terraces to [[Varanji]]

Shipments are:

• small  
• sealed  
• tightly controlled

# Economic Importance

Anjara Red is a **luxury dye**, not a staple commodity.

It supports:

• elite textile production  
• ceremonial industries  
• high-value trade networks

Economic roles are split:

• inland regions → cultivation and extraction  
• [[Jaspar]] / [[Kalore]] → refinement, finishing, export

Its value lies in:

> scarcity, symbolism, and controlled distribution

# Cultural Significance

Anjara Red is a color of meaning.

It is associated with:

• ceremony  
• authority  
• transition and declaration

• formal garments  
• ritual textiles  
• high-ranking individuals

> to wear Anjara is to be seen—and to be judged
