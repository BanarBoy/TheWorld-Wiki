# Overview

Velmora Cap is a carefully cultivated fungal resource known for its unique effects on perception, focus, and cognitive clarity. Unlike common medicinal plants, Velmora does not primarily affect the body—it acts on the mind, enhancing awareness, stabilizing attention, and allowing for exceptional precision when used correctly.

The mushroom grows in pale, layered caps with a faint matte sheen, typically in low-light, high-humidity environments. While its natural growth is limited to specific ecological niches, most Velmora is not foraged but deliberately cultivated in controlled conditions, where its potency and consistency can be carefully managed.

Its value lies in its reliability. In measured doses, Velmora enables heightened concentration and steadiness, making it indispensable for disciplines requiring exact control—such as surgery, fine metalwork, and advanced craft production. However, its effects are highly dose-dependent, and improper use can lead to disorientation or sensory distortion. For this reason, its cultivation, preparation, and distribution are tightly regulated.

# Geographic Distribution

Velmora Cap is found in:

• naturally occurring cave systems along the [[Chandral Range]] foothills  
• shaded, humid groves near [[Devora]]  
• controlled subterranean cultivation chambers near [[Varanji]]

It requires:

• stable humidity  
• low light or indirect light conditions  
• mineral-rich substrate and organic matter

Natural growth occurs in:

• river-cut caverns  
• mist-heavy foothill environments  
• enclosed, shaded ecosystems

Because of its environmental sensitivity, large-scale wild growth is uncommon, and most production relies on managed cultivation.

# Production

Velmora production is deliberate and highly controlled.

### Cultivation

• grown in enclosed chambers or shaded terraces  
• substrates are prepared from organic matter and mineral-rich soil  
• humidity and airflow are carefully regulated

---

### Growth Cycle

• spores are introduced into prepared environments  
• growth is monitored continuously  
• timing of harvest affects potency and effect profile

---

### Harvesting

• caps are collected at peak maturity  
• handled carefully to avoid damage or contamination  
• sorted by size, density, and potency

Production is:

• slow  
• knowledge-dependent  
• limited by environmental control capacity
