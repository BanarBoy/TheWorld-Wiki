# Overview

Frostpine is the dominant conifer of Eryndor’s cold forests, a slow-growing, resin-rich tree adapted to high winds, low temperatures, and nutrient-poor soils. Recognized by its pale bark, dense grain, and sharp, clean-burning resin, it is one of the most versatile natural resources in the region.

Unlike softer or fast-growing woods, Frostpine develops tightly packed growth rings, resulting in timber that is both strong and resistant to warping under cold conditions. Its resin content gives it unique properties when burned, producing a steady, aromatic smoke used extensively in both preservation and brewing traditions.

Nearly every aspect of the tree is used—its wood for building, its resin for industrial and culinary purposes, and its byproducts for fuel and treatment.

> It grows slowly.  
> It is used completely.

# Geographic Distribution

Frostpine forests are found across:

• the slopes and lower elevations of the [[Crown of Winter]]  
• western highland forest belts  
• cold interior uplands and ridgelines  
• wind-protected valleys within mountainous regions

These environments are characterized by:

• long winters and short growing seasons  
• strong, persistent winds  
• poor, rocky soils

Frostpine thrives where:

• other trees fail to establish  
• conditions limit rapid growth  
• environmental stress favors resilience

Because of this, Frostpine forests are:

• widespread but slow to regenerate  
• critical to regional ecosystems

# Production

Frostpine harvesting is carefully managed due to slow growth rates.

---

### Logging Practices

• selective cutting of mature trees  
• long rotation cycles to allow regrowth  
• seasonal logging to avoid soil damage

Dwarven operations often focus on:

• precision cutting for structural timber  
• efficient use of high-density wood

Human logging groups tend toward:

• broader extraction  
• mixed-use harvesting (timber + resin)

---

### Material Characteristics

Frostpine wood is:

• dense and strong  
• resistant to cracking in cold climates  
• relatively lightweight compared to its strength

Its resin is:

• highly flammable  
• aromatic when burned  
• preservative in certain applications
