# Overview

Varanji Steel is a refined, patterned metal renowned for its strength, flexibility, and flowing internal grain. Produced through a highly controlled forging process unique to the region, it stands apart from the mass-produced metals of industrial centers, emphasizing precision, technique, and generational knowledge over scale.

The defining feature of Varanji Steel is its internal structure. When properly forged, the metal reveals subtle, wave-like patterns across its surface—evidence of repeated folding, layering, and treatment during production. These patterns are not merely aesthetic; they reflect the material’s balanced composition, allowing it to maintain both durability and resilience under stress.

Unlike the heavy, output-driven metallurgy of [[Khordun-Zhal]], Varanji Steel is produced in limited quantities and is rarely used for large-scale construction. Instead, it is reserved for applications where precision and performance matter most. Each piece is treated as a crafted object rather than a product, often carrying the mark of its maker and the lineage of its technique.

Varanji Steel production is centered in:

• the forge districts of [[Varanji]]  
• workshop enclaves in the foothills of the [[Chandral Range]]  
• interior craft districts near [[Mandor]]

The region provides:

• access to workable iron deposits in nearby highlands  
• stable workshop environments protected from extreme climate  
• proximity to trade and artisan networks

The surrounding terrain supports:

• controlled charcoal production  
• access to water for quenching and refinement  
• isolation necessary for maintaining guarded techniques

Production remains localized due to the difficulty of replicating its methods elsewhere.

# Production

Varanji Steel is produced through a slow, multi-stage forging process.

---

### Material Preparation

• base iron is refined and purified before forging  
• impurities are carefully reduced to ensure consistency  
• raw material quality directly affects final output

---

### Folding and Layering

• metal is heated, folded, and hammered repeatedly  
• layers are built over multiple cycles  
• internal structure is gradually refined

---

### Treatment

• controlled heating cycles alter the metal’s properties  
• quenching techniques vary by workshop  
• final tempering balances hardness and flexibility

---

### Finishing

• surfaces are polished to reveal internal patterns  
• edges are honed with precision  
• each piece is inspected for structural integrity

Production is:

• slow  
• skill-intensive  
• limited by artisan capacity
