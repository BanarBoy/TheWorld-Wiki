# Overview

Jadefire Silk is the most prized textile produced in Kiyora, a luminous silk woven from the threads of a rare, region-bound silkworm strain. Unlike conventional silk, its fibers possess a faint inner radiance—subtle under daylight, but unmistakable in shadow or lamplight, where the fabric seems to carry a soft, living glow.

Its value lies in both rarity and behavior. Jadefire Silk is exceptionally fine, strong, and responsive to heat and motion. When worn, it shifts in tone and depth, reacting to the body beneath it. In formal garments, this creates an effect of quiet movement, as though the cloth itself is alive. Higher-grade weaves can retain warmth or hold minor enchantments, making them both aesthetic and functional.

Production is inseparable from place. The silkworms that produce Jadefire Silk cannot survive outside Kiyora’s controlled valley climates, and attempts to replicate them elsewhere have consistently failed. As a result, the silk is not merely a luxury—it is a geographic monopoly, tying wealth, prestige, and cultural identity directly to the land that produces it.

More than a commodity, Jadefire Silk defines Kiyora’s external image. Where other regions export strength or abundance, Kiyora exports refinement—and Jadefire Silk is its clearest expression.

# Geographic Distribution

Jadefire Silk production is concentrated in:

• the humid river valleys surrounding [[Laoshan]] within the **Laohe basin**  
• terraced agricultural zones near [[Fenghai]] within the **Fenghe basin**  
• cultivated mulberry districts around [[Jinshan]] within the **Jinghe basin**  
• fertile river corridors near [[Qinghe]] and [[Minghu]] within the **Qinghe** and **Minghe** basins

These regions provide:

• stable humidity and temperature  
• reliable freshwater systems from interconnected river basins  
• dense, carefully maintained mulberry groves

The environmental conditions are:

• warm but moderated by river systems  
• influenced by seasonal mist and low wind exposure  
• protected from extreme climatic variation

Because of this, production remains:

• tightly localized  
• dependent on established agricultural infrastructure  
• difficult to replicate outside central Kiyora

# Production

Jadefire Silk production is hierarchical, controlled, and labor-intensive.

---

### Silkworm Cultivation

• silkworms are raised in enclosed, climate-controlled houses  
• fed selectively cultivated mulberry leaves  
• monitored continuously for health and output quality

The silkworm strain is:

• delicate  
• difficult to breed  
• highly sensitive to environmental changes

---

### Cocoon Harvesting

• cocoons are harvested at precise developmental stages  
• timing directly affects thread strength and luminosity  
• imperfect cocoons are downgraded or discarded

---

### Thread Extraction

• silk threads are unwound in long, continuous strands  
• breakage significantly reduces value  
• only trained handlers process high-grade silk

---

### Weaving

• silk is woven by master artisans in guild houses  
• patterns often encode lineage, rank, or regional identity  
• advanced weaves use layered threading for depth and sheen

Production is:

• slow  
• skill-intensive  
• intentionally limited to preserve quality

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Raw Jadefire Silk undergoes:

• cleaning and grading by thread quality  
• dyeing with mineral and botanical pigments  
• weaving into finished textiles or prepared bolts

Higher-grade silk may also be:

• treated to enhance luminosity  
• prepared for ceremonial or arcane use

---

### Transport

Due to its value, Jadefire Silk is transported:

• via river routes across the **Qinghe**, **Minghe**, and **Fenghe** systems  
• through secured caravan routes linking [[Qinghe]] and [[Minghu]] to [[Laoshan]]  
• in sealed and protected consignments

Most shipments are:

• pre-contracted  
• tightly tracked  
• rarely sold in open markets

# Economic Importance

Jadefire Silk is one of Kiyora’s most important luxury exports.

It:

• supports elite artisan industries  
• generates high-value, low-volume trade  
• reinforces Kiyora’s influence in foreign courts

Key economic roles:

• [[Laoshan]] — administrative control and high-value trade (Laohe basin)  
• [[Fenghai]] — artisan weaving and refinement (Fenghe basin)  
• [[Qinghe]] — agricultural backbone of silkworm cultivation (Qinghe basin)  
• [[Minghu]] — inland processing and redistribution hub (Minghe basin)

It exists primarily as:

> a symbol of controlled wealth and cultural authority

# Controlling Powers

Control of Jadefire Silk is centralized and strictly enforced.

Primary actors include:

• **Imperial Silk Bureau** — regulates production quotas and distribution  
• noble weaving houses — control high-grade designs and output  
• artisan guilds — enforce technical standards  
• provincial magistrates — oversee taxation and legal enforcement

Unauthorized production or trade is:

• illegal  
• punishable  
• actively suppressed

Jadefire Silk represents:

• refinement  
• legitimacy

# Dangers and Limitations

• extreme sensitivity of silkworm populations  
• vulnerability to disease or environmental disruption  
• limited scalability of production  
• risk of counterfeit goods entering foreign markets

Additionally:

• loss of skilled artisans reduces output quality  
• overproduction risks degrading prestige and control
