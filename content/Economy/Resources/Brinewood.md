# Overview

Brinewood is a dense, oil-rich tropical hardwood native to the coastal jungles of [[Sapphire Reach]]. Unlike most timber, it exhibits a natural resistance to salt saturation, rot, and marine parasites, making it exceptionally well-suited for prolonged exposure to open sea conditions.

Its value lies not in raw strength—woods such as those found in [[Valecaryn]] surpass it in rigidity—but in its durability under stress. Brinewood resists warping, absorbs impact from waves, and degrades far more slowly in humid, salt-heavy environments. When used correctly, it extends the operational lifespan of ships and reduces the need for frequent repair.

The resource has reshaped regional shipbuilding practices. Rather than replacing traditional timbers, brinewood is incorporated into critical sections of a vessel—particularly outer hull layers and high-wear components. This hybrid construction has become increasingly sought after by merchants and captains operating long-distance routes.

Within [[Sapphire Reach]], brinewood represents both economic opportunity and political leverage. Control over its harvest and distribution is fragmented, but closely watched.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Brinewood grows primarily along the coastal margins of [[Sapphire Reach]], where jungle ecosystems meet salt-laden air and tidal influence.

It is most commonly found:

• in low-elevation coastal forests  
• along tidal inlets and estuarine environments  
• in areas with consistent exposure to sea spray and high humidity

The wood’s defining properties are believed to result from this environment. Constant exposure to salt, moisture, and marine organisms forces the trees to develop dense, oil-rich fibers that resist decay.

Attempts to cultivate brinewood outside of these conditions have largely failed, limiting its production to specific regions.

---

# Production

Brinewood is harvested through logging operations along the coastal jungle.

• Trees are selectively cut to preserve long-term yield  
• Harvesting is labor-intensive due to density and terrain  
• Crews must contend with heat, humidity, and dangerous wildlife

Production is moderate rather than large-scale:

• overharvesting reduces future viability  
• accessible groves are limited  
• expansion inland reduces the wood’s desirable properties

Most harvesting is conducted by crews aligned with one of the dominant fleets, though independent operations exist in smaller quantities.

---

# Processing and Transport

Raw brinewood is not immediately suitable for ship use.

It must undergo a curing process:

• drying under controlled airflow  
• gradual stabilization to prevent warping  
• removal of excess surface moisture

Properly cured brinewood becomes significantly more stable and valuable.

Transport occurs primarily by sea:

• logs are moved to coastal yards near [[Corsair’s Rest]]  
• processed timber is shipped through both [[Corsair’s Rest]] and [[Bluewake]]  
• Bluewake handles most “legitimate” exports

Due to its weight and density, overland transport is limited and inefficient.

---

# Economic Importance

Brinewood is a strategic resource within maritime economies.

It is most valuable to:

• long-distance merchant fleets  
• naval forces operating in harsh conditions  
• pirate crews requiring durable, low-maintenance vessels

Cities and regions dependent on sea trade—particularly [[Valecaryn]]—benefit from its use, even if indirectly.

While not required for all shipbuilding, its integration provides a measurable advantage, especially over extended voyages.

---

# Controlling Powers

No single entity controls brinewood production outright.

Instead, influence is divided among the dominant powers of [[Sapphire Reach]]:

• [[Albrecht Lanmast]] — controls key harbor access and processing flow  
• [[Cirel Redmark]] — influences distribution and high-value transactions  
• [[Rathen Gustmore]] — exerts pressure over harvesting regions  
• [[Niamh Auldrahm]] — operates indirectly through selective intervention  
• [[Seressa Fairwind]] — maintains strategic interest in its application

This fragmented control prevents monopoly but ensures constant oversight.

---

# Trade Networks

Brinewood is exported primarily through:

• [[Bluewake]] — formal trade interface  
• [[Corsair’s Rest]] — bulk and unregulated movement

Key destinations include:

• [[Valecaryn]] shipyards  
• independent maritime operators  
• frontier ports requiring durable vessels

Trade is often layered:

• raw timber → processed locally  
• finished material → exported through controlled channels

---

# Cultural Significance

Within [[Sapphire Reach]], brinewood carries a reputation more than symbolic meaning.

It is associated with:

• endurance at sea  
• long voyages without failure  
• vessels that “hold together when others don’t”

Among experienced sailors, ships built with brinewood are often regarded with quiet respect.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

• Overharvesting reduces long-term supply  
• Improper curing renders the wood unstable  
• Dense jungle environments make extraction dangerous  
• Control disputes between factions can disrupt supply

Additionally:

• brinewood is heavier than many alternatives  
• misuse in construction can reduce efficiency rather than improve it
