---

# Overview

Blue Mussels are one of the most abundant and accessible food resources along the coasts of Eryndor. Found clustered in dense beds along rocky shorelines and tidal flats, they provide a reliable source of protein for coastal communities.

Recognized by their dark blue-black shells and firm, briny flesh, they thrive in cold, nutrient-rich waters where strong tides bring constant flow and filtration. Unlike large fish or deep-sea catches, mussels require little specialized equipment to harvest, making them a cornerstone of everyday coastal subsistence.

While not considered prestigious, they are widely valued for their consistency, ease of preparation, and ability to feed large numbers of people with minimal effort.

> They are not hunted.  
> They are gathered.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Blue Mussels are found along:

• rocky shorelines and tidal flats  
• sheltered bays and inlets  
• estuaries where freshwater meets the sea

Major concentrations exist around:

• [[Stormhaven]] — large natural beds along exposed coastlines  
• [[Frostgate]] — cold-water inlets and harbor zones  
• [[Threewaters]] — estuarine systems with ideal growth conditions

They thrive in:

• cold, oxygen-rich water  
• areas with strong tidal movement  
• surfaces such as rocks, pilings, and reef formations

---

# Production

Blue Mussels are primarily harvested rather than farmed, though some regions manage beds.

---

### Harvesting Methods

• hand-gathering during low tide  
• scraping from rocks and coastal structures  
• small-scale boat collection in shallow waters

Harvesting is:

• low-cost  
• labor-based  
• accessible to most coastal populations

---

### Managed Beds

In some regions:

• mussel beds are maintained or protected  
• harvesting is regulated to prevent depletion  
• simple cultivation methods are used to increase yield

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Blue Mussels are:

• cleaned and purged of sand  
• cooked fresh (boiled, steamed, or stewed)  
• sometimes dried or lightly preserved (less common)

They are most commonly used in:

• broths and stews  
• simple tavern dishes  
• coastal communal meals

---

### Transport

Due to perishability, mussels are:

• consumed locally whenever possible  
• transported short distances to nearby settlements

Long-distance trade is:

• rare  
• limited to preserved or specially packed batches

---

# Economic Importance

Blue Mussels are a staple resource for coastal Eryndor.

They:

• provide inexpensive, reliable food  
• support local fishing economies  
• reduce dependence on inland food supply

While individually low in value, their abundance makes them:

> economically significant at scale

Control is largely local and community-based.

• coastal fishing families  
• harbor guilds  
• village cooperatives

• access to productive beds is regulated  
• disputes arise over harvesting rights

# Trade Networks

Blue Mussels are distributed:

• within coastal regions  
• to nearby inland settlements

They are rarely exported due to:

• short shelf life  
• limited preservation methods

# Dangers and Limitations

• spoil quickly after harvest  
• vulnerable to water contamination  
• overharvesting in unmanaged areas  
• seasonal variation in quality

Additionally:

• unsafe harvesting areas can lead to illness  
• storms can disrupt mussel beds
