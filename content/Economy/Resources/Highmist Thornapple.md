---
# Overview

Highmist Thornapple is a hardy, spiked shrub that grows along the mist-laden slopes of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]], particularly in elevations surrounding [[Kelresh]]. Sustained by constant condensation rather than direct rainfall, the plant absorbs mineral-rich moisture from the mountain air, giving it its distinctive medicinal potency.

Both its leaves and pale, thorned berries contain compounds that induce deep sleep, ease chronic pain, and treat severe respiratory illness. Unlike many herbal remedies, Thornapple acts quickly and reliably, making it one of the most important medical resources for controlled sedation and recovery.

Its bitterness and potency require careful handling, and its effects can vary based on preparation. In Thalassar, it represents discipline in medicine—measured, precise, and often life-saving when used correctly.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Highmist Thornapple grows exclusively in:

- mist-heavy slopes of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]]
- high-altitude zones near [[Kelresh]]
- areas where fog and condensation persist year-round

It requires:

- consistent mist exposure
- cool, high-altitude climates
- mineral-rich stone and soil

It does not thrive in lowlands or humid jungle environments, making it geographically limited and difficult to replicate elsewhere.

---

# Production

Unlike jungle-harvested medicines, Thornapple is **partially cultivated**.

Production involves:

- identifying natural growth zones in mist corridors
- transplanting and maintaining shrubs on controlled terraces
- harvesting leaves and berries at precise maturity stages

Primary production occurs in:

- cultivated slopes surrounding [[Kelresh]]
- smaller highland settlements maintaining healer gardens

Harvesting is:

- carefully timed to preserve potency
- performed by trained gatherers or healers
- limited in volume due to environmental constraints

---

# Processing and Transport

Thornapple requires controlled preparation to remain effective.

Processing methods include:

- drying leaves for steeped medicinal tea
- crushing berries into paste or tincture
- refining extracts for surgical use

Specialized uses:

- brewed as a bitter tea for respiratory illness
- burned as a fumigant to cleanse enclosed spaces
- distilled into concentrated sedatives

Transport methods:

- carried via caravan routes from [[Kelresh]]
- distributed through highland trade networks
- exported through Seluqar for international markets

Due to potency, it is often measured and packaged in small, regulated quantities.

---

# Economic Importance

Highmist Thornapple is a **specialized medical resource**.

It is essential for:

- treating lung infections and cold-weather illness
- controlled sedation during surgery
- maintaining medical infrastructure in highland and maritime settings

Key dependencies:

- Kelresh medical practitioners rely on it for advanced care
- Seluqar naval surgeons use it for controlled sedation
- apothecaries across Thalassar use it as a premium medicinal ingredient

It is not widely consumed, but critically important in specific contexts.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Thornapple is structured and disciplined:

- highland healer circles regulate knowledge and usage
- cultivation guilds manage production zones
- Kelresh authorities oversee trade and export

Seluqar buyers exert influence through demand, but do not control production directly.

---

# Trade Networks

Highmist Thornapple moves through:

- caravan routes descending from [[Kelresh]]
- trade networks linking to the [[Glasswater Reach]]
- export systems centered in [[Seluqar]]

In foreign markets, it is known as:

- **“Kelresh sleeper”**

It competes with Valecaryn poppy derivatives, but is favored for respiratory treatment and faster onset.

---

# Cultural Significance

Highmist Thornapple is associated with **discipline and restraint**.

Among highland healers:

- dosage is considered a sacred responsibility
- improper use is seen as both dangerous and dishonorable

It is commonly said:

> “The mist gives sleep—but not mercy.”

Healers are trained to administer it with precision, as too much can lead to prolonged unconsciousness or death.

---

# Dangers and Limitations

- narrow geographic range
- highly dosage-sensitive
- dangerous if improperly prepared
- limited production volume

Its potency is both its greatest strength and its greatest risk.
