# Virelois Red

![[Pasted image 20260324191826.png]]

**Resource Type:** Agricultural Product / Commercial Wine  
**Primary Region(s):** [[Allacroix Vineyards]], [[Virelois Delta]]  
**Major Production Centers:** [[Merrowfen]] and surrounding vineyard estates  
**Controlling Groups:** [[House Allacroix]], vineyard estates, vintners’ guilds, wine merchant houses  
**Primary Uses:** Tavern consumption, merchant trade, ship provisions, export markets  
**Trade Value:** Common / Profitable Export

---

# Overview

Virelois Red is the most widely produced red wine of the [[Allacroix Vineyards]] and serves as the commercial foundation of the region’s wine trade. Named for the fertile lands surrounding the [[Virelois River]], it is produced in large quantities across vineyard estates throughout the delta.

While prestigious wines such as [[Merrowfen Grand Red Wine]] and the rare [[Suncrest White]] dominate noble cellars, Virelois Red is the wine most commonly encountered in markets, taverns, and merchant ships across Valecaryn.

Known for its deep color, bold flavor, and reliable quality, Virelois Red has become a staple of everyday wine consumption throughout the continent.

---

# Geographic Origin

Virelois Red originates from vineyards spread across the fertile lands of the [[Virelois Delta]] and the surrounding hills of the [[Allacroix Vineyards]].

The region’s winemaking success is shaped by several natural advantages:

• rich river-fed soils deposited by the Virelois River  
• gentle vineyard slopes surrounding [[Merrowfen]]  
• warm growing seasons that allow grapes to ripen fully  
• abundant water supporting large vineyard estates

These conditions allow for consistent harvests capable of supporting large-scale wine production.

---

# Production

Virelois Red is produced across many vineyard estates throughout the Merrowfen region.

During harvest season, grapes are collected from a wide range of vineyard plots. Because the wine is intended for commercial markets, production focuses on consistency and volume rather than the strict selection used for prestige vintages.

After harvesting, grapes are fermented in large vats before being transferred to barrels or storage casks. The wine is aged for a relatively short period before being distributed to markets across the continent.

---

# Processing and Storage

Following fermentation, Virelois Red is stored in large wine cellars and barrel houses throughout [[Merrowfen]].

Because the wine is intended for widespread consumption, it is usually bottled or shipped in casks within a year of harvest.

Many taverns and merchant houses purchase Virelois Red in bulk barrels for resale.

---

# Economic Importance

Virelois Red represents one of the most important commercial exports of the Merrowfen region.

The wine trade supports:

• vineyard workers across the Merrowfen countryside  
• vintners and cellar masters  
• barrel makers and cooperages  
• merchant houses transporting wine throughout Valecaryn

Because of the scale of production, Virelois Red provides the economic foundation that allows Merrowfen estates to also produce their prestigious wines.

---

# Trade Networks

Virelois Red travels widely throughout Valecaryn and is one of the most commonly traded wines in the region.

Major trade routes connect Merrowfen to cities such as:

• [[Aureth Caldras]]  
• [[Lethryn Prime]]  
• [[Velossa]]  
• [[Westport]]

From these cities, merchant ships often carry barrels of Virelois Red to distant markets overseas.

---

# Cultural Significance

Virelois Red is closely associated with everyday social life throughout Valecaryn.

Unlike rare noble wines, it is commonly served in taverns, merchant households, and aboard trading vessels.

Many travelers and sailors consider a cup of Virelois Red a familiar taste of home, making it one of the most recognizable wines produced in the continent.

# Rumors and Conflicts

Some merchants claim that certain estates secretly blend grapes intended for Virelois Red into higher-end vintages in order to increase production.

Others believe that rival wine merchants sometimes attempt to counterfeit Allacroix wines in distant markets.

There are also whispers that expanding vineyards may soon begin competing directly with the prestigious estates producing Merrowfen Grand Red.
