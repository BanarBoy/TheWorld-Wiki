# Overview

Lotus Root is a cultivated aquatic crop grown in the shallow waters of Kiyora’s ponds, wetlands, and slow-moving river systems. Harvested from beneath the surface, its segmented structure and crisp, pale flesh make it both visually distinctive and highly versatile in cooking.

Unlike Lianmi, which serves as the foundation of daily sustenance, Lotus Root occupies a more refined place in Kiyoran life. It is used in a wide range of dishes—from simple broths to elaborate preparations—and is valued for both its texture and its ability to absorb surrounding flavors.

Its importance extends beyond cuisine. Lotus Root is also used in medicinal preparations, believed to support balance within the body and improve circulation and vitality. Because it grows in still, carefully maintained water systems, it has also taken on symbolic meaning tied to clarity, patience, and cultivation.

While widely available within Kiyora, high-quality Lotus Root—uniform, unblemished, and precisely harvested—is considered a mark of skilled cultivation and commands higher value in urban markets.

# Geographic Distribution

Lotus Root is cultivated in:

• shallow pond systems near [[Qinghe]] within the **Qinghe basin**  
• wetland basins and managed water fields around [[Minghu]] within the **Minghe basin**  
• slow-moving river edges and irrigation spill zones near [[Fenghai]] within the **Fenghe basin**

It thrives in:

• still or slow-moving freshwater  
• soft, silty sediment  
• warm, stable growing conditions

These environments are ideal because:

• controlled water depth allows consistent growth  
• sediment-rich beds support root expansion  
• irrigation overflow zones provide reliable hydration

Unlike Lianmi, Lotus Root is not grown in open flooded fields, but in:

• enclosed pond systems  
• managed wetlands  
• carefully maintained aquatic plots

# Production

Lotus Root cultivation is precise and physically demanding.

---

### Cultivation

• planted in submerged sediment beds  
• requires controlled water depth and clarity  
• often grown alongside managed aquatic vegetation

---

### Growth Management

• water quality must be maintained  
• sediment must remain loose but stable  
• overcrowding reduces root quality

---

### Harvesting

• roots are extracted manually from mud and sediment  
• requires careful digging to avoid damage  
• washed immediately after removal

Harvesting is:

• labor-intensive  
• skill-dependent  
• sensitive to timing and technique

---

### Processing

Lotus Root is prepared in several ways:

• cleaned and sliced for fresh consumption  
• dried or preserved for storage  
• processed into medicinal extracts or powders

Higher-grade products include:

• perfectly segmented root sections for culinary presentation  
• preserved Lotus Root prepared with spices or syrups

---

### Transport

Due to its perishability, Lotus Root is:

• transported quickly from farms to markets  
• moved via river routes across the **Qinghe**, **Minghe**, and **Fenghe** systems  
• packed carefully to prevent bruising

Preserved forms are:

• easier to transport  
• traded more widely across Kiyora
