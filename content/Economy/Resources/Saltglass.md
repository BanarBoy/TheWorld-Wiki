# Overview

Saltglass is the finest glass produced in the known world, crafted exclusively in [[Seluqar]] using ultra-pure crystalline material known as **Crownshard of Ashur**. Unlike common glass, Saltglass is defined not only by its clarity, but by its **controlled imperfection**—a faint internal shimmer that refracts light in subtle, living colors.

The material achieves a level of thinness and strength unmatched by conventional glassmaking. It can be drawn into delicate forms without losing structural integrity, making it ideal for both luxury objects and precision instruments.

Saltglass is not merely a material—it is a demonstration of Seluqar mastery over refinement, process, and trade control.

> It is never perfectly clear.  
> It is always alive with light.

# Geographic Distribution

Saltglass is not naturally occurring—it is entirely produced.

Its existence depends on two locations:

---

### 🧊 [[Ashur-Taal]] (Raw Material Source)

• origin of **Crownshard crystals**  
• ultra-pure silica structure  
• cannot be replicated with common sand or mineral sources

---

### 🔥 [[Seluqar]] (Processing Hub)

• site of all true Saltglass production  
• controlled kiln environments  
• concentration of expert artisans and guild knowledge

---

No other region has successfully reproduced both:

• the raw material purity  
• the processing precision

---

# Production

Saltglass production is a tightly controlled, multi-stage process.

---

## Raw Material

• Crownshard crystals extracted from Ashur-Taal caverns  
• selected for purity and structural consistency  
• transported under contract to Seluqar

---

## Refinement Process

Seluqar artisans:

• grind Crownshard into an ultra-fine silica base  
• refine it in controlled wood-fired kilns  
• introduce **Masari Redwood ash** as a flux

This process results in:

• extreme clarity  
• internal mineral shimmer  
• high strength at low thickness

---

## Signature Trait

When held to light, Saltglass reveals:

• faint rainbow refractions  
• subtle internal movement of color  
• a clarity that is never perfectly uniform

This is considered proof of authenticity.

Saltglass is one of Seluqar’s most valuable exports.

It:

• commands extremely high prices in foreign markets  
• reinforces Seluqar’s reputation for refinement  
• connects multiple regional systems (Ashur-Taal, Masari, trade networks)

Its value lies in:

> craftsmanship + control of process + control of supply
