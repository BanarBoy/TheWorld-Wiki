# Overview

Velari Weave is the dominant textile of the river civilizations, produced from fine, soft fibers cultivated in the warm, irrigated lowlands of the region. Known for its exceptional lightness, breathability, and ability to hold complex dyes, it is the most widely used fabric across both everyday life and high-end craftsmanship.

Unlike heavier textiles from colder regions, Velari is designed for heat and movement. The fabric drapes easily, allowing airflow while maintaining structural integrity, making it ideal for both labor and formal wear in warm climates. Its true distinction, however, lies in its responsiveness to dye and layering techniques. Velari cloth serves as the primary medium for the region’s advanced textile artistry, capable of displaying intricate patterns, gradients, and symbolic designs.

Because of its versatility, Velari Weave exists at every level of society—from simple undyed cloth worn by laborers to highly refined, multi-layered garments commissioned by nobility. It is not merely a material, but a platform for expression, trade, and identity.

# Geographic Distribution

Velari fiber crops are cultivated across:

• the irrigated fields of the [[Varanji Basin]]  
• the fertile lowlands of [[Kavartha]]  
• the humid growing regions near [[Devora]]  
• river-fed agricultural zones along the [[Ravasha River]]

The crop thrives in:

• warm, humid climates  
• well-irrigated soil systems  
• regions with long growing seasons

Its cultivation depends on:

• stable water supply  
• controlled harvesting cycles  
• dense agricultural labor networks

These conditions make the river basins of the region uniquely suited to large-scale Velari production.

# Production

Velari production is both agricultural and artisanal.

---

### Fiber Cultivation

• plants are grown in dense, irrigated fields  
• fibers are harvested once mature and processed quickly  
• quality depends on soil condition and water control

---

### Fiber Processing

• raw fibers are cleaned and separated  
• spun into fine thread using specialized techniques  
• threads are sorted by thickness and strength

---

### Weaving

• threads are woven on tension-controlled looms  
• patterns can be embedded during weaving or added later through dyeing  
• advanced weaves incorporate layered thread densities for texture and movement

Production is:

• large-scale but skill-dependent  
• highly organized within guild systems  
• capable of both bulk and luxury output

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Finishing

Velari cloth is:

• washed and softened after weaving  
• prepared for dyeing or left in natural form  
• cut into bolts for trade

---

### Dye Integration

Most Velari is processed further through:

• dye houses using pigments such as **Indrava Blue**  
• layered coloring techniques  
• pattern-setting processes

---

### Transport

Velari textiles are transported via:

• river barges from [[Varanji]] and [[Kavartha]]  
• caravan routes to [[Mandor]] and [[Talgarh]]  
• maritime export from southern ports

They are traded as:

• raw cloth  
• dyed fabric  
• finished garments

# Economic Importance

Velari Weave is one of the most important economic resources in the region.

It supports:

• large agricultural labor systems  
• extensive artisan guild networks  
• high-volume trade markets

Cities such as [[Varanji]] and [[Devora]] rely on Velari for:

• domestic clothing supply  
• export revenue  
• artisan industry stability

Because of its global demand, Velari functions as both:

> a staple material and a strategic trade commodity

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Velari production is distributed but structured.

Primary actors include:

• weaver guilds controlling production standards  
• dye houses specializing in advanced coloration  
• merchant syndicates managing bulk trade  
• noble patrons commissioning high-end work

Power is concentrated among those who control:

• high-quality fiber production  
• advanced weaving techniques  
• access to dye resources

---

# Trade Networks

Velari Weave is exported widely across the known world.

Primary trade routes include:

• river systems connecting [[Varanji]] to inland markets  
• overland routes to [[Mandor]] and beyond  
• maritime export through [[Talgarh]]

It is traded to:

• [[Valecaryn]] as fine cloth  
• [[Thalassar]] as breathable fabric for warm climates  
• foreign courts as luxury garments

• daily clothing
