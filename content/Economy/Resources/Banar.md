# Overview

Banar is a thick-skinned, starchy fruit that serves as the primary staple of Thalassar, occupying a role similar to grain while retaining the flexibility of a fruit. Firm enough to be boiled, roasted, or fried and sweet enough to be eaten ripe, Banar forms the dietary foundation of both rural and urban populations.

Unlike more centralized crops, Banar is a **distributed agricultural resource**, grown across the full span of Thalassar’s river systems. From the structured floodplains of the [[Zhar Basin]] to the humid jungles of the [[Verdant Expanse]] and the sheltered valleys of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]], Banar adapts to a wide range of environments, making it uniquely resilient and essential.

Its importance extends beyond subsistence. Banar is the base of countless dishes, from simple boiled meals to elaborate desserts paired with Reedgold and Kelresh coffee. Its presence enables population density, labor stability, and long-distance trade, making it one of the most quietly powerful resources in the Thalassari world.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Banar grows throughout:

- the floodplains of the [[Zhar Basin]] along the **Zhar River**
- major cultivation zones such as [[Tal'mir Fields]]
- wetland and river-edge regions like [[Sarith Fen]]
- agricultural corridors surrounding [[Tarzhir]] and [[Zharakel]]
- jungle-adjacent zones in the [[Verdant Expanse]] near [[Irathel]]
- sheltered valleys and lower slopes of the [[Khaldren Spine Highlands]]

It thrives in:

- warm, humid climates
- nutrient-rich soils
- areas with consistent water access

The Zhar Basin produces the most reliable and scalable yields, while Verdant and highland regions produce more localized or variable harvests.

---

# Production

Banar cultivation exists on a **tiered scale across regions**:

- **[[Tal'mir Fields]]** → largest and most organized production zone
- **[[Sarith Fen]]** → wetland-grown Banar with higher moisture content
- **[[Tarzhir]]** → coordination and redistribution hub
- **[[Zharakel]]** → administrative and logistical control
- **[[Irathel]]** → frontier cultivation and jungle expansion

Production in the Zhar Basin is highly structured:

- planting cycles align with flood patterns
- irrigation is controlled by temple authorities
- labor is organized at scale

Outside the Basin:

- Verdant Expanse production is semi-wild and decentralized
- Khaldren production is limited to valley terraces and local consumption

Harvesting requires careful handling due to the weight and density of fruit clusters.

---

# Processing and Transport

Banar is heavy and perishable, requiring processing before long-distance transport.

Common processing methods:

- drying into slices or chips
- pressing into dense travel cakes
- partial cooking and sealing for preservation

Processing centers include:

- [[Zharakel]] — large-scale processing
- [[Tarzhir]] — redistribution and mid-tier processing
- [[Irathel]] — frontier processing for jungle-grown Banar

Transport methods:

- river barges along the Zhar system to [[Port Iskene]]
- caravan routes across the [[Ochre Promontory]]
- maritime export through [[Seluqar]]

Preserved Banar is a key ration for caravans and naval voyages.

---

# Economic Importance

Banar is the **primary caloric foundation** of Thalassar.

It:

- supports large population centers
- feeds labor forces in mining, agriculture, and trade
- stabilizes food supply across multiple regions

Key dependencies:

- the [[Zhar Basin]] relies on it for mass agriculture
- urban centers like [[Seluqar]] depend on imported Banar
- caravan and naval systems depend on preserved Banar

It is not just important—it is **necessary**.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Banar is tied to water and land access:

- the **[[Azhariel Clan]]** controls large-scale production in the Zhar Basin
- temple authorities regulate irrigation and planting cycles
- local groups and frontier communities manage Verdant production
- trade networks influence distribution rather than production

Unlike [[Reedgold]], Banar is harder to monopolize completely due to its widespread growth.

---

# Trade Networks

Banar moves through layered trade systems:

- river transport from Zhar Basin to [[Port Iskene]]
- redistribution through [[Que’lehr]] into Seluqar markets
- inland trade through caravan routes

It also flows:

- north toward [[Old Nerethis]] via river and lake systems
- southeast through river networks toward [[Houjoum]]

In foreign markets:

- dried Banar is a luxury novelty
- associated with Thalassari cuisine and culture

---

# Cultural Significance

Banar is the foundation of Thalassari cuisine.

It is used in:

- daily meals (boiled or stewed)
- street food (fried Banar)
- signature dishes such as **charred Banar**
- desserts combined with Reedgold

It exists across all social classes:

- staple for the poor
- refined ingredient for the wealthy

---

# Dangers and Limitations

- highly perishable when fresh
- vulnerable to irrigation disruption
- heavy and difficult to transport raw
- labor-intensive harvest

Large-scale disruption of Banar production would cause immediate food instability.
