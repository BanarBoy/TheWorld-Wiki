# Overview

Shevar Nut is one of the foundational agricultural resources of Thalassar, harvested from hardy, drought-resistant trees that grow along the unstable boundary between river-fed basins and arid land. The nuts themselves are dense and oil-rich, encased in thick shells that preserve their contents even under extreme heat and prolonged storage.

When processed, Shevar yields a pale-gold to amber oil that is stable under high temperatures and widely used for cooking, preservation, and food preparation. In addition to oil, the crushed nut produces a dense, nutrient-rich paste that serves as a staple food in caravan travel and drought-prone regions.

Unlike luxury crops or specialized goods, Shevar is defined by its reliability. It grows where other crops struggle, stores well over long distances, and provides both caloric and economic stability. Across Thalassar, it is not considered a luxury—but a requirement for sustained habitation.

---

# Geographic Distribution

Shevar Nut trees grow primarily along transitional ecological zones where water availability fluctuates between abundance and scarcity.

These include:

• the floodplains and terraces of the [[Zhar River Basin]]  
• the cultivated lowlands of the [[Tal’Mir Fields]] and [[Talresh Point]]  
• the eastern edge of the [[Glasswaste Expanse]]  
• the lower valleys of the [[Pom River]] and [[Velashar River]]

Shevar thrives in:

• semi-arid climates with strong seasonal variation  
• deep-rooted soils capable of retaining intermittent moisture  
• regions shaped by periodic flooding followed by long dry periods

It does not grow in the deep desert or dense eastern forests, but instead occupies the critical boundary between them—making it a defining feature of Thalassar’s interior geography.

# Production

Shevar Nut production combines both wild harvesting and managed cultivation.

---

### Cultivation

• trees are grown in dispersed groves rather than dense plantations  
• irrigation is minimal; trees rely on deep root systems and seasonal water access  
• groves are often maintained near old flood channels and river terraces

In regions like [[Tal’Mir Fields]]:

• semi-managed orchards increase yield  
• irrigation channels support younger trees

---

### Harvesting

• nuts are collected once fully hardened and fallen  
• shells are cracked manually or with simple tools  
• kernels are dried prior to processing

Production scale is:

• moderate but widespread  
• highly resilient to environmental fluctuation  
• less vulnerable than grain-based agriculture

---

# Processing and Transport

---

### Oil Extraction

Shevar Nuts are processed through:

• **cold pressing** → lighter, higher-quality oil used in cooking  
• **heat pressing** → higher yield, darker oil used for bulk consumption and fuel

The oil is:

• stable under high heat  
• resistant to spoilage  
• suitable for repeated use

---

### Byproducts

• crushed paste → staple food and travel ration  
• press-cake → animal feed or dense food blocks  
• shells → used as fuel or burned for low-grade charcoal

---

### Transport

Shevar is transported as:

• raw nuts (long-distance caravan trade)  
• pressed oil in sealed ceramic or metal containers  
• compressed paste for caravan use

Primary transport routes include:

• river systems along the [[Zhar River]] and southern waterways  
• caravan routes linking the Glasswaste fringe to interior cities  
• trade roads connecting to [[Seluqar]] and eastern markets

---

# Economic Importance

Shevar Nut is a cornerstone of Thalassar’s food economy.

It supports:

• daily cooking across urban and rural populations  
• preservation systems for meat and grain  
• caravan provisioning and long-distance travel  
• secondary industries such as lamp oil and trade goods

Cities such as [[Zharakel]], [[Talresh Point]], and [[Old Nerethis]] depend heavily on Shevar production and processing.

Without Shevar:

> large portions of Thalassar’s population would lack a stable and scalable source of cooking fat

Its importance rivals that of staple grains in other regions, making it one of the most strategically significant agricultural resources in Thalassar.

---

# Controlling Powers

Control of Shevar production is tied to land, water, and transport.

Primary actors include:

• irrigation authorities managing river access  
• agricultural collectives overseeing grove production  
• regional landholders controlling key basin territories  
• caravan syndicates distributing Shevar products across Thalassar

Power is determined by:

• access to productive river-adjacent land  
• control of processing infrastructure  
• ability to secure and move bulk shipments

---

# Trade Networks

Shevar is distributed throughout Thalassar and beyond.

Primary trade flows:

• from the [[Zhar River Basin]] outward to interior settlements  
• south toward [[Seluqar]] and coastal markets  
• eastward into more fertile regions lacking oil-producing crops

Exports include:

• bulk cooking oil  
• refined oil for urban markets  
• compressed paste for travel and military supply

In foreign regions, Shevar oil is valued for its:

• stability  
• transport efficiency  
• versatility

# Cultural Significance

Shevar Nut represents:

• resilience in harsh environments  
• the balance between water and arid land  
• the foundation of daily sustenance

It is associated with:

• communal food preparation  
• caravan survival  
• seasonal harvest cycles tied to river behavior

In many regions, access to Shevar is seen as:

> a baseline measure of stability and survival

# Dangers and Limitations

• dependence on seasonal water cycles  
• vulnerability to disruption of river systems  
• slow tree maturation limiting rapid expansion  
• overharvesting of wild groves

Additionally:

• poor processing reduces oil quality  
• transport losses can impact regional supply
