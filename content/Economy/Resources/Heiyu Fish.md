# Overview

Heiyu Fish are an invasive freshwater species that have spread extensively throughout the interconnected river systems and floodplain waters of central Kiyora. Originally introduced through unknown means, they have rapidly adapted to local conditions, outcompeting native species and establishing dense populations across multiple basins.

Despite their ecological disruption, Heiyu have become an important food source. They are easy to catch, reproduce quickly, and thrive in the same managed water systems used for agriculture, particularly in regions surrounding [[Qinghe]] and [[Jinshan]].

Heiyu are not considered refined cuisine, but their abundance has made them a staple protein source for lower and middle populations. Over time, Kiyoran cooks have developed a variety of preparation methods—drying, fermenting, and heavily seasoning—to make use of the fish’s otherwise plain or muddy flavor.

What began as an ecological problem has become a functional part of Kiyora’s food system, though not without ongoing tension between sustainability and control.

# Geographic Distribution

Heiyu Fish are found in:

• the **Qinghe River system** near [[Qinghe]]  
• irrigation channels and agricultural waterways across the **Qinghe basin**  
• floodplain basins around [[Heiwah]] connected to the **Fenghe** and **Laohe systems**  
• connected waterways near [[Hang'an]] (Laohe basin) and [[Jinshan]] (Jinghe basin)  
• secondary spread into linked channels across multiple river basins

They thrive in:

• slow-moving or stagnant water  
• nutrient-rich agricultural runoff  
• densely interconnected water systems

Their spread has been accelerated by:

• irrigation networks used for [[Lianmi]] cultivation  
• canal links between major river basins  
• lack of natural predators  
• high reproductive rates

# Production

Heiyu are not farmed in the traditional sense, but are harvested from the wild.

---

### Harvesting

• caught using nets, traps, and river barriers  
• often collected in large quantities due to density  
• seasonal fluctuations affect yield

---

### Management

• some regions attempt population control through aggressive harvesting  
• others allow populations to grow due to food demand

---

### Processing Preparation

• fish are cleaned and sorted by size  
• lower-quality catches are used for fermentation or feed  
• fresher catches are sold directly in markets

Production is:

• abundant  
• inconsistent in quality  
• tied to environmental conditions

# Processing and Transport

---

### Processing

Heiyu Fish are commonly:

• cooked fresh in local markets  
• dried for preservation  
• fermented into strong, long-lasting food products

Processed forms:

• travel further than fresh fish  
• are more valuable in trade

---

### Transport

Heiyu are transported via:

• river routes across the **Qinghe**, **Jinghe**, **Laohe**, and **Fenghe** systems  
• local trade networks into [[Heiwah]] and [[Hang'an]]  
• overland routes in preserved form

Fresh fish:

• remain local due to perishability

# Economic Importance

Heiyu Fish are a **low-cost, high-volume food resource**.

They:

• provide protein for large populations  
• support local fishing economies  
• reduce pressure on more controlled food systems

Key regions include:

• [[Qinghe]] — primary harvesting (Qinghe basin)  
• [[Heiwah]] — floodplain supply  
• [[Jinshan]] — redistribution (Jinghe basin)  
• [[Hang'an]] — local consumption and trade (Laohe basin)

They are:

> economically useful, but not prestigious
